# Methodology — Bananas Foster Paper Drive

## Research Approach

This paper drive uses a structured web-search methodology. Each of the 7 sections was researched through targeted search queries designed to surface primary, scholarly, and journalistic sources.

## Search Strategy

**Iteration 1 — Broad sweep (10 searches):**
- Bananas Foster history, Brennan's Restaurant, Owen Brennan, Richard Foster
- New Orleans banana port, United Fruit Company, banana republic politics
- Flambé and tableside cooking history, restaurant theater, hospitality performance
- Creole cuisine, New Orleans food culture, multicultural influences
- Gros Michel vs. Cavendish, Panama disease, banana botany and monoculture
- Banana Foster spread and recipe mutations globally
- Banana colonial labor exploitation, commodity food supply chain
- Brennan's fine dining history, French Quarter, 1940s-1950s
- Flambé history: Crêpes Suzette, Cherries Jubilee, Victorian era
- Guéridon service history, tableside cooking, Escoffier, American adoption

## Source Standards

- Citations are real URLs found during web searches
- No fabricated sources
- Wikipedia accepted as secondary overview (not as primary citation)
- Preference for: academic publications, established journalism (NOLA.com, Smithsonian, JSTOR), institutional histories (HNOC, Brennan's), and scholarly research portals (PubMed, PMC)

## NPOV Standards

- No authorial first or second person
- No editorial superlatives
- Tensions presented as competing evidence, not editorial conclusions
- Passive or third-person construction throughout

## Section Threshold

Each section was assessed against a minimum of 5 sources before writing began. Sections with fewer than 5 high-quality sources would have been marked "planned" rather than built.
