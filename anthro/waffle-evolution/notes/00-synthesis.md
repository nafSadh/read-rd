# Synthesis Notes — waffle-evolution

## Core argument

The waffle is not a single food but a family of related technologies, each embedded in a distinct social context. The waffle iron precedes the waffle in every meaningful sense: the iron was the instrument of power (communion production, guild control, aristocratic patronage), and the food followed its technology.

## Key structural insight

The evolution maps onto a recurring pattern:
1. **Religious/sacred origin** — communion wafer, church vendor
2. **Secularization** — street vendor, guild formation, recipe proliferation
3. **National/regional identity formation** — Belgian gaufre, Dutch stroopwafel, Norwegian hjertevafler
4. **American absorption and transformation** — Jefferson import, waffle party, waffle house
5. **Industrialization and mass production** — Eggo, frozen waffle market
6. **Global recombination** — Hong Kong egg waffle, Korean variants, global bubble waffle trend

## Tensions that run through every section

- **Authenticity vs. standardization**: The "real" Belgian waffle bears almost no resemblance to what Americans call Belgian waffles. The Eggo outsells all artisan waffles. What counts as authentic?
- **Origin myth vs. history**: Nearly every waffle origin story is contested — Thomas Jefferson and waffles, Gerard Kamphuisen and stroopwafels, the Liège prince's cook. The myth matters as much as the fact.
- **Sacred vs. profane**: The communion wafer and the Eggo are separated by 1,000 years and the same basic grid pattern. The transition is total yet structurally invisible.
- **Local identity vs. globalization**: Belgian waffles became a global export product defined by American taste preferences. The 1964 World's Fair version is not what Belgians eat.

## Section assessments

### S1: Medieval origins
- Strong source base (Smithsonian, Wikipedia/Waffle, Aleteia, Encyclopedia.com, The Kitchn)
- Key facts: oublies guild 1270, Le Ménagier de Paris recipe ~1390, gaufre vs. oublie distinction emerges 15th c.
- Religious–secular transition is well documented

### S2: Belgian identity
- Strong source base (Belgian Waffle Wikipedia, Liège Waffle Wikipedia, Taste of Belgium, Brussels Times)
- 1958 Expo, 1962 Seattle, 1964 NYC sequence well documented
- Vermersch family detail (2,500 waffles/day) is compelling specificity

### S3: American adoption
- Jefferson waffle iron story: Garden & Gun piece notes evidence is limited
- Waffle frolics well documented
- Dutch settler Pennsylvania waffle tradition separate from Belgian import

### S4: Waffle House
- Excellent source base (FEMA/Snopes, Wikipedia, wafflehouse.com)
- Founded 1955, Avondale Estates, GA
- Craig Fugate coined Waffle House Index 2004 after Hurricane Charley
- 1,900+ locations, 25 states

### S5: Chicken & waffles
- Two distinct lineages: Pennsylvania Dutch (shredded chicken, gravy) and Harlem soul food (fried chicken, jazz clubs)
- Roscoe's (1975, Herb Hudson) as national dissemination vector
- The convergence story is genuinely complex

### S6: Industrialization / Eggo
- Frank Dorsa invented 1953; "Froffles" → "Eggo" 1955; Kellogg acquisition 1968
- 73% frozen waffle market share by 2009
- Electric waffle iron: Simplex 1906, GE prototype 1911, mass production 1918+

### S7: Global variations
- Hong Kong egg waffle: 1950s origin, grocery shop owner, evaporated milk
- Stroopwafel: Gouda c.1810–1840, 100+ bakers by mid-19th c., "armenkoeken"
- Norwegian hjertevafler: heart shape, cardamom, lingonberry/brunost
- Waffle Day (March 25) coincides with Feast of Annunciation in Scandinavia
