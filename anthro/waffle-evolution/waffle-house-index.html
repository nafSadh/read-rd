The Metric

Three Colors, One Question

The Waffle House Index reduces complex infrastructure assessment to a single observable variable: is the Waffle House open, and what menu is it serving?

Coined by Craig Fugate in August 2004 after Hurricane Charley while he was directing Florida’s Division of Emergency Management. Fugate and his team were surveying devastation and got hungry — the only restaurant open for miles was a Waffle House, running a limited menu. The further south they drove, the more Waffle Houses they found open while everything else was dark.

G
GREEN — Full Menu
The restaurant has power and water. Area is likely functional. Minimal emergency resources needed. Community infrastructure intact.
Power ✓ Water ✓ Roads ✓ Supply chain ✓
Y
YELLOW — Limited Menu
Power may be out but gas cooking is available. Partial infrastructure disruption. Generator likely running. Moderate response indicated.
Power ✗ Water ? Roads ? Generator ✓
R
RED — Closed
Waffle House is closed. Severe disaster conditions. Maximum response and resources required. If Waffle House can’t stay open, almost nothing can.
Power ✗ Water ✗ Roads ✗ Supply chain ✗

20 Years of the Index

A History in Hurricanes

Since Craig Fugate coined the term in 2004, every major Atlantic hurricane season has tested the Index. Here’s the record.

AUG 2004
Hurricane Charley — The Index Is Born
Category 4, 150 mph. Fugate finds a Waffle House serving limited menu while surveying devastation. The color-coded map begins.
INDEX COINED
AUG 2005
Hurricane Katrina
Category 5. 7 Waffle Houses destroyed, 100+ temporarily closed. Journalist Matt Dellinger maps the destruction by tracing closed locations. The Index gains journalistic currency.
7 DESTROYED · 100+ CLOSED
MAY 2011
Joplin Tornado
EF5, $2.8B damage, 158 killed. Both Joplin Waffle Houses stayed open. Fugate, now FEMA Administrator under Obama, references the Index publicly. FEMA blog post follows.
BOTH STAYED OPEN
FEB 2014
Nor’easter — Southeast Ice Storm
FEMA creates a color-coded Waffle House Index map showing disruptions across Georgia and South Carolina. The Index visualized for the first time as a formal-looking map.
FEMA MAP PUBLISHED
OCT 2016
Hurricane Matthew
Waffle House preemptively closes 25 restaurants in FL, GA, and SC. Pre-storm closures become a recognized warning signal for residents.
25 PRE-EMPTIVE CLOSURES
OCT 2018
Hurricane Michael
Category 5, 160 mph. A billboard hurtles through the roof of a Panama City Waffle House. The image becomes iconic. FEMA’s Alex Amparo tells reporters: “There is no official use for it.”
BILLBOARD THROUGH ROOF
MAR 2020
COVID-19 Pandemic
418 Waffle Houses closed simultaneously. An unprecedented event. Waffle House tweets the Index has gone red. News outlets treat it as a severity indicator: if Waffle House is closing nationwide, this is serious.
418 CLOSED · 1,574 OPEN
SEP 2022
Hurricane Ian
Category 4, 150 mph winds. 35 Waffle Houses closed in Florida before landfall — an extremely rare pre-emptive mass closure. Ian peaked as Category 5 over the Atlantic.
35 PRE-EMPTIVE CLOSURES
SEP 2024
Hurricane Helene
Category 4. All Tallahassee and Crawfordville locations closed pre-emptively. Two weeks later, dozens remained closed across the Carolinas and Georgia.
WEEKS OF CLOSURES
OCT 2024
Hurricane Milton
Category 5, upgraded twice. 33 locations closed in Tampa Bay and Fort Myers before landfall. Residents cite Waffle House closures as their signal to evacuate.
33 CLOSED · EVACUATION SIGNAL

Disaster Preparedness

Why Waffle House Survives

The Index isn’t just a coincidence. Waffle House invests in disaster resilience as a core business strategy. The company has built infrastructure that most restaurants would never consider.

⚡
Generator Protocols
Pre-positioned generators and fuel at strategic locations. A limited menu is designed specifically for gas-only cooking when the grid fails.
📋
Hurricane Playbook
Every employee gets a copy. Instructions for reopening, what to serve without power, how to manage supply chain disruptions in real time.
🚀
Jump Teams
Experienced employees deployed rapidly to reopen closed locations as soon as safety permits. Hotel rooms pre-reserved near affected stores.
📡
Storm Tracking
The company starts tracking storms at tropical depression stage. C-suite executives deploy on-site to manage response from the front lines, not from HQ.
📦
Supply Pre-Positioning
Food and supplies staged in safe storage locations before predicted landfall. Supply chain narrowed to essential items for rapid restocking.
🍳
Limited Menu System
A stripped-down menu designed for degraded conditions: no power, no running water, limited supplies. Optimized for what matters — hot food, fast.

Tension T4

Tool or PR?

The Waffle House Index is both genuinely useful and strikingly good for Waffle House. These two facts coexist without canceling each other.

Genuine Public Utility

Fugate’s observation was operational, not promotional. The Index works because the investment is real. First responders use it because it is useful. Dismissing it as PR ignores actual emergency management value.

FEMA staff cited it publicly during Harvey (2017), Irene (2011), and subsequent events. The three-level system proxies for measurable infrastructure states.

Reputational Infrastructure

Every disaster story featuring the Index mentions “Waffle House” multiple times in a context of civic resilience. This may be the most cost-effective branding operation in American corporate history.

FEMA has never formally adopted the Index. A 2017 FOIA response revealed it was Fugate’s “personal project,” not an institutional methodology. Zero accountability, maximum brand association.
