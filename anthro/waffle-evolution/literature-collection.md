## S1: Medieval Origins

1. **Wikipedia — Waffle**
   https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waffle
   Comprehensive overview of waffle history from oublie to modern forms; covers guild formation, Le Ménagier de Paris recipe, etymology (gaufre, wafla, walfre).

2. **Smithsonian Magazine — "These Beautiful Medieval Wafer Presses Are Where Waffles Come From"**
   https://www.smithsonianmag.com/smart-news/these-beautiful-medieval-wafer-presses-are-where-waffles-come-180963809/
   Primary-artifact focused; examines extant medieval wafer presses, their religious imagery, and transition to secular use.

3. **Smithsonian Magazine — "A Brief History of the Waffle Iron"**
   https://www.smithsonianmag.com/innovation/brief-history-waffle-iron-180972980/
   Traces the waffle iron as a technology object from medieval hearth to electric appliance; GE 1911 prototype noted.

4. **Aleteia — "How waffles evolved from communion wafers"**
   https://aleteia.org/2021/06/29/how-waffles-evolved-from-communion-wafers/
   Catholic perspective on the sacred-to-secular transition; fer à hosties vs. moule à oublies distinction.

5. **Encyclopedia.com — "Waffles and Wafers"**
   https://www.encyclopedia.com/food/encyclopedias-almanacs-transcripts-and-maps/waffles-and-wafers
   Academic encyclopedia entry; good on medieval social context of oublie vendors outside churches.

6. **Wikipedia — Waffle iron**
   https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waffle_iron
   Technical and social history of the waffle iron as an instrument; covers medieval construction, heraldic designs, and electrification.

7. **The Kitchn — "From Wafers to Cones: A Short History of the Waffle"**
   https://www.thekitchn.com/from-wafers-to-cones-a-short-h-113627
   Accessible summary of the oublie-to-gaufre transition; notes Crusades spice trade connection.

8. **Cassidy Cash — "Waffles and Waffle Irons of the 16–17th Century"**
   https://www.cassidycash.com/waffles-and-waffle-irons-of-the-16-17th-century/
   Focused on the 16th–17th century period; good on recipe evolution and social contexts.

9. **Gofrex — "History of waffles from the Middle Ages to the present day"**
   https://gofrex.com/en/history-of-waffles-from-the-middle-ages-to-the-present-day/
   Comprehensive timeline; useful for chronological scaffolding.

10. **Historic New Orleans Collection — "Ursuline Communion Wafer Iron"**
    https://www.hnoc.org/publications/first-draft/what-waffle-iron-look-alike-tells-us-about-female-agency-and-spirituality
    Primary artifact case study: Ursuline communion wafer iron; discusses female agency and religious production.
