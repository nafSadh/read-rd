# Methodology — Religion in the Contemporary World

> An anthropological survey of religion in the contemporary world, examining the global religious landscape across six dimensions.

---

## Research Design

This project follows the paper-drive methodology: literature collection, source assessment, deep reading, per-section deep-dives, and synthesis. It is a **discourse survey** — mapping what researchers, pollsters, and institutions are reporting about religion today — rather than a primary research project.

## Scope

- **Geographic:** Global, with particular attention to the US (best data), Europe, South/Southeast Asia, Middle East, Sub-Saharan Africa, Latin America
- **Temporal:** Contemporary (2020-2026), with historical context where needed
- **Disciplinary:** Sociology of religion, anthropology, demography, political science, philosophy of religion, psychology

## Source Types

1. **Large-scale surveys:** Pew Research Center (Global Religious Landscape, Religious Landscape Study, Religious Nationalism), Gallup International (Global Religiosity Index), PRRI (American Values Atlas), KPMG/Melbourne (AI trust — cross-reference)
2. **Academic research:** Inglehart (existential security theory), Ecklund (scientists and religion), Molteni (insecurity theory refinement), Nature Communications (secularization sequence)
3. **Institutional reports:** Barna Group (atheism tracking), Lifeway Research (Christianity trends), Stanford AI Index (cross-reference for digital religion)
4. **Journalism and analysis:** NPR, Religion News Service, Deseret News, The Banner, Religion Unplugged
5. **Market research:** Global Wellness Institute, Grand View Research, McKinsey (wellness economy)

## Dimensions

1. **The Global Religious Landscape** — Demographics, distribution, growth trajectories
2. **Rise of the Nones** — Unaffiliation, "spiritual but not religious," generational shifts
3. **Fundamentalism & Politicization** — Religious nationalism, identity politics, evangelical politics
4. **Religion & Science** — Warfare thesis, scientists' beliefs, evolution acceptance
5. **New Religious Movements & Spirituality** — Digital religion, psychedelics, wellness economy
6. **Interfaith & Pluralism** — Coexistence models, religious literacy, secular democracy

## Key Tensions Identified

1. **Growth vs. decline** — Religion is growing globally (Islam, Pentecostalism) while declining in wealthy nations
2. **Belief vs. belonging** — Many who leave institutions retain spiritual beliefs
3. **Nationalism vs. pluralism** — Religious identity mobilized for politics while interfaith movements grow
4. **Science vs. faith framing** — Public assumes conflict; historians and scientists disagree
5. **Secular wellness vs. religious practice** — $6.8T wellness economy borrows from religious traditions
6. **Digital expansion vs. institutional decline** — Online religion grows while physical attendance drops

## Quality Standards

- Minimum 5-6 sources per section before building deep-dive
- Every claim anchored to specific data point from named source
- Tensions require sources on both sides
- SVG diagrams for key quantitative findings
- Deep-dive panels target 6-10K characters each
