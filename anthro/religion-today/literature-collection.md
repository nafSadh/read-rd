# Religion in the Contemporary World — Literature Collection

> An anthropological survey of religion today: demographics, disaffiliation, politicization, science, new movements, and pluralism.

**Compiled:** March 25, 2026
**Scope:** Global religious demographics, secularization, religious nationalism, science-religion interface, new spiritualities, interfaith movements
**Method:** Web research across survey data (Pew, Gallup, PRRI), academic sociology/anthropology, journalism, market research

---

## 1. The Global Religious Landscape

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| G-01 | **Pew Research: "How the Global Religious Landscape Changed from 2010 to 2020"** | 2,700+ data sources. Christians: 2.3B (28.8%, down 1.8pp). Muslims fastest-growing: +347M to 25.6% (up 1.8pp). Buddhists only major group to decline (-19M). Sub-Saharan Africa now home to 30.7% of Christians (was Europe). Christianity shrank in 41 countries. Islam-Christianity parity projected ~2070. | Jun 2025 |
| G-02 | **Pew Research: "Religious Diversity Around the World as of 2020"** | Measures religious diversity index across 200+ countries. Asia-Pacific most diverse. Middle East-North Africa least diverse. Singapore most religiously diverse country. | Feb 2026 |
| G-03 | **Pew Research: "How Religion Declines Around the World"** | Mapping patterns of religious decline. Decline most pronounced in wealthy, secure nations. Global South remains strongly religious. Pattern: economic security → individual-choice norms → religious decline. | Sep 2025 |
| G-04 | **Gallup International: "Two Decades of Change: Global Religiosity Declines While Atheism Rises"** | 43,250 persons, 42 countries. Religious: 68% (2005) → 56% (2024). Not religious: 21% → 28%. Convinced atheists: 6% → 10%. High-income: 14% atheist vs 3% low-income. Japan and Germany sharpest rises in atheism. Pakistan and India remain strongly religious. | 2024-25 |
| G-05 | **NPR: "Islam is the fastest growing religion in the world, Pew study says"** | Analysis of Pew 2025 data. Muslim growth driven by fertility rates, young population age structures in Sub-Saharan Africa and South/Southeast Asia. Christianity's decline driven by switching out in Americas and Europe. | Jun 2025 |
| G-06 | **Nature Communications study (U. Lausanne, Oxford, Pew)** | Religion declines worldwide in a predictable sequence. Data from World Values Survey, European Values Study, 100+ countries. Existential security → individual-choice norms → fertility decline → religious decline. Sequence holds across traditions. | Aug 2025 |
| G-07 | **Inglehart, Ronald F.: "Religion's Sudden Decline"** (Oxford UP) | Existential security thesis. Countries with high political stability and low inequality are more secular. "Individual-choice norms" undermine pro-fertility norms, which undermine traditional religions. Applies across all major faith traditions. | 2021, cited 2025 |
| G-08 | **Sociology of Religion: "Rising Security and Religious Decline"** (Molteni) | Refines and extends Inglehart's insecurity theory. Tests across multiple national contexts. Confirms pattern but adds nuance: cultural memory of religion persists even as practice declines. | 2025 |
| G-09 | **Peter Berger: Desecularization thesis** | Former secularization proponent reversed position in 1999. "The world today is not heavily secularized" — two exceptions: Western/Central Europe and international intellectual class. Global South "more religious than ever." Pentecostal/charismatic growth in Brazil, Nigeria, Korea. | 1999, reassessed 2024 |
| G-10 | **Lifeway Research: "9 Encouraging Trends for Global Christianity in 2025"** | Christianity growing faster than population in Africa and Asia. 1.4B Christians in Global South (majority for first time). 600M+ Pentecostals/charismatics worldwide. Translation projects in 3,400+ languages. Church planting movements in Iran, China. | Feb 2025 |

## 2. Rise of the Nones

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| N-01 | **Pew Research: "Religious 'Nones' in America"** | 29% of US adults unaffiliated: 5% atheist, 6% agnostic, 19% "nothing in particular." 78% say can be moral without religion. 64% question religious teachings. 54% don't need religion to be spiritual. Most believe in God/higher power. Very few attend services. | Jan 2024 |
| N-02 | **Pew Research: 2023-24 Religious Landscape Study** | Decline of Christianity may have leveled off. Nones plateaued at ~29% after rapid growth from 16% (2007). Christian share stabilizing around 64-65%. Biggest shift already happened. Key question: is this a pause or permanent plateau? | Feb 2025 |
| N-03 | **Pew Research: "Many Religious Nones Around the World Hold Spiritual Beliefs"** | Global study of unaffiliated. Many retain belief in God, afterlife, spiritual forces despite leaving organized religion. "Spiritual but not religious" is global phenomenon, not just Western. Cultural resonance of spirituality persists. | Sep 2025 |
| N-04 | **PRRI: "Religiously Unaffiliated Is Fastest Growing Religious Category"** | Nones largest single "religious" group in US. Growth from 2% (1948) to 24% (2025). Driven by generational replacement more than individual deconversion. White Christians: 41% of US (down from 54% in 2008). | 2024-25 |
| N-05 | **NPR/Pew: "People say they're leaving religion because of anti-LGBTQ teachings and abuse"** | 67% leave because stopped believing. 20% dislike organized religion (corruption, abuse, anti-LGBTQ). Pull factors: autonomy, modern worldview, meaningful alternatives. 40M Americans stopped attending in 25 years. | Mar 2024 |
| N-06 | **Pew Research: "Why Americans Leave, Stay in Their Childhood Religion"** | Two-thirds left because stopped believing. Religious trauma, cultural stagnation, progressive shift. Many retain spiritual beliefs after leaving. Staying correlated with community bonds, family tradition, personal experience. | Dec 2025 |
| N-07 | **Barna Group: "Tracking the Rise of Atheism"** | Atheists + agnostics doubled since 2013. Gen Z: 18% atheist/agnostic (9% each) vs 4% Silent Generation. Rise attributed to Gen Z entering adulthood, cultural normalization, internet access to secular arguments. | 2024 |
| N-08 | **American Survey Center: "Generation Z and the Future of Faith"** | 35% of 18-29 have no religious identity. Only 33% say religion very important. Gen Z less likely to pray, attend services, read scripture. But: Gen Z men and highly educated showing signs of return to religion in some studies. | 2024 |
| N-09 | **Religion Unplugged: "The Plateau of the Religious Nones Continues"** | Analysis of 2024 data showing Nones growth has genuinely stalled. Not just a pause — structural ceiling may have been reached. Remaining religious population has stronger commitment. "Easy switchers" already left. | Apr 2025 |

## 3. Fundamentalism & Politicization

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| F-01 | **Pew Research: "Religious Nationalism Around the World"** | 55,000 people, 36 countries. Indonesia highest religious nationalism (46%), Bangladesh (45%). US relatively low (6%). Israel only high-income country with >33% religious nationalism. Four questions: religion-national identity, leader's religion, sacred text influence, laws. | Jan 2025 |
| F-02 | **PRRI: "Christian Nationalism Across All 50 States"** | 22,260 adults. 32% Americans are CN Adherents (11%) or Sympathizers (21%). White evangelicals: 67% CN. Mississippi/Oklahoma highest (51%). Strong correlation with Trump vote. Stable since 2022. Church attendance major predictor. | 2024-25 |
| F-03 | **PRRI: "Mapping Christian Nationalism Across 50 States"** (2025 update) | Updated American Values Atlas. CN support stable. 54% of weekly churchgoers qualify as CN Adherents/Sympathizers vs 20% of non-attenders. White Christians (46%) more likely than Christians of color (39%). | 2025 |
| F-04 | **Pew Research: "Religious Values and the 2024 Election"** | 81% of White evangelicals voted Trump. 85% identify with/lean GOP (up 20pp in 3 decades). 61% of Trump's evangelical backers want government to support religious values. 22% want Christianity declared official religion. | Jun 2024 |
| F-05 | **East Asia Forum: "Hindu nationalism threatens India's secular foundations"** | BJP governance since 2014 eroding secularism. Waqf Amendment Act, Citizenship Amendment Act. Violence against Muslims and Christians increasing. 2024 election: Modi used Islamophobic rhetoric, "love jihad" messaging. Nagpur clashes March 2025. | May 2025 |
| F-06 | **ACT Alliance: "Faith, Power, and Politics: How Religious Fundamentalism Is Fuelling Political Extremism"** | Global analysis of religion-politics nexus. Fundamentalism distinct from orthodoxy: seeks political power, rejects pluralism, claims exclusive truth. Rise across Christianity, Islam, Hinduism, Buddhism (Myanmar). | 2024 |
| F-07 | **Kettering Foundation: "Project 2025: Blueprint for Christian Nationalist Regime Change"** | Analysis of Heritage Foundation's policy blueprint. Evangelical policy agenda: traditional gender roles, LGBTQ opposition, church-state boundaries erosion. Broader context of institutional capture strategy. | 2024 |
| F-08 | **Saiya (2024): "Christian Nationalism and Violence Against Religious Minorities"** | Published in Journal for the Scientific Study of Religion. Cross-national analysis showing correlation between political empowerment of CN and physical attacks on minorities. Mechanism: dehumanization of out-groups. | 2024 |
| F-09 | **Azra on Islamic Fundamentalism and Wasathiyah** (Tandfonline) | Indonesian Islamic thought: fundamentalism vs wasathiyah (moderation). Azyumardi Azra's framework for understanding contemporary Islamic diversity. Indonesia as largest Muslim-majority democracy navigating religious nationalism. | 2025 |

## 4. Religion & Science

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| RS-01 | **Ecklund, Elaine Howard: Religion among Scientists in International Context** | 9,422 scientists, 8 regions (US, UK, France, India, Italy, Taiwan, Turkey, Hong Kong). >50% of scientists in India, Italy, Taiwan, Turkey self-identify as religious. Only minority in any region see science-religion conflict. UK: only 32% see conflict. | 2016, cited 2024 |
| RS-02 | **History for Atheists: "The Great Myths 16: The Conflict Between Science and Religion"** | Comprehensive debunking of warfare thesis. Historians of science "long since rejected" Draper-White thesis. Galileo, Darwin cases far more nuanced than popular narrative. "Complexity thesis" is current consensus. | Aug 2025 |
| RS-03 | **Sociology of Religion (2025): Sociological Study of Science and Religion** | New paper examining persistence of conflict thesis through sociological lens. Individualized religious expressions "largely compatible with scientific worldviews." Conflict narrative maintained more by popular culture than by evidence. | 2025 |
| RS-04 | **PNAS: "Even atheists in secular countries show intuitive preferences favoring religious belief"** | Cross-cultural study. Even in highly secular societies, atheists intuitively associate religious belief with positive outcomes. Deep cultural associations between religion and morality shaped by millennia. Suggests "belief in belief" as human cognitive default. | 2025 |
| RS-05 | **Gallup International: Evolution and Creation Beliefs** | Global variation in evolution acceptance. Turkey, Indonesia, Malaysia: majority reject evolution. Western Europe: >70% accept. US: 40% accept unguided evolution (up from 33% in 2010). Education strongest predictor. | 2024 |
| RS-06 | **phys.org: "Did humans evolve to prefer religion?"** | New research suggesting humans may have evolved cognitive biases that favor religious belief. Even secular individuals show implicit preferences for belief over non-belief. Evolutionary psychology of religion gaining ground. | May 2025 |
| RS-07 | **Religion News Service: "New atheism decline"** (synthesized) | New Atheism movement (Dawkins, Hitchens, Harris, Dennett) largely exhausted by 2025. Replaced by nuanced "post-secular" curiosity. Public intellectuals defending religion's civilizational role (Peterson, Pageau, McGilchrist). Science-religion dialogue more productive than science-religion warfare. | 2024-25 |
| RS-08 | **Cabello, Matías: "Science and Religious Dogmatism"** (pre-print) | Economist examines relationship between scientific knowledge and religious dogmatism. Finds that scientific literacy reduces dogmatism but not necessarily belief. Educated believers hold beliefs more flexibly. | Dec 2024 |

## Tensions Synthesis

Six tensions where sources directly contradict each other or reveal deep paradoxes:

| # | Tension | Sources |
|---|---------|---------|
| T1 | **Growth vs. decline** — Islam and Pentecostalism growing fastest ever while wealthy nations secularize rapidly. Global religion is simultaneously booming and collapsing depending where you look. | G-01, G-04, G-07, G-10 |
| T2 | **Belief vs. belonging** — 29% of Americans are unaffiliated, but most still believe in God/higher power. Leaving the institution does not mean leaving the sacred. The "nones" are not atheists. | N-01, N-03, N-07 |
| T3 | **Nationalism vs. pluralism** — Religious identity mobilized for political power (CN: 32%, Hindu nationalism, political Islam) while interfaith movements and pluralistic theology grow simultaneously. | F-01, F-02, IF-01, IF-02 |
| T4 | **Science vs. faith framing** — Public assumes warfare; historians rejected it decades ago. >50% of scientists in four countries are religious. Even atheists show intuitive preference for belief. | RS-01, RS-02, RS-04 |
| T5 | **Secular wellness vs. religious practice** — $6.8T wellness economy borrows meditation, mindfulness, community from religion while religion itself declines. Wellness is religion with the theology removed. | NR-01, NR-03, N-01 |
| T6 | **Digital expansion vs. institutional decline** — Online religion thriving (91% of churches welcome AI, TikTok spirituality booming) while physical attendance collapses (40M fewer Americans in pews). Digital expansion may accelerate rather than reverse institutional decline. | NR-04, NR-07, N-05 |
