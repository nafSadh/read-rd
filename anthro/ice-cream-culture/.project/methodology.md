# Methodology — Ice Cream Culture

## Research Question

> How has ice cream — as technology, social space, cultural artifact, and industry — shaped and been shaped by the societies that consumed it?

## Scope

This survey covers seven dimensions: aristocratic origins, cold chain technology, soda fountains as civic space, industrial mass production, cultural identity and global variation, the nostalgia industry, and environmental contradictions. The time range is approximately the 16th century to 2026.

## Source Selection Criteria

- Peer-reviewed academic sources where available (ResearchGate, MDPI, OpenStax)
- Established institutional sources (Smithsonian, IDFA, Britannica, Wikipedia for factual reference)
- Market research and industry data (Grand View Research, Fortune Business Insights)
- Journalism from dateline-verifiable outlets (HISTORY, National Geographic, Smithsonian Magazine)
- Primary documents via archives where accessible (Food Timeline, Stanford Digital Repository)

**Minimum threshold:** 5-6 sources per section before building a deep-dive. All 7 sections cleared this threshold (7-8 sources each).

## Known Limitations

- Origin stories (cone, sundae) are contested — multiple competing accounts treated as evidence of myth-making rather than resolved
- Market data varies by methodology; figures from Grand View Research, Fortune Business Insights, and Mordor Intelligence are estimates with different methodologies
- Cultural identity sections rely partly on journalistic sources (KERA, National Geographic) rather than academic ethnography; noted where relevant
- Environmental impact data primarily covers GHG emissions; full lifecycle analysis of packaging, labor, land use would require additional sourcing

## NPOV Handling

- No advocacy for plant-based alternatives even where environmental data is stark
- Origin myths presented as sociological phenomena, not resolved by asserting "true" inventors
- Civil rights dimension treated as documented historical record, not framed editorially
- Corporate consolidation described factually without anti-corporate framing
