# Methodology — History & Evolution of Flags

> How the vexillology paper-drive project was researched and built.

---

## Research Approach

This project began as a single deep-dive built from the [flags.fyi](https://flags.fyi) collection of 294 flags. It was subsequently restructured into a full paper-drive project with seven per-section deep-dives, each covering a distinct era or analytical dimension of flag history.

### Primary Source

The **flags.fyi** collection (294 flags, SVG source files, historical metadata) served as the anchor dataset. Every section references specific flags from the collection, with direct links to flag pages where available.

### Research Dimensions

The project covers seven dimensions of vexillological history:

1. **Ancient Origins** (c. 2400 BCE -- 651 CE) — Pre-cloth vexilloids, military standards, religious fusion
2. **The Heraldic Age** (12th--15th centuries) — Codified visual language, Crusader identification, the Dannebrog
3. **Empire & Conquest** (15th--20th centuries) — Ottoman crescent diffusion, colonial flag imposition, Mughal banners
4. **Revolution & the Tricolor** (1572--1848) — Dutch origin, French Revolution, Latin American independence
5. **Decolonization & Pan-Movements** (1916--1970s) — Pan-Arab, Pan-African, Pan-Slavic color families, Indian flag evolution
6. **Design Patterns & Symbolism** — NAVA principles, layout taxonomy, color semantics, symbol families
7. **The flags.fyi Collection** — Statistical analysis of the 294-flag dataset, evolution chains, variant portfolios

### Source Types

- **Primary dataset:** flags.fyi (294 flags with SVG source, metadata, historical context)
- **Academic vexillology:** Whitney Smith's foundational work, FIAV publications, Flag Research Center archives
- **Design analysis:** Ted Kaye's "Good Flag, Bad Flag" (NAVA principles), flag design competitions
- **Historical sources:** Per-era academic histories, Encyclopaedia Britannica, Oxford Reference
- **Wikipedia:** Used for historical context and cross-referencing, not as primary source
- **Institutional sources:** FIAV congresses, national archives, heraldic authorities

### Analytical Framework

Each section deep-dive includes:
- 6--8 subsections with expandable detail panels (6--10K chars each)
- SVG diagrams (timelines, family trees, comparison charts, layout taxonomies)
- Inline flag SVGs at max-width 120px for illustration
- Source citation links (flags.fyi, Wikipedia, scholarly references)
- Cross-references to related sections
- Analytical commentary (not merely descriptive)

### Limitations

- The flags.fyi collection, while comprehensive, is not exhaustive — sub-national, military, and maritime flags are underrepresented
- Historical claims about ancient vexilloids rely on limited archaeological evidence
- Color symbolism analysis reflects dominant Western and Islamic scholarly traditions; East Asian vexillology deserves separate treatment
- The project does not cover flag protocol, flag etiquette, or the semiotics of flag-burning/desecration
