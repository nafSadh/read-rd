# Methodology — Retrocausality, Hope & Depression: A Writer's Physics

## Research Question
How do concepts from theoretical physics — the block universe, retrocausality, bidirectional causation, eternalism — map onto the phenomenology of emotional experience (hope, depression, grief, healing)? And what does this framework offer a writer working at the intersection of physics and confessional poetry?

This is not a physics paper. It's a writer's sourcebook: rigorous enough to stand on its physics, literary enough to be mined for images, metaphors, and structural conceits. The target reader is the poet who wants to know *exactly* what they mean when they write "everything that will happen has already happened."

## Scope & Dimensions
The survey covers 7 axes:

1. **Block Universe & Eternalism** — the physics foundation. B-theory of time, Minkowski spacetime, the Rietdijk-Putnam argument. What does it mean for the future to already exist?
2. **Retrocausality & Bidirectional Causation** — the mechanism. TSVF (Two-State Vector Formalism), Huw Price, Ken Wharton, Yakir Aharonov. Advanced waves. How causes can flow backward.
3. **Hope as Retrocausal Signal** — emotional application. If success is already in the block, is hope the advanced wave traveling backward from it? Prospection research. Anticipatory affect.
4. **Depression as Bidirectional Loop** — the dark mirror. If hope is retrocausal, depression is too — a boundary condition leaking backward. Predictive processing, learned helplessness, the "low hum."
5. **Free Will, Agency & Determinism** — the philosophical tension. Compatibilism in a block universe. Sean Carroll's "your atoms have no wants, but you do." The experience of choosing as a feature of the geometry.
6. **Time, Memory & Embodiment** — neuroscience of temporal experience. Why we feel time flowing. Somatic memory. The body as timekeeper. Rovelli's "time is ignorance."
7. **Literary Applications** — how writers have used these ideas. Vonnegut (Slaughterhouse-Five), Chiang (Story of Your Life), Nolan (Tenet/Interstellar), Borges. Structural conceits for poetry.

## Deliberately Excluded
- Quantum computing / quantum information theory (too technical, not emotionally productive)
- Many-worlds interpretation (covered separately in existing block-universe deep-dive)
- Hard consciousness studies (Chalmers, IIT — tangential)

## Source Strategy
- **Physics foundations:** Carroll, Rovelli, Barbour, Greene, Price, Wharton, Aharonov — books and accessible papers
- **Philosophy of time:** Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, Callender, Ismael
- **Neuroscience of prospection/prediction:** Seligman, Barrett, Clark (predictive processing)
- **Psychology of hope/depression:** Snyder's hope theory, Beck's cognitive model, Seligman's learned helplessness
- **Literary criticism:** essays on Vonnegut, Chiang, Borges and time
- **Existing read-rd material:** block-universe.dd.html, nonlinear-time.dd.html, time-travel-fiction.dd.html — these are foundations, not duplicates

## Reading & Synthesis Approach
Discourse survey with technical anchors. The physics must be correct, but the synthesis is oriented toward writerly application — what images, metaphors, structural conceits emerge from each concept?
