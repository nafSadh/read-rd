## Overall Assessment

This is a mature, well-executed paper-drive project that synthesizes theoretical physics, prospective psychology, somatic neuroscience, and literary craft into a coherent writer's sourcebook. The 7-section architecture (ontology, mechanism, emotional application, philosophy, embodiment, literary output) is logically sequenced and intellectually grounded. 43 sources across 7 sections, all marked adequate. Since round 1, several weaknesses have been addressed. Both HTML papers now include consolidated reference lists with hyperlinked entries. The 00-synthesis.md note has been completed -- the "Key Bridges" section maps 9 inter-section connections and the "Images and Metaphors" section collects actionable poetic images from all 7 sections. The index page now clearly differentiates the two papers (survey paper for academic reference; geometry-of-feeling essay for creative application). The accent color scheme across the deep-dives now uses violet (#7c3aed/#9b7aed) consistently, matching the papers and index.

The remaining weaknesses are: S4 and S5 remain at minimum source adequacy (5 each), no experimental retrocausality evidence is cited, the slide deck has limited SVG coverage relative to its 26 slides, and the survey paper has a duplicate reference number (two entries sharing value="23"). The markdown synthesis paper (geometry-of-feeling.md) still lacks inline citations, though this is acceptable for an essayistic format.

## Content Quality

**Intellectual depth:** The project operates genuinely at the intersection of three domains: quantum foundations (Price, Wharton, Aharonov), prospective psychology (Seligman, Barrett, Clark), and somatic neuroscience (Levine, Porges, van der Kolk). The synthesis identifies a precise structural isomorphism between TSVF's two-state-vector characterization and Seligman's prospection framework, and hedges it appropriately. The six tensions framework is the project's strongest intellectual contribution, holding contradictions as productive frontier zones rather than forcing premature resolution.

**Physics accuracy:** The treatment of retrocausality is careful. Price's time-symmetry argument, Leifer and Pusey's generalization, Berkson's bias as mechanism for Bell correlations, and the distinction between retrocausality and superdeterminism are correctly presented. The project accurately identifies retrocausality as a minority but growing research program. The absence of experimental evidence remains a gap -- TSVF predictions are discussed but no actual experimental results (weak measurement confirmations, delayed-choice experiments) are cited.

**Psychology accuracy:** Roepke and Seligman's reframing of depression as faulty prospection is correctly identified as a causal claim. Snyder's hope theory decomposition is accurately represented. Clark's predictive processing framework is applied appropriately. The observation that none of CBT's 32 adherence-protocol procedures explicitly target prospection is a well-deployed empirical detail.

**Synthesis quality:** The essay ("The Geometry of Feeling") sustains a single argument across 6,500 words without losing rigor or readability. The survey paper provides the same thesis with formal citation apparatus. The two formats now serve clearly differentiated purposes, documented in the index page. The cross-section synthesis note (00-synthesis.md) has been completed and provides a useful writer's quick-reference, with 21 collected images organized by section.

**Writer's application:** Every section deep-dive includes a "Writer's Takeaway" callout. S7 provides six named structural conceits specific enough to be actionable. The connection to two named creative works (Second Seconds and Schrodinger's Gelato) grounds the project in a real workflow. The images collected in 00-synthesis.md are concrete and poetically productive.

## Strengths

- The 7-section architecture has genuine intellectual logic. The progression from ontology through mechanism to application to philosophy to embodiment to literary output is not merely organizational but argues for a specific direction of inquiry.
- The tensions framework (T1-T6) is the project's most original contribution. T3 (prospection vs retrocausality: metaphor or mechanism?) and T4 (depression as broken machinery vs boundary condition) are genuinely productive contradictions. The formulation of T4 -- "the therapeutic answer must be the first; the poetic truth may be the second" -- is sophisticated and ethically careful.
- Source selection draws on primary sources (Price 2012, Leifer & Pusey 2017, Seligman et al. 2013) rather than secondary summaries, alongside accessible treatments for readability.
- The now-complete 00-synthesis.md provides a valuable cross-cutting reference, mapping inter-section bridges and collecting poetic images systematically.
- Both HTML papers now include consolidated reference lists (43 and 42 entries respectively), completing the citation apparatus.
- The index page clearly differentiates the survey paper ("structured for reference and citation") from the essay ("essayistic prose oriented toward creative application"), resolving the round 1 overlap concern.
- Accent colors are now consistent across deliverables (violet #7c3aed in light mode, #9b7aed/#b598ff in dark mode).
- The project is well-scoped, with explicit exclusions (quantum computing, many-worlds, hard consciousness) that maintain focus.
- 17 deliverables across 2 sessions with consistent template usage and design coherence is efficient execution.

## Weaknesses

- **S4 (Depression) and S5 (Free Will) remain at minimum source adequacy (5 each).** S4 would benefit from Abramson, Metalsky & Alloy's hopelessness theory (1989), which directly connects prospective cognition to depressive onset. S5 leans on Carroll for 3 of 5 sources, giving a somewhat one-sided treatment of compatibilism. Neither section has been expanded since round 1.
- **No experimental evidence for retrocausality is cited.** The project discusses TSVF predictions and Bell correlation mechanisms but cites no actual experimental tests. Weak measurement results (Aharonov, Albert & Vaidman 1988 and subsequent confirmations) and delayed-choice quantum eraser experiments would strengthen the claim that retrocausality is "not metaphor" without adding excessive technical detail.
- **Duplicate reference numbering in survey-paper.html.** Two list items share `value="23"` (Beck's Cognitive Triad and Clark's Surfing Uncertainty). Clark's entry also lacks a hyperlink, unlike nearly all other entries. This is a minor but visible citation apparatus error.
- **The slide deck has limited visual support.** With 26 slides and approximately 9 SVG/diagram references, many content slides lack visual anchoring. The ratio is adequate for a text-forward presentation but falls short of the visual richness in the deep-dives.
- **The geometry-of-feeling.md has no inline citations.** This is defensible for an essayistic format, but given that the HTML version now has a reference list, the markdown version would benefit from at least parenthetical author-date references to connect claims to sources.
- **No hero images for section deep-dives (S1-S7).** The image-banana-claude.md file documents 7 planned hero images with prompts, but none have been generated. Only the overview has a hero image configured.
- **Two references in both papers lack hyperlinks.** Clark's "Surfing Uncertainty" and Seligman's "Learned Helplessness" appear as plain text without links in both reference lists. All other entries are hyperlinked.

## Recommendations

1. **Add 1-2 sources each to S4 and S5.** S4: Abramson, Metalsky & Alloy's hopelessness theory (1989) and/or MacLeod & Byrne on prospective cognition in suicidality. S5: Dennett's *Freedom Evolves* or Fischer & Ravizza's reasons-responsive theory to reduce Carroll-dependence. This would bring both sections from "adequate (minimum)" to "adequate."

2. **Add experimental retrocausality citations to S2.** At minimum: Aharonov, Albert & Vaidman (1988) on weak measurements, and one recent experimental confirmation (e.g., Denkmayr et al. 2014 on neutron interferometry, or Kim et al. 2000 delayed-choice quantum eraser). This fills the most significant empirical gap.

3. **Fix the duplicate reference number in survey-paper.html.** Clark's entry should be ref-24 (value="24"), with subsequent entries renumbered. Add a hyperlink for Clark's "Surfing Uncertainty" and Seligman's "Learned Helplessness" in both papers.

4. **Add SVG diagrams to 3-5 more slides.** The deep-dives demonstrate strong SVG diagram capability. Slides covering the block universe, hope-as-advanced-wave, depression loop, and body-time could each benefit from a diagram matching the style already established.

5. **Consider adding parenthetical citations to geometry-of-feeling.md.** Even lightweight "(Seligman et al. 2013)" style references would connect the markdown essay to its sources without disrupting the essayistic tone.

6. **Generate section hero images.** The prompts in image-banana-claude.md are well-crafted and specific. Generating these would bring visual consistency across all deep-dives and strengthen the project's presentation layer.
