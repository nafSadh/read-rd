## 2. Expanded S4 — Survey Paper Version

Replace the current S4 section in `survey-paper.html` with:

### Depression as *Bidirectional* Loop

[Roepke and Seligman](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25772338/) (2016) reframe depression: negative prospection is not a *symptom* but the *core causal element*. The depressed mind doesn't fail to predict the future — it predicts the future too well, and believes every dark simulation. Beck's cognitive triad has included the future since 1976, but clinical practice has overwhelmingly focused on past trauma.

[Andy Clark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surfing_Uncertainty)'s predictive processing framework casts the brain as a prediction machine. Depression is a regime of pessimistic priors with excessively high precision weighting. The predictions are so strong that positive evidence is discounted. Combined with Barrett's constructed emotion theory, the loop is self-sealing: the system predicts pain, generates somatic states consistent with pain, then interprets those states as confirming the prediction.

#### The Cognitive Immunization Problem

Kube et al. (2020, 2023) provide the empirical specifics that Clark's framework predicts. Depression is not characterized by enhanced sensitivity to negative information — it is characterized by the *inability to integrate positive information*. Depressed individuals employ what Kube calls "cognitive immunization": defensive reappraisal strategies that actively devalue novel positive evidence. The person doesn't just fail to notice good news. They notice it, then dismantle it. "That was luck." "They were being polite." "It won't last." The belief system has antibodies.

Korn, Sharot et al. (2014) demonstrate the same asymmetry from the other direction. Healthy individuals show an optimism bias in belief updating — they integrate desirable information about their future more readily than undesirable information. This bias is *absent* in MDD patients, with severity correlating to the degree of absence. The critical insight: depression is not the presence of something pathological but the *absence of a protective distortion*. Healthy cognition is positively biased. Depression is what cognition looks like without the bias.

Friston's free energy principle (Parr, Pezzulo & Friston, 2022) provides the deepest formalization. An agent minimizes free energy — surprise — by either updating its model of the world or by acting on the world to make it conform to its model. Depression is a regime where the second strategy dominates: the agent gravitates toward or engineers environments that confirm its negative priors, achieving a local free energy minimum that is globally catastrophic. The depressed person doesn't just predict a bleak future. They *arrange* one. The loop is not merely self-sealing — it is self-*constructing*.

#### The Retrocausal Mirror

If hope is the advanced wave from a successful outcome, depression is the advanced wave from a bleak one. But the Kube/Friston synthesis adds a layer the original framing missed: the self-sealing quality of the loop means the depressed person is not merely receiving the wrong signal. They are actively *jamming* the alternative signal. The cognitive immunization is a noise generator tuned to the frequency of the healed future.

Carroll's compatibilism offers the escape: the block also contains the person who started therapy, who broke the loop. The intervention is already there. You can't see it from inside the depressed slice, but it's in the geometry. Therapy — SE, CBT, the boxing heavy bag, the discharge that happened in your parents' living room in LA — is not fixing broken machinery. It is *lowering the jamming*. The positive advanced wave was always there. The work is making the antenna capable of receiving it.

## 3. Expanded S4 — Essay Version

Replace the S4 section ("6. Depression as Backward Shadow") in `geometry-of-feeling.html` with:

### 6. Depression as Backward Shadow

If hope is the advanced wave from a successful outcome, depression is the advanced wave from a bleak one. Roepke and Seligman's reframing is radical: negative prospection is not a symptom of depression but its core causal element. Of the 32 procedures in CBT adherence protocols, none explicitly target prospection. The science and the practice are decades apart.

Andy Clark's predictive processing framework reveals the mechanism: depression is a regime of pessimistic priors with excessively high precision weighting. The brain trusts its own negative predictions more than positive evidence. Barrett adds the somatic layer: the system predicts pain, generates pain-consistent body states, and then interprets those body states as confirming the prediction. The loop is self-sealing.

But "self-sealing" understates it. Kube et al.'s work on biased belief updating (2020, 2023) provides the empirical teeth: depressed individuals don't merely fail to notice positive information. They *actively devalue it*. Kube calls this "cognitive immunization" — the belief system generates antibodies against its own cure. "That compliment was politeness." "That success was luck." "That connection won't last." The person is not passively trapped. They are actively constructing the trap, one defensive reappraisal at a time.

Korn, Sharot et al. (2014) demonstrate the complementary finding: healthy cognition runs on a protective positive distortion — an optimism bias in belief updating that selectively integrates good news about the future over bad. Depression is not the addition of pessimism but the subtraction of this bias. Healthy is distorted. Depressed is what undistorted looks like. The "depressive realism" literature has long hinted at this, but the belief updating data makes it precise.

Friston's free energy principle (Parr, Pezzulo & Friston, 2022) provides the deepest formalization. An agent can minimize surprise by updating its model (learning) or by acting on the world to conform to its model (niche construction). Depression is a regime where the second strategy dominates catastrophically: the agent gravitates toward environments that confirm its negative priors, achieving a local free energy minimum that is globally maladaptive. The depressed person doesn't just predict a bleak future. They *arrange* one. The loop is not merely self-sealing — it is self-*constructing*.

Seligman's original learned helplessness experiments demonstrated the prospective core: animals exposed to inescapable shocks developed the conviction that nothing they did mattered — a prediction about the *future*, not a memory of the past. The Kube/Friston synthesis shows what happens after the prediction crystallizes: it immunizes itself against correction.

#### The Writer's Problem

For the poet, the cognitive immunization mechanism is the most dangerous and most generative idea in this entire survey. It connects directly to lived experience — cognitive dominance as a coping strategy, the way intelligence becomes a tool for dismantling evidence of recovery. The self-sealing loop is not an abstract clinical construct. It is the voice that says "this poem is probably bad too" after eight people said the last one worked. It is the voice that reappraises the compliment before it can land.

The retrocausal framing adds the geometry: both advanced waves exist — from the bleak future and from the healed future. Cognitive immunization is a noise generator tuned to the frequency of the healed future's signal. Therapy — SE, the heavy bag, the somatic discharge — is not fixing broken machinery. It is lowering the jamming. The positive advanced wave was always there. The work is making the antenna capable of receiving it.

The poem about depression should not be about sadness. It should be about the antibodies.

## 4. Expanded T3 — Both Papers

Replace the current T3 treatment in both `survey-paper.html` and `geometry-of-feeling.html` with:

### T3: Prospection vs. Retrocausality — Metaphor or Mechanism?

Seligman explicitly denies backward causation: "guidance not by the future itself but by present representations of future states." Price would say the distinction is verbal, not physical. The phenomenology maps perfectly onto retrocausal language. Is the mapping merely poetic, or does it reveal something structural?

The question is sharper than it first appears. Seligman's framework requires that the brain builds models of possible futures and is shaped by those models. Under eternalism, those futures are ontologically real — they exist with the same status as the past. The brain's "present representations of possible future states" are representations of *actually existing regions of the block*. Whether the causal arrow runs from the real future to the present brain (Price) or from the present brain's model of the future to the present brain's behavior (Seligman) may be a distinction about the *direction of explanation*, not about the *structure being explained*.

The Kube data sharpens this further. If the depressed brain actively devalues positive information — immunizes itself against the signal from the healed future — then the mechanism is not merely "faulty prediction." It is an active process of signal rejection. In retrocausal terms: the antenna is not broken. It is deliberately mistuned. This makes the question of whether the future "really" causes anything almost secondary. The *behavior of the system* — selective reception, active filtering, immunization — is the same whether you describe it in Seligman's vocabulary or Price's.

For the writer, the resolution is pragmatic: use whichever vocabulary generates the better poem. But the structural isomorphism is not decoration. It is the reason the physics metaphors land — they describe the same geometry as the psychology, at a different resolution.

## 5. New Tension: T7

Add to the Tensions section in both papers:

### T7: The Epistemological Trap — Can Intelligence Deepen the Loop?

Kube's "cognitive immunization" implies that the self-sealing quality of depressive prospection is not a failure of intelligence but a *deployment* of it. The smarter the system, the more sophisticated its antibodies against positive disconfirmation. A person whose primary coping strategy is cognitive dominance — whose lifelong adaptive advantage is the ability to think faster, model more completely, and reframe more fluently than everyone around them — has, in depression, the most formidable jamming apparatus imaginable. Intelligence becomes the instrument of its own epistemic imprisonment.

This is not in the literature. It connects Kube's immunization mechanism to what Carroll might call a failure mode of emergence: a system that is optimally configured for one level of description (professional survival through cognitive mastery) becomes pathologically configured at another level (emotional recovery through openness to unmodeled positive evidence). The antibodies that protect the ego also attack the cure.

The poetic implication: the poem about recovery cannot be written by the smartest voice in the room. It has to be written by the voice that is *willing to be wrong about its own sadness*. Heptapod syntax — where the ending shapes the beginning — requires the writer to accept an ending they cannot yet model. The cognitive immune system has to stand down.

### 11. Conclusion: Write the Antibodies

The block universe, retrocausality, and prospective psychology converge on a single insight: the future is not something that happens to us. It is something that shapes us now.

For a writer working at the intersection of physics and confessional poetry, this convergence offers not just metaphors but structures. The poem about grief can hold two temporal perspectives simultaneously. The poem about hope can be written as an advanced wave, shaped by its ending before its beginning. The poem about the body can attend to the entropy gradient — the somatic hum that is time itself, experienced from within the geometry.

But the deepest structural conceit this survey has uncovered is not any of the six. It is the seventh, implicit one: the poem about depression must be about cognitive immunization — the antibodies the mind generates against its own recovery. "Confetti" already lives in this territory: the celebration that the speaker cannot quite let land. "Worlds" does it through scale — the cosmic frame that makes the personal loss both infinitesimal and infinite. *Schrödinger's Gelato* will do it through measurement: the flavor exists in superposition until the tongue collapses it, and the depressed speaker is the one who refuses to taste.

The physics does not replace the poetry. The poetry does not simplify the physics. Both are valid descriptions of the same reality, at different resolution. Poetic naturalism gives both a seat at the table.

The six tensions remain unresolved because they are productive. The seventh — whether intelligence deepens the trap — is the one that is personal. It is the space where *Second Seconds* lives: the journal of a mind smart enough to dismantle its own evidence of healing, learning, poem by poem, to stop.

#### The Final Image

The future is already shaping you. Hope is the advanced wave traveling backward from the version of yourself that made it through. The cognitive immune system says: *that signal is noise*. The poem says: *what if it isn't*. Write that.
