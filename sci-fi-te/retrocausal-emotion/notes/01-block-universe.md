# S1: Block Universe & Eternalism

> All times coexist. Your birth and your death are equally real. "Now" is an address, not an edge.

**Sources:** 7 (Carroll, Rovelli, Smolin, Vaccaro, Peterson & Silberstein, SEP, IEP)
**Adequacy:** ✅ Adequate

---

## 1. The Relativity of Simultaneity

Special relativity eliminates absolute simultaneity. If Observer A and Observer B, moving at different velocities, disagree about whether events X and Y happen at the same time, there is no physical basis for preferring one judgment over the other. The Rietdijk-Putnam argument (1966-67) formalizes the implication: if an event can be in A's future and B's past simultaneously, that event must exist — it has ontological status regardless of where it sits relative to any particular observer's "now."

This is not a thought experiment. Time dilation has been measured with atomic clocks on airplanes. GPS satellites must correct for relativistic time differences. The physics is settled. What remains contested is the philosophical interpretation.

## 2. Three Positions

**Eternalism / Block Universe:** All times are equally real. The universe is a four-dimensional static block. Past, present, and future coexist. The experience of flow is a feature of being embedded in the block, not a feature of the block itself. Supported by: Carroll, Vaccaro, most relativists.

**Presentism:** Only the present exists. The past has ceased; the future is unborn. Matches everyday intuition but conflicts with special relativity's demolition of a universal present. Philosophical defenders exist (Bourne, Bigelow) but the physics is against them.

**Growing Block:** Past and present exist; the future does not. New moments are added at the boundary. A compromise — but has its own problems (how do you know you're at the boundary and not in the past?).

## 3. The Entropy Connection

Carroll's crucial contribution: the arrow of time is not fundamental — it's thermodynamic. The second law (entropy increases) is the *only* law of physics that distinguishes past from future. All other fundamental equations are time-symmetric. We remember the past because it was lower-entropy. We experience flow because our brains are entropy-increasing processes.

The Past Hypothesis: the universe started in an extraordinarily low-entropy state. Why? Carroll treats this as a boundary condition, not something to be explained by the laws themselves. Rovelli suggests an anthropic angle: we exist in a pocket of low entropy because only in such pockets can time-experiencing beings arise.

## 4. Smolin's Dissent

Lee Smolin argues time is physically fundamental and the block universe is a mistake. In *Time Reborn*, he proposes that the laws of physics themselves evolve over time via cosmological natural selection. The future is genuinely open. This is a minority position but philosophically significant — it preserves the openness that most humans intuitively feel.

## 5. What Sources Agree On

- Special relativity eliminates a universal present
- The experience of temporal flow is not reflected in fundamental physics
- Entropy is the only physical basis for temporal asymmetry
- The interpretation of these facts (eternalism vs alternatives) remains contested

## 6. Where Sources Disagree

- **Carroll vs Smolin**: Is time emergent (Carroll) or fundamental (Smolin)?
- **Rovelli vs Carroll**: Rovelli goes further than Carroll — not just "time is emergent" but "time doesn't exist" at the fundamental level; what exists is change between events.
- **Vaccaro vs mainstream block**: Vaccaro argues the block universe is consistent with a quantum theory of time that provides physical basis for the *experience* of temporal flow — partially bridging eternalism and our subjective experience.
