# NPOV & Rigor Review — Retrocausal Emotion

**Date:** 2026-04-20
**Reviewer:** Claude (npov-mix-r2)
**Files reviewed:** `survey-paper.html`

## Summary
A seven-dimension survey connecting the block universe, retrocausality, prospective psychology, and somatic theory — framed explicitly as source material for poetry rather than as physics. The paper's methods section is unusually candid: "this is not a physics paper", retrocausality is "a minority interpretation", and the cross-domain mapping is "a structural analogy, not a causal claim." Within that framing, the paper is measured. The second-person-address concern flagged in the task description appears to have been addressed — I did not find instances of "you should"/"you feel"/"watch for" etc. in the physics-explanation sections. The main residual concerns are: (a) the abstract introduces the core reinterpretation with strong framing ("suggest a reinterpretation of the future's influence on the present") that could be read as asserting the mapping rather than proposing it; (b) several physics claims about retrocausality and the two-state-vector formalism are summarized without hedging despite their minority-interpretation status; (c) a few claims about somatic theory (Levine, Porges) are stated more assertively than the evidence supports.

## POV Issues

- `#abstract — "these domains suggest a reinterpretation of the future's relationship with the present" — "suggest" is appropriately hedged but "the future's relationship with the present" presupposes that such a relationship exists beyond the standard causal one; defensible in context of the paper's framing but worth making explicit — suggested fix: clarify that the "reinterpretation" is interpretive/metaphorical.
- `#abstract — "psychological states such as hope and depression can be reinterpreted as reflecting the future's influence on the present, rather than solely as reactions to anticipated events." — "reflecting the future's influence on the present" restates retrocausality as a description of psychological states; this is the paper's proposed framing rather than an accepted one — suggested fix: "can be reinterpreted, within this framework, as suggestive of a bidirectional temporal structure" or similar.
- `#hope callout "The Bridge" — "Seligman explicitly denies backward causation… This conceptualization would require a theoretical framework for retrocausality, which is not supported by the psychological theories of prospection and represents a significant departure from Seligman's explicit stance against backward causation. Such an interpretation would necessitate substantial additional argumentation to bridge the gap between an ontological claim and a causal mechanism." — this is exemplary hedging; the callout is explicit about the interpretive leap. No POV issue here.
- `#block — "'Now' is an address, not an edge." — poetic epigram; it is the paper's own voice — suggested fix: acceptable for a paper that declares it is source material for poetry, but mark as such or attribute (e.g., this is a common informal characterization in eternalism literature; attribute).
- `#block — "A person's birth and death are equally real." — declarative statement of a philosophical position (eternalism) without hedging — suggested fix: "Under eternalism, a person's birth and death are treated as equally real features of spacetime."
- `#retro — "This interaction implies a determination of the outcome across time." — for Cramer's transactional interpretation; "implies" is presented as description but the transactional interpretation is one of many QM interpretations — suggested hedge: "Within the transactional interpretation, this handshake provides the basis for outcome determination across time; this is one of several competing interpretations."

## Logical Consistency Issues

- The paper's methods section firmly disclaims causal claims: *"The mapping between prospection and retrocausality is presented as a structural analogy, not a causal claim."* Some of the section bodies (especially `#hope` and `#depression`) use language that occasionally slips from structural analogy to functional mapping, e.g. the subtitle "Hope as *Retrocausal* Signal" and "Depression as *Bidirectional* Loop". The section titles propose the mapping more strongly than the body arguments sustain. This is partly an intentional rhetorical choice for poetry source material; but for scholastic rigor, titles and bodies should match.
- `#tensions T3 — "However, aspects of the phenomenology are described using retrocausal language. This raises the question of whether such a mapping is merely metaphorical or indicative of an underlying structural relationship." — this explicit acknowledgment of the metaphor/mechanism ambiguity is good, and is internally consistent with the methods disclaimer.
- `#body — "trauma may be conceptualized as a specific configuration within this block. In this framework, the body's trajectory through spacetime (its 'worldline') reflects the persistence of these traumatic states. Consequently, therapeutic processes… can be conceptualized as specific segments of the body's worldline…" — the word "Consequently" implies logical entailment from the block-universe premise to claims about therapeutic trajectories; this is a non sequitur in strict physics (nothing about the block universe entails anything about Somatic Experiencing efficacy) — suggested fix: "Within this interpretive frame" or "As a conceptual device".

## Scholastic Rigor Issues

- `#block — "Special relativity eliminates absolute simultaneity." — standard textbook claim; no citation needed.
- `#block — "The Rietdijk-Putnam argument (1966–67) formalizes the implication: if an event can be in one observer's future and another's past simultaneously, that event must already exist." — cited to IEP (ref-2); the Rietdijk-Putnam primary papers (Rietdijk 1966 in *Philosophy of Science*; Putnam 1967 in *Journal of Philosophy*) should ideally be cited directly — minor.
- `#block — "The second law of thermodynamics is the primary macroscopic law that distinguishes past from future. All other fundamental equations are time-symmetric." — technically correct for the Standard Model at macroscopic energies, but K-meson CP violation is a microscopic asymmetry that is typically mentioned in careful presentations — suggested hedge: "With the notable exception of CP-violating processes, fundamental equations are time-symmetric."
- `#retro — "Price (2012) showed that if the quantum state is real and quantum mechanics is time-symmetric, retrocausality is a logical implication within certain interpretations of time-symmetry." — "showed" is a strong verb for a philosophical argument whose premises are contested — suggested hedge: "argued that…"
- `#retro — "Leifer and Pusey (2017) generalized this: even if the quantum state is merely epistemic, time-symmetry still entails backward causation. They constructed a temporal analog of Bell's theorem." — "entails" is strong; the Leifer/Pusey result is a conditional theorem whose premises are also contested — suggested hedge: "Leifer and Pusey proved a no-go theorem showing that under natural operational assumptions, time-symmetry is incompatible with the absence of retrocausality."
- `#retro — "Cramer's Transactional Interpretation models quantum events as 'handshakes' between an offer wave (forward in time) and a confirmation wave (backward in time)… This interaction implies a determination of the outcome across time." — correct summary; "implies a determination of the outcome across time" is within the interpretation but should be hedged as such — suggested hedge: "Within this interpretation, outcomes are fixed only once both waves are combined."
- `#hope — "The brain's default circuit — activated when subjects are told to 'think of nothing' — is functionally identical to the circuit activated when subjects deliberately imagine future events." — "functionally identical" is a strong claim; Buckner & Carroll (2007) discuss substantial overlap, not strict functional identity — suggested hedge: "overlaps substantially with…"
- `#hope — "At rest, human cognition exhibits a propensity for prospection. The default mode of human consciousness is thus oriented towards the future." — the inferential "thus" extends the DMN-prospection overlap to a general claim about "human consciousness", a philosophically larger claim than the neuroscience supports — suggested hedge: remove "thus" and attribute to Seligman et al.
- `#depression — "Roepke and Seligman (2016) propose a reframing of depression, suggesting that negative prospection functions not merely as a symptom but as a core causal element." — appropriately attributed and hedged.
- `#depression — "the depressed mind does not fail to predict the future; rather, it predicts negative futures with excessive certainty, accepting these dark simulations as inevitable." — "dark simulations" is loaded phrasing; the empirical claim ("predicts negative futures with excessive certainty") should be cited to specific experimental findings — suggested fix: cite specific Roepke/Seligman experiments.
- `#body — "Research in Somatic Experiencing, as documented by Peter Levine, indicates that the autonomic nervous system retains unresolved survival responses" — Somatic Experiencing is a clinical practice with a developing evidence base; the claim is better framed as Levine's theoretical model — suggested hedge: "Levine's theoretical framework for Somatic Experiencing posits…"
- `#body — "Neurological studies by Bessel van der Kolk further demonstrate that trauma induces integrated rewiring of the brain and body." — "demonstrate that trauma induces… rewiring" is strong; van der Kolk's popular book synthesizes research from multiple labs — suggested hedge: "van der Kolk synthesizes neuroimaging and clinical findings suggesting that trauma is associated with durable changes in brain-body regulation."
- `#body — "Stephen Porges' polyvagal theory offers a mechanistic explanation" — polyvagal theory is a contested theoretical framework; Grossman 2023 and others have challenged its empirical foundations — suggested hedge: "Porges' polyvagal theory offers one proposed mechanistic framework" (note: this is a contested theory in the literature).
- `#literary — "Vonnegut's Billy Pilgrim, unstuck in time, experiences his life non-sequentially; the Tralfamadorians respond to death with 'So it goes' — not indifference but the recognition that the dead person is fine in plenty of other moments." — "not indifference but the recognition…" is an interpretive gloss on Vonnegut; acceptable for a survey mapping literary precedents, but should be attributed to a literary-critical source rather than stated as the meaning — suggested hedge: "which critics such as [X] have interpreted as…"
- `#literary — "Louise 'chooses' to have her daughter despite foreknowing the death. But choice dissolves in simultaneous perception. Grief and joy are concurrent." — interpretive reading of Chiang; attributed implicitly to the story but the reading is the paper's — suggested hedge: "A common critical reading…"

## Tone Issues

- `#block — "'Now' is an address, not an edge." — epigrammatic — neutral replacement for scholarly tone: "Under eternalism, the present moment is a location in spacetime rather than a temporal boundary."
- `#block — "A person's birth and death are equally real." — declarative poetic register — neutral replacement: "All events in spacetime are treated as ontologically on par."
- `#block — "We are the source of time." (end of first section) — first-person-plural; attributed to Rovelli's view but reads as aphorism — neutral replacement: "In Rovelli's view, the temporal dimension as experienced emerges from the observer."
- `#hope section heading "Hope as Retrocausal Signal" — the title asserts the framing; the body (including the callout) more carefully presents it as an interpretive move. Scholarly version: "Hope and the Prospective Account" with a subtitle about the retrocausal mapping.
- `#depression heading "Depression as Bidirectional Loop" — same issue.
- `#body — "the body retaining memory of past experiences is well-established" (tension T6) — overstatement; "the body keeps the score" is a popular thesis, not a settled scientific finding — neutral replacement: "The claim that somatic states encode persistent effects of past experience is widely invoked in trauma-related clinical practice and is supported by some neuroimaging evidence."
- `#tensions T3 — "However, aspects of the phenomenology are described using retrocausal language. This raises the question of whether such a mapping is merely metaphorical or indicative of an underlying structural relationship." — this phrasing is well-hedged.
- Overall register is measured and generally academic; fewer tone issues than the culture papers.

## Priority Fixes
1. **Hedge the polyvagal / somatic claims.** Polyvagal theory and several somatic-therapy claims are presented more assertively than the underlying evidence supports. Frame them as theoretical models or clinical frameworks, not as demonstrated neuroscience.
2. **Soften "showed" / "entails" in the retrocausality section.** Price (2012) and Leifer-Pusey (2017) argue/prove conditional results; their premises are contested in the philosophy-of-physics literature. Replace with "argued that" and "proved a no-go theorem showing that, under [X] assumptions, …"
3. **Section titles "Hope as Retrocausal Signal" and "Depression as Bidirectional Loop" overstate the mapping** relative to the hedged body text. Either rename or add a subtitle clarifying the interpretive status.
4. **Mention CP violation** when claiming "all other fundamental equations are time-symmetric." This is a minor but technically important asterisk.
5. **Cite Rietdijk 1966 and Putnam 1967 directly** for the Rietdijk-Putnam argument rather than relying on the IEP entry.

## Confirmation re: Prior Concern
The prior-concern note about second-person address in the physics explanations **appears to have been addressed**. The survey-paper.html physics sections (Block Universe, Retrocausality, Free Will) do not use "you/your" in exposition. Formulations that could have been second-person ("you can imagine", "you should notice") have been rewritten in third-person or passive constructions (e.g., "A person's birth and death are equally real"; "The central organizing feature of human cognition is… prospection"). Good.

## Overall Assessment
**B+** — The paper is self-aware, explicitly interdisciplinary, and the methods section is unusually candid about the limits of the cross-domain mapping. The callout in `#hope` ("The Bridge") is a model of how to flag an interpretive leap. The main residual rigor issues concern minority-interpretation physics presented with stronger verbs than warranted, and somatic-therapy claims presented with more assertion than the evidence supports. With hedges added to the Price/Leifer-Pusey/Cramer summaries and the polyvagal/somatic claims, this would be A−.
