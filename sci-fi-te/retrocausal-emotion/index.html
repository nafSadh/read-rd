Key Tensions

T1: Is time fundamental or emergent? Smolin says fundamental. Rovelli says emergent. Barbour says it doesn’t exist. Carroll bridges.
T2: Retrocausality — real physics or fringe? Price/Wharton say it’s the natural reading. Mainstream physics is skeptical. Bell confused it with superdeterminism.
T3: Prospection vs retrocausality — metaphor or mechanism? Seligman denies backward causation. The phenomenology maps perfectly. The vocabulary differs; the geometry may be the same.
T4: Depression as broken machinery vs boundary condition. Clinical: fix the prediction system. Retrocausal: the body is reading the block accurately. Therapeutic answer must be the first. Poetic truth may be the second.
T5: Compatibilism’s adequacy. Carroll says free will is real at the emergent level. Critics say he changed the subject.
T6: Body time vs block time. If all moments coexist equally, why does the body carry the past forward so stubbornly? The body experiences the gradient, not the block.
T7: The Epistemological Trap. Kube's "cognitive immunization" implies the self-sealing quality of depressive prospection is not a failure of intelligence but a deployment of it. The smarter the system, the more sophisticated its antibodies against positive disconfirmation.
