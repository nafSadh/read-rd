## S4: Depression as Bidirectional Loop

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| D-01 | Roepke & Seligman, "Depression and prospection" (British J Clinical Psychology) | Negative prospection as *core causal element* of depression, not merely a symptom; biased future simulations; faulty prospection drives hopelessness | 2016 |
| D-02 | Beck's Cognitive Triad | Negative view of self, world, AND future; future-orientation has been in CBT since 1976 but underemphasized | 1976 |
| D-03 | Seligman, *Learned Helplessness* | Animals/humans who learn they cannot control outcomes stop trying even when control becomes available; laboratory model of depression | 1975 |
| D-04 | Clark, *Surfing Uncertainty* (Predictive Processing) | Brain as prediction machine; depression as systematically pessimistic prediction error; top-down priors that override positive evidence | 2015 |
| D-05 | Barrett, *How Emotions Are Made* | Constructed emotion theory; emotions as predictions, not reactions; relevance to anticipatory affect in depression | 2017 |
| D-06 | Kube et al., "Biased belief updating in depression" (Clinical Psychology Review) | Depression characterized by impaired positive belief updating — not enhanced negative updating. Depressed individuals use defensive cognitive strategies ("cognitive immunization") to devalue novel positive information. Self-reinforcing loop: persistent negative beliefs → low mood → further resistance to positive information. Predictive processing account: negative priors with inflated precision reject disconfirming evidence. | 2020/2023 |
| D-07 | Korn, Sharot, Walter, Heekeren & Dolan, "Depression is related to an absence of optimistically biased belief updating about future life events" (Psychological Medicine) | Healthy individuals show asymmetric belief updating — integrating desirable information about the future more than undesirable. This optimism bias is absent in MDD. Symptom severity correlates with pessimistic updating. Depression is not the presence of pessimism but the absence of a protective positive filter. | 2014 |
| D-08 | Parr, Pezzulo & Friston, *Active Inference: The Free Energy Principle in Mind, Brain, and Behavior* (MIT Press) | Depression as failure of active inference: agents minimize free energy by gravitating toward environments that confirm negative models rather than updating models. Insecurely attached individuals develop inflexible top-down priors ("It's no use trying"). Depression becomes a self-fulfilling hypothesis — a free energy minimum that is locally stable but globally maladaptive. | 2022 |

## Tensions

| # | Tension | Sources |
|---|---------|---------|
| T1 | **Is time fundamental or emergent?** Smolin says time is physically real and fundamental; Rovelli says time emerges from entropy and ignorance; Barbour says time doesn't exist at all. Carroll bridges: "different ways of talking about the world." | B-05, B-07, T-01, T-02 |
| T2 | **Retrocausality: real physics or fringe speculation?** Price/Wharton argue it's the most natural reading of time-symmetric QM. Mainstream physics treats it as a curiosity. Bell himself dismissed it (confusing it with superdeterminism). Leifer: "the onus is on us to flesh out the idea." | R-02, R-04, R-05 |
| T3 | **Prospection vs retrocausality: metaphor or mechanism?** Seligman explicitly denies backward causation: "guidance not by the future itself but by present representations." But the *phenomenology* maps perfectly onto retrocausal language. Is the mapping merely poetic, or does it reveal something structural? | P-01, R-01 |
| T4 | **Depression as faulty prospection vs bidirectional doom.** Clinical psychology (Roepke/Seligman) frames it as broken prediction machinery — fixable via therapy. The retrocausal metaphor frames it as boundary condition — the outcome already in the block, leaking backward. These have radically different implications for agency. | D-01, F-01, B-03 |
| T5 | **Compatibilism's adequacy.** Carroll says free will is real at the emergent level. Critics say compatibilists "changed the subject" — the felt experience of authorship (libertarian free will) is what matters, and calling emergent patterns "free will" is semantic sleight of hand. | F-01, F-02 |
| T6 | **Body time vs block time.** Somatic experiencing (Levine, van der Kolk) says trauma is stored in the body — the body has its own temporal logic. Block universe says all moments coexist equally. If the body "keeps the score," what is it scoring in a universe where all moments are equally real? | T-04, T-06, B-03 |
| T7 | **The Epistemological Trap: can intelligence deepen the loop?** Kube's "cognitive immunization" implies that the self-sealing quality of depressive prospection is not a failure of intelligence but a *deployment* of it. The smarter the system, the more sophisticated its antibodies against positive disconfirmation. A system optimally configured for cognitive mastery becomes pathologically configured for emotional recovery. The antibodies that protect the ego also attack the cure. | D-06, D-08, F-01 |
