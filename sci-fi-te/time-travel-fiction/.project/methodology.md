# Methodology -- Time Travel in Fiction: Paradoxes, Physics, and Narrative

## Research Question
How has fiction shaped our understanding of time travel -- from ancient mythology through pre-Einstein literature to the 21st-century renaissance -- and what are the intersections between narrative structure, physical theory, and philosophical reasoning that make time travel fiction uniquely productive as a site of thought?

This project expands the original "Before Einstein" overview deep-dive into a comprehensive survey of time travel in fiction across six dimensions: paradoxes and causal logic, narrative structures and temporal mechanics, scientific accuracy, philosophical implications, notable works analysis, and the modern renaissance.

## Scope & Dimensions
The survey covers 6 axes:

1. **Paradoxes & Causal Logic** -- The grandfather paradox, bootstrap/ontological paradox, predestination paradox, Novikov self-consistency principle, branching timeline resolution. How fiction has been the primary laboratory for exploring temporal paradoxes.
2. **Narrative Structures & Temporal Mechanics** -- The five structural archetypes (time slip, fixed timeline, branching, loop, reverse chronology). How different temporal mechanics enable different kinds of storytelling.
3. **Scientific Accuracy in Fiction** -- Real physics of time travel (Thorne, CTCs, wormholes, relativistic dilation, Tipler cylinders, Alcubierre drives). Where fiction gets it right, where it simplifies, and where simplification serves narrative.
4. **Philosophical Implications** -- Free will vs. determinism, the block universe, eternalism vs. presentism, McTaggart's A-series and B-series, David Lewis on time travel, personal identity through time.
5. **Notable Works Analysis** -- Deep analysis of landmark works: Dark (Netflix), Primer, Arrival/Story of Your Life, Slaughterhouse-Five, Tenet, "All You Zombies," The Time Machine, A Sound of Thunder, 12 Monkeys.
6. **The Modern Renaissance** -- 21st-century explosion in time travel fiction across TV, film, literature, and interactive media. The Netflix era, videogame temporality, cultural impact, and why time travel is more popular than ever.

## Deliberately Excluded
- Alternate history as a separate genre (unless it involves explicit time travel mechanics)
- Superhero time travel (MCU Endgame etc.) -- too franchise-specific, insufficiently rigorous
- Time travel in children's literature (separate scope)
- Hard physics derivations (referenced but not derived)

## Source Strategy
- **Fiction primary texts:** Wells, Gaspar, Dickens, Vonnegut, Heinlein, Asimov, Bradbury, Chiang, Nolan (film), Carruth (Primer), bo Odar/Friese (Dark), Le Guin
- **Physics:** Thorne (Black Holes and Time Warps), Hawking (Brief History of Time), Novikov, Morris & Thorne (1988 wormhole paper), Godel, Tipler
- **Philosophy of time:** McTaggart, David Lewis ("The Paradoxes of Time Travel"), SEP entries on time and time travel, IEP on eternalism
- **Literary criticism:** Gomel (Narrative Space and Time), Wittenberg (Time Travel: The Popular Philosophy of Narrative), Alkon (Origins of Futuristic Fiction)
- **Film/TV criticism:** Academic analyses of Dark, Primer, Arrival; Micklethwait (Models of Time Travel)
- **Existing read-rd material:** block-universe.dd.html, nonlinear-time.dd.html, ../../sci-fi-te/retrocausal-emotion project -- foundations, not duplicates

## Reading & Synthesis Approach
Discourse survey combining narrative analysis, physics exposition, and philosophical argument. Each section deep-dive treats fiction as a thought experiment laboratory, examining how narrative choices reflect (or productively distort) physical and philosophical ideas.
