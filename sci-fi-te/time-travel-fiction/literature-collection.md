# Literature Collection -- Time Travel in Fiction

## Fiction: Classic & Foundational

1. **Wells, H.G.** *The Time Machine* (1895). The genre-defining novel. Fourth dimension as navigable axis. Pre-dates Einstein by a decade.
2. **Gaspar y Rimbau, Enrique.** *El Anacronopete* (1887). The actual first time machine in fiction. Spanish-language, largely unknown until 21st-century recovery.
3. **Dickens, Charles.** *A Christmas Carol* (1843). First bidirectional time travel narrative: past, present, future, and return.
4. **Twain, Mark.** *A Connecticut Yankee in King Arthur's Court* (1889). Time travel as critique of modernity. Violence of technology transplanted.
5. **Irving, Washington.** "Rip Van Winkle" (1819). The time-slip archetype: involuntary forward travel via sleep.
6. **Mitchell, Edward Page.** "The Clock That Went Backward" (1881). Early mechanical time travel. Published anonymously in the New York Sun.
7. **Madden, Samuel.** *Memoirs of the Twentieth Century* (1733). Possibly the first time traveler in English literature (an angel courier).

## Fiction: Golden Age & Mid-Century

8. **Heinlein, Robert A.** "By His Bootstraps" (1941). The bootstrap paradox as narrative structure. Self-causing time loops.
9. **Heinlein, Robert A.** "All You Zombies--" (1959). The ultimate closed-loop story: a character is their own mother, father, and recruiter.
10. **Asimov, Isaac.** *The End of Eternity* (1955). Temporal manipulation as institutional bureaucracy. The "Eternals" manage history.
11. **Bradbury, Ray.** "A Sound of Thunder" (1952). The butterfly effect in fiction, over a decade before Lorenz formalized chaos theory.
12. **Vonnegut, Kurt.** *Slaughterhouse-Five* (1969). Billy Pilgrim "unstuck in time." The Tralfamadorian block universe. War as temporal trauma.
13. **Le Guin, Ursula K.** *The Lathe of Heaven* (1971). Dreams that retroactively alter reality. Soft time travel through consciousness.
14. **Dick, Philip K.** "A Little Something for Us Tempunauts" (1974). Time travel as political theater and existential trap.

## Fiction: Modern & Contemporary

15. **Chiang, Ted.** "Story of Your Life" (1998). Sapir-Whorf hypothesis applied to temporal perception. The heptapod language as non-linear consciousness. Basis for *Arrival* (2016).
16. **Fforde, Jasper.** *The Eyre Affair* (2001). Literary time travel -- characters entering novels. Playful metafiction.
17. **Niffenegger, Audrey.** *The Time Traveler's Wife* (2003). Involuntary time travel as chronic condition. Emotional architecture of displacement.
18. **Willis, Connie.** *Doomsday Book* (1992) / *To Say Nothing of the Dog* (1997). Oxford time-travel historians. Rigorous historical fiction.
19. **Liu, Ken.** *The Man Who Ended History* (2011). Time travel as witness to atrocity. Ethical dimensions of temporal observation.

## Film & Television

20. **Carruth, Shane (dir.).** *Primer* (2004). The most logically rigorous time travel film. Overlapping timelines, engineering-mindset mechanics.
21. **bo Odar, Baran & Friese, Jantje (creators).** *Dark* (2017-2020). Three-season German series. Closed causal loops across four time periods. The most ambitious temporal structure in television.
22. **Villeneuve, Denis (dir.).** *Arrival* (2016). Adaptation of Chiang's "Story of Your Life." Non-linear perception as linguistic achievement.
23. **Nolan, Christopher (dir.).** *Tenet* (2020). Temporal inversion as military strategy. Entropy reversal. The temporal pincer movement.
24. **Nolan, Christopher (dir.).** *Interstellar* (2014). Relativistic time dilation as emotional devastation. Kip Thorne as science advisor.
25. **Gilliam, Terry (dir.).** *12 Monkeys* (1995). Fixed timeline: the future cannot be changed, only understood. Based on *La Jetee* (1962).
26. **Zemeckis, Robert (dir.).** *Back to the Future* (1985). Branching timeline mechanics. The cultural touchstone for popular time travel.
27. **Marker, Chris (dir.).** *La Jetee* (1962). Photo-roman. Memory as time travel. The image that haunts.
28. **Rian Johnson (dir.).** *Looper* (2012). Closed loops and the ethics of killing your future self.

## Interactive Media & Games

29. **Blow, Jonathan.** *Braid* (2008). Time manipulation as core game mechanic. Each world introduces a different temporal rule.
30. **Dontnod Entertainment.** *Life is Strange* (2015). Player-driven time rewind with butterfly-effect consequences.
31. **Supergiant Games.** *Hades* (2020). Roguelike as narrative time loop -- death and repetition as storytelling structure.
32. **Mobius Digital.** *Outer Wilds* (2019). 22-minute time loop. Exploration and knowledge as the only persistent state.

## Physics of Time Travel

33. **Thorne, Kip S.** *Black Holes and Time Warps: Einstein's Outrageous Legacy* (1994). Accessible overview of GR, CTCs, wormholes.
34. **Morris, Michael S. & Thorne, Kip S.** "Wormholes in spacetime and their use for interstellar travel" (1988). *American Journal of Physics* 56(5). The paper commissioned by Carl Sagan for *Contact*.
35. **Morris, Thorne & Yurtsever.** "Wormholes, Time Machines, and the Weak Energy Condition" (1988). *Physical Review Letters* 61(13). CTCs from traversable wormholes.
36. **Novikov, Igor.** "An analysis of the operation of a time machine" (1989). *JETP*. The self-consistency principle: physics enforces paradox-free histories.
37. **Godel, Kurt.** "An Example of a New Type of Cosmological Solutions of Einstein's Field Equations" (1949). *Reviews of Modern Physics*. The rotating universe admits CTCs.
38. **Hawking, Stephen.** "Chronology protection conjecture" (1992). *Physical Review D*. Nature conspires to prevent time travel.
39. **Tipler, Frank.** "Rotating Cylinders and the Possibility of Global Causality Violation" (1974). *Physical Review D*. Infinite rotating cylinders as time machines.

## Philosophy of Time

40. **McTaggart, J.M.E.** "The Unreality of Time" (1908). *Mind* 17. A-series vs. B-series. The foundational paper in philosophy of time.
41. **Lewis, David.** "The Paradoxes of Time Travel" (1976). *American Philosophical Quarterly* 13(2). The classic philosophical defense of time travel's logical possibility.
42. **SEP.** "Time Travel" (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy). Comprehensive philosophical overview. https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/time-travel/
43. **IEP.** "Time Travel" (Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy). Accessible philosophical survey. https://iep.utm.edu/timetrav/
44. **Price, Huw.** *Time's Arrow and Archimedes' Point* (1996). Retrocausality, the symmetry of time, and why we privilege the past-to-future direction.

## Literary Criticism & Analysis

45. **Wittenberg, David.** *Time Travel: The Popular Philosophy of Narrative* (2013). Fordham UP. Time travel as narrative theory laboratory.
46. **Gomel, Elana.** *Narrative Space and Time* (2014). Chronoclasm: the tension between determinism and character agency in time travel fiction.
47. **Alkon, Paul.** *Origins of Futuristic Fiction* (1987). Historical recovery of early time travel and future-set narratives.
48. **Prucher, Jeff (ed.).** *Brave New Words: The Oxford Dictionary of Science Fiction* (2007). Documentation of SF vocabulary entering common language.
49. **Micklethwait, Guy Roland.** "Models of Time Travel: A Comparative Study Using Films" (2014). Taxonomy of temporal mechanics across cinema.
50. **Rahwan, Iyad; Shariff, Azim; Bonnefon, Jean-Francois.** "Science Fiction Science" (2025). *Nature*. Using fictional scenarios as formal research methodology.
