Time Travel in Fiction

Paradoxes, physics, narrative structures, and philosophical implications — from the Mahabharata to Dark, from Wells to Arrival.

Time travel fiction is the primary conceptual laboratory where humans have worked out ideas about temporal paradoxes, causal logic, and the nature of time itself. In multiple documented cases, fiction preceded and directly influenced the science. Wells described the block universe 13 years before Minkowski formalized spacetime. Heinlein explored self-consistent causal loops 48 years before Novikov published the self-consistency principle. Bradbury dramatized chaos theory 11 years before Lorenz.

This project surveys time travel in fiction across 6 dimensions and 50 sources — classic novels, modern film and television, interactive media, physics, philosophy of time, and literary criticism. Each section is built as an interactive deep-dive with 8 subsections and expandable detail panels.
