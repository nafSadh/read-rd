## Research Highlights

### ROMA: Recursive Open Meta-Agent Framework for Long-Horizon Multi-Agent Systems
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

ROMA addresses one of the most persistent bottlenecks in production agentic systems: the inability to handle tasks that exceed any single agent's context window. The framework decomposes large tasks into recursive subtask trees, distributing branches across multiple agents that execute in parallel and report results upward through a meta-agent coordinator. Experiments on long-horizon planning benchmarks show substantial improvements over linear chaining approaches, with the recursive structure also providing natural fault isolation when individual subtask agents fail.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00851)

### HiMAC: Hierarchical Macro-Micro Learning for Long-Horizon LLM Agents
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

HiMAC proposes a two-level planning hierarchy: a macro layer that decomposes goals into high-level subgoals and tracks progress across the full task, and a micro layer that handles concrete action execution within each subgoal. The split prevents the macro planner from getting bogged down in low-level details while giving the micro layer a clear local objective at every step. On long-horizon agent benchmarks, HiMAC outperforms flat-planning baselines and shows especially strong results on tasks requiring replanning after unexpected environmental changes.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00977)

## Agent & Tool Use

### ROMA Framework Tackles Context Window Limits in Production Agent Systems
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

The ROMA paper's recursive task decomposition is a direct answer to one of the most common complaints from teams building production agentic workflows: context windows fill up long before complex tasks finish. By breaking tasks into subtree branches that run in parallel across independent agents, ROMA sidesteps the context ceiling entirely rather than trying to compress or summarize past state. The framework is model-agnostic and the authors provide an open-source reference implementation, making it immediately testable against existing multi-agent pipelines.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00851)
