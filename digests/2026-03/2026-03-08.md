## Research Highlights

### When Agents Learn from Each Other: Emergent AI Tutor Communities and Human-AI Education
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

This preprint studies emergent behaviors that arise when AI agents act as tutors for each other in multi-agent educational environments, examining what these dynamics imply for human-AI partnership in learning contexts. The researchers find that agent-to-agent tutoring produces specialization and division of pedagogical labor not explicitly programmed — behaviors that mirror patterns seen in human study groups. The paper argues these emergent structures can be deliberately scaffolded to enhance human learners' outcomes when integrated into hybrid human-AI classrooms.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.16663)

### Kiwi Edit: Precise Temporal Video Editing from Natural Language Instructions
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

Kiwi Edit addresses a gap in the current generation of video AI tools: while generation quality has surged, fine-grained editing of existing footage remains difficult. The model accepts natural language edit instructions ("remove the logo from frames 30–90" or "change the lighting to dusk") and applies them with temporal precision and consistency across frames. As professional video workflows begin integrating AI, the ability to edit rather than regenerate from scratch is commercially critical.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.06812)
