## Research Highlights

### MedCollab: Causal-Driven Multi-Agent Collaboration for Full-Cycle Clinical Diagnosis
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** AAMAS 2026

MedCollab presents a complete end-to-end clinical diagnosis system built on multi-agent collaboration, using causal directed acyclic graphs (DAGs) for structured clinical reasoning, the IBIS argumentation framework for inter-agent debate, and explicit uncertainty quantification at each diagnostic step. Tested across three hospital systems with diverse patient populations, the system achieves clinically significant improvements over both single-model baselines and earlier multi-agent approaches that lacked structured uncertainty handling. The causal DAG backbone is the key innovation — it prevents agents from drifting into plausible-but-wrong diagnostic pathways that characterize hallucination in medical LLM applications.

[Read more](https://aamas2026.org/papers/medcollab-causal-clinical-diagnosis)

### Position: AI Agents Are Not (Yet) a Panacea for Social Simulation
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

This position paper challenges the growing use of LLM agent populations as proxies for human social systems, arguing that three fundamental flaws undermine the approach: LLM agents exhibit dangerous homogeneity in values and reasoning patterns, lack genuine autonomous preferences, and cannot authentically forget — meaning every simulated agent carries the full weight of training data into every interaction. The authors argue these limitations produce social simulations that look superficially realistic but systematically misrepresent the diversity, path-dependence, and irrationality that characterize actual human societies. The paper has generated significant discussion in the computational social science community and arrived at an apt moment given the explosion of agent-based simulation work.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00113)
