## Research Highlights

### Scaling Multiagent Systems with Process Rewards
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

This paper tackles one of the thorniest problems in multi-agent LLM training: credit assignment. Rather than relying on sparse end-of-episode reward signals, the authors propose per-action process rewards derived from AI feedback, giving each agent a clearer learning signal at every decision step. The approach improves both sample efficiency and final policy quality when finetuning multi-agent systems, and the results hold across diverse task types. This is directly relevant to anyone training or fine-tuning collaborative LLM agents at scale.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00841)

---

### Task-Aware LLM Council with Adaptive Decision Pathways
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

Rather than committing all tasks to a single LLM, this framework routes each decision step to whichever model in a heterogeneous council has the best track record for that type of task, using semantic matching against each model's historical success profile. The routing mechanism is lightweight and does not require expensive ensemble inference at every step. Benchmarks show meaningful gains over fixed-model baselines, particularly on tasks that mix reasoning styles — such as multi-step math followed by open-ended generation.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01204)
