## Research Highlights

### MedCollab: Causal-Driven Multi-Agent Collaboration for Full-Cycle Clinical Diagnosis
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

MedCollab proposes a multi-agent LLM framework for clinical diagnosis that uses causal reasoning and IBIS-structured argumentation to model the full diagnostic cycle — from symptom intake through differential diagnosis to treatment suggestion. Multiple specialist agents argue structured positions and a moderator agent synthesizes their reasoning into a final diagnosis, with causal graphs used to surface dependencies that purely associative models miss. Evaluations on clinical NLP benchmarks show strong performance gains over single-model and naive ensemble baselines.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06856)

### Evaluating Multi-Agent LLM Architectures for Rare Disease Diagnosis
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

This benchmark study examines how different multi-agent LLM configurations perform on rare disease diagnosis — a domain where training data is inherently scarce and errors are high-stakes. The authors compare flat ensembles, hierarchical specialist configurations, and debate-style frameworks across a curated set of rare disease cases, finding that structured argumentation architectures consistently outperform simpler ensembles. The work also surfaces failure modes specific to rare disease contexts that general-purpose agent evaluations tend to miss.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.07102)
