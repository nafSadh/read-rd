## Research Highlights

### TraceSIR: A Multi-Agent Framework for Structured Analysis of Agentic Execution Traces
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

As multi-agent systems move into production, understanding what agents did and why across long execution traces has become a critical and largely unsolved engineering problem. TraceSIR introduces a systematic methodology for analyzing, categorizing, and summarizing agentic execution traces — providing structured introspection into decision chains, tool calls, and inter-agent handoffs. The framework is designed to accelerate debugging of production agent systems and lays groundwork for automated trace-based monitoring at scale.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.07241)

### NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Super: 120B Hybrid Mamba-Transformer MoE
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

NVIDIA released Nemotron 3 Super, a 120B-parameter hybrid Mamba-Transformer Mixture-of-Experts model with only 12B active parameters per forward pass and a 1-million-token context window. The architecture delivers 2.2× throughput compared to GPT-OSS-120B at equivalent quality, demonstrating that combining selective state-space layers with sparse expert routing is a viable path to high-capability, cost-efficient inference. Strong performance on reasoning benchmarks makes it immediately competitive with models that cost significantly more to run.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.07380)

### Galbot Robotics Demonstrates Real-Time Tennis Rally with Unitree G1 Humanoid
**Category:** Industry News | **Source:** IEEE Spectrum

Galbot Robotics published video of a Unitree G1 humanoid robot sustaining a live tennis rally with a human player, trained on just five hours of fragmented motion capture data. The system achieved a 96% forehand success rate in simulation and transferred successfully to hardware, with sub-100ms reaction latency to incoming shots. The result is notable less for the tennis itself than for what it demonstrates about data-efficient imitation learning — that small, messy real-world datasets can now bootstrap robust dynamic motor policies.
