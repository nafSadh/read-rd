## Top Story

**NASA Perseverance Rover Completes First Mars Drives Planned by AI** · Industry News

NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory announced that the Perseverance rover successfully completed two autonomous Mars drives — covering a combined 456 meters — with navigation waypoints generated entirely by Anthropic's Claude vision-language models rather than human mission planners. Claude analyzed orbital imagery to identify safe traversal paths, replacing a complex manual planning task that human operators had performed for each drive over the rover's 28-year Mars surface history. The deployment is remarkable not only as a milestone in autonomous planetary exploration but as a proof of concept for agentic AI making safety-critical physical decisions under 20-minute communication latency, with no possibility of human intervention mid-execution.

## Research Highlights

### DyTopo: Dynamic Topology Routing for Multi-Agent Reasoning via Semantic Matching
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

DyTopo challenges the standard practice of fixing a communication graph between agents before inference begins. Instead, the paper proposes dynamically rewiring which agents communicate with which at each reasoning step, guided by semantic similarity between agent outputs. Experiments on complex multi-hop reasoning benchmarks show significant margin improvements over static topology baselines — including fully connected, ring, and hierarchical configurations — because dynamic routing concentrates information flow where it is most relevant at each stage rather than broadcasting uniformly. The approach adds modest computational overhead and is compatible with existing multi-agent frameworks.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10187)

### AI Cancer Metastasis Prediction Model Published in Full
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

Following ScienceDaily's preview coverage yesterday, the full paper describing the cancer metastasis prediction model is now available. The model takes as input routine tumor biopsy genomics data — the kind collected as standard of care — and outputs per-patient metastasis probability scores, without requiring additional imaging or specialist workup. On held-out validation cohorts, it outperformed consensus oncologist predictions on both sensitivity and specificity, with the largest gains in cases where expert agreement was historically low. The authors frame the system as a decision-support tool rather than a replacement for clinical judgment, and note that prospective trial enrollment is underway.

**Category:** Industry News | **Source:** NVIDIA Developer Blog

Ahead of GTC, NVIDIA previewed NemoClaw, an open-source software stack designed to pair DGX Spark and DGX Station hardware with a full pipeline for developing, deploying, and operating autonomous, long-running agents entirely on-premises. The stack targets enterprise customers for whom data sovereignty, latency, or regulatory constraints make cloud-based agent APIs impractical. ## Agent & Tool Use

### NASA's Mars Rover as the Highest-Stakes Real-World Agent Deployment to Date
**Category:** Industry News | **Source:** NASA JPL / New Scientist

The Perseverance deployment sets a new benchmark for what it means to trust an AI agent with consequential, irreversible physical action. The operational context — a robot on another planet, 20-minute one-way communication delay, no human review of individual waypoints before execution, and catastrophic failure modes if the agent misreads terrain — strips away every safety net that production enterprise deployments typically rely on. That Claude passed this test over two drives and 456 meters is meaningful signal for the broader agent community: vision-language models are now reliable enough for safety-critical spatial reasoning tasks, not just information retrieval.
