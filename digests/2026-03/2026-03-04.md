## Top Story

**Silo-Bench: New Benchmark Tests LLM Agent Coordination in Siloed Distributed Environments** · Research Paper

Most existing agent benchmarks assume that agents share a common context or can freely communicate — a condition that rarely holds in real enterprise deployments. Silo-Bench fills this gap by constructing a scalable evaluation environment specifically for testing how LLM agents coordinate when operating in distributed, siloed setups without shared state. The benchmark includes tasks that require agents to infer what peers know, negotiate handoffs across information boundaries, and recover from coordination failures. It is likely to become a standard reference for teams building production multi-agent systems in regulated or partitioned environments.

[Source](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09411)

## Research Highlights

### CollabEval: Enhancing LLM-as-a-Judge via Multi-Agent Collaboration
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

Single-model LLM judges exhibit well-documented biases — positional preference, self-enhancement, and verbosity bias among them. CollabEval addresses this by using a panel of LLM agents as collaborative judges, each providing structured arguments for their evaluations before a meta-judge synthesizes a final verdict. The multi-agent setup measurably improves inter-rater reliability over single-model judging and reduces individual model biases, making it a more robust option for automated evaluation pipelines in RLHF and benchmark development workflows.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00993)

### H-AdminSim: A Multi-Agent Simulator for Realistic Hospital Administrative Workflows
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

H-AdminSim builds a simulation environment for end-to-end hospital administrative workflows — scheduling, billing, prior authorization, referral management — using multi-agent LLMs with FHIR data integration. The simulator is designed for stress-testing healthcare automation systems before production deployment, enabling researchers to surface failure modes in edge-case administrative scenarios without patient risk. The FHIR integration makes it compatible with real healthcare data standards, lowering the barrier to practical adoption.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08823)

## Agent & Tool Use

### Silo-Bench Addresses the Coordination Gap in Enterprise Agent Deployments
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

The practical significance of Silo-Bench is hard to overstate for teams building multi-agent systems in enterprise settings. Healthcare, finance, and legal deployments almost universally involve siloed data environments where agents cannot freely share context — yet no prior benchmark was designed to evaluate coordination under these constraints. Silo-Bench's task suite includes realistic scenarios: one agent holds patient history while another holds lab results, and they must reach a correct joint conclusion without violating data access boundaries. This directly maps to HIPAA-compliant agent architectures and similar regulated deployment patterns.
