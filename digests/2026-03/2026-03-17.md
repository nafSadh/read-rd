## Research Highlights

### CollabEval: Enhancing LLM-as-a-Judge via Multi-Agent Collaboration
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

CollabEval addresses one of the most persistent problems in LLM evaluation: the systematic bias introduced when a single model serves as both judge and de facto arbiter of quality. The paper shows that routing evaluation through a panel of collaborating LLM judges reduces individual model bias and improves inter-rater reliability by 24% over single-judge baselines, lending statistical legitimacy to ensemble evaluation as a standard practice. As LLM-as-a-judge becomes ubiquitous in production pipelines, the multi-agent judging approach proposed here could become the new baseline for reliable automated evaluation.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00993)

### Evaluating Multi-Agent LLM Architectures for Rare Disease Diagnosis
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

This paper benchmarks several multi-agent LLM configurations against single-model generalists on rare disease diagnosis tasks, finding that specialist agent panels using structured debate protocols outperform solo generalist models by 31% on held-out benchmarks. Critically, the structured disagreement protocols — where agents must formally defend and revise their positions — also reduce hallucination rates, suggesting that adversarial collaboration is a tractable path toward more reliable medical AI. The findings arrive as health systems worldwide are evaluating agentic AI for clinical decision support.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06856)
