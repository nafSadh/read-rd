## Industry & Product News

### Anthropic Challenges Pentagon Supply-Chain Risk Designation in Federal Court
**Category:** Industry News | **Source:** TechCrunch

Anthropic's legal challenge to the Pentagon's supply-chain risk designation moved forward in federal court, with filings that frame the case as a test of whether AI companies have a legally protected right to impose ethical constraints on their own technology's use. The core argument is that a designation designed for foreign adversaries and sanctioned entities cannot be applied to a domestic company exercising ordinary commercial judgment about which use cases it will support. Legal experts tracking the case note that a ruling in Anthropic's favor would significantly constrain government ability to compel AI capabilities, while a ruling against would have chilling effects on any AI company's ability to maintain usage policies that conflict with government preferences.

### Apple Introduces MacBook Pro with M5 Pro and M5 Max
**Category:** Industry News | **Source:** Apple Newsroom

Apple announced the MacBook Pro lineup with M5 Pro and M5 Max chips, featuring a substantially expanded Neural Engine specifically architected for on-device AI inference workloads. The new neural engine capabilities are targeted directly at developers running local AI models — a market that has grown dramatically with the availability of open-weight models like Nemotron and Qwen3 that can run on consumer hardware.
