## Research Highlights

### Epistemic Gain, Aleatoric Cost: Uncertainty Decomposition in Multi-Agent Debate for Math Reasoning
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** AAMAS 2026

This AAMAS 2026 paper applies formal uncertainty decomposition to multi-agent debate systems, distinguishing between reducible epistemic uncertainty (which more agents and more debate rounds can resolve) and irreducible aleatoric uncertainty (which no amount of discussion eliminates). The core finding is that naive scaling of agent panels improves performance on problems with knowable answers but provides no benefit — and may introduce noise — on problems where the ground truth is genuinely ambiguous or stochastic. The result has important implications for how practitioners should calibrate confidence in multi-agent reasoning pipelines and when to escalate to human review.
