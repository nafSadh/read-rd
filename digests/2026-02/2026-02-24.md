## Research Highlights

### Framework of Thoughts: Dynamic and Optimized Reasoning Based on Chains, Trees, and Graphs
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

This paper proposes a unified foundation framework that subsumes chain-of-thought, tree-of-thought, and graph-of-thought reasoning paradigms under a single formalism, enabling dynamic selection and combination of reasoning structures depending on the problem at hand. Rather than treating each paradigm as a separate inference strategy, the Framework of Thoughts treats them as instantiations of a more general reasoning graph, allowing a model to switch between linear chains for sequential tasks, branching trees for exploration, and cyclic graphs for tasks requiring iterative refinement. The result is a more flexible and theoretically grounded approach to structured reasoning that outperforms individual paradigms on several benchmarks.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/search/?searchtype=all&query=framework+of+thoughts+chains+trees+graphs+reasoning+2026)

### MC-Search: Multimodal Agentic Search with Structured Long Reasoning Chains
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

MC-Search introduces a framework for evaluating and improving multimodal agentic search — the class of tasks in which an agent must retrieve, synthesize, and reason over both visual and textual information across multiple steps. The paper's central contribution is a method for constructing and training on structured long reasoning chains in the multimodal setting, addressing the well-documented brittleness of short-chain reasoning when tasks require sustained inference across modalities. Benchmark results show meaningful gains over single-step and short-chain baselines, particularly on tasks that interleave image understanding with document retrieval.
