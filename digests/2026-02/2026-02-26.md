## Research Highlights

### Toward Scalable Verifiable Reward: Proxy State-Based Evaluation for Multi-turn Tool-Calling LLM Agents
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

One of the persistent challenges in training LLM agents is defining reward signals that are both verifiable and scalable — particularly for multi-turn interactions where the agent must use external tools across many steps. This paper proposes a proxy state-based evaluation framework that sidesteps the need for hand-labeled ground-truth trajectories by instead checking observable intermediate system states to determine whether an agent is on track. The approach has strong implications for reinforcement learning pipelines targeting agentic tasks, where sparse end-task rewards make training unstable.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00100)

### Position: AI Agents Are Not (Yet) a Panacea for Social Simulation
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

This position paper pushes back against the growing enthusiasm for using LLM-based agents as drop-in replacements for human subjects in social science research and simulation. The authors document specific failure modes — including inconsistent persona maintenance, sensitivity to prompt phrasing, and an inability to model genuine uncertainty or social context — that undermine the validity of agent-based social simulations. It is a valuable corrective to hype and will likely shape how researchers design hybrid human-agent studies going forward.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00113)

## Industry & Product News

### EU AI Act: European Commission Releases Draft Code of Practice on AI Content Transparency
**Category:** Industry News | **Source:** European Commission

The European Commission published the first draft of its Code of Practice on transparency for AI-generated content, a key implementation step under the EU AI Act. The code would require providers to mark AI-generated content — including deepfakes, synthetic voice, and AI-written text — in machine-readable, interoperable formats, enabling downstream platforms and regulators to detect and label such content automatically. While still a draft open for industry consultation, the document sets the clearest regulatory benchmark yet for AI content provenance and will apply to all providers operating in EU markets regardless of where they are headquartered.

[Read more](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/ai-act)

### Hollywood Escalates Legal Fight Against ByteDance's Seedance 2.0
**Category:** Industry News | **Source:** Variety

Major Hollywood studios coordinated a wave of cease-and-desist actions targeting ByteDance after its Seedance 2.0 video generation model produced hyperrealistic clips depicting recognizable actors in scenarios they never filmed. The legal campaign marks a significant escalation in the entertainment industry's response to AI video — moving from lobbying and policy advocacy to direct legal action against specific model outputs. The studios are arguing that training on unlicensed footage and generating likenesses without consent constitute both copyright infringement and violation of right-of-publicity laws.
