## Research Highlights

### Graph-theoretic Agreement Framework for Multi-agent LLM Systems
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

As multi-agent LLM systems grow more complex, one of the unsolved problems is how a group of agents should reach reliable consensus without a central coordinator. This preprint formalizes the consensus problem using graph theory, modeling agents as nodes and communication channels as edges, and proves conditions under which agreement is guaranteed even under adversarial or unreliable agents. The framework has direct applications for decentralized agent networks where no single agent can be trusted as ground truth — a configuration increasingly relevant in enterprise deployments and autonomous research systems.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00121)

### Epistemic Gain, Aleatoric Cost: Uncertainty Decomposition in Multi-Agent Debate for Math Reasoning
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** AAMAS 2026

Accepted at AAMAS 2026, this paper dissects why multi-agent debate sometimes improves and sometimes degrades LLM performance on mathematical reasoning tasks. The authors decompose uncertainty into epistemic uncertainty (reducible through more information or deliberation) and aleatoric uncertainty (irreducible noise), finding that debate reliably reduces epistemic uncertainty but can amplify aleatoric error when agents reinforce each other's systematic mistakes. The result is a clearer design principle: debate architectures should include mechanisms to detect and break correlated errors, not just aggregate votes.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00145)
