## Research Highlights

### When Agents Fail to Act: A Taxonomy of Tool-Use Failures in Multi-Agent LLM Systems
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv / VoltAgent

Tool-use reliability is one of the least-studied failure surfaces in deployed LLM agents, and this paper addresses it systematically by presenting a 12-category error taxonomy covering everything from incorrect tool selection and malformed arguments to premature task termination and cascading failures across agent handoffs. The authors evaluate both open-weight and proprietary models against a diagnostic benchmark designed to stress-test each failure category, finding that even frontier models fail reliably on a subset of tool-use scenarios — particularly those involving ambiguous state or mid-task error recovery. The taxonomy provides a shared vocabulary that practitioners and researchers can use to characterize and compare agent reliability.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00189)

### DyTopo: Dynamic Topology Routing for Multi-Agent Reasoning via Semantic Matching
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

Most multi-agent reasoning architectures use fixed communication topologies — every agent always talks to the same set of neighbors. DyTopo challenges this assumption by dynamically rewiring agent-to-agent connections at each reasoning step based on semantic similarity between intermediate outputs, routing information toward agents best positioned to evaluate or extend a particular line of reasoning. Experiments across math, coding, and logical reasoning benchmarks show consistent improvements over static topologies, with particular gains on tasks requiring diverse sub-skills that no single fixed configuration handles well.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00204)

### Survey: More Than Half of American Teenagers Now Use AI on Schoolwork
**Category:** Industry News | **Source:** Pew Research Center

A new survey found that over 50% of American teenagers report using AI tools for schoolwork, with 10% saying they rely on chatbots for all or most assignments. More strikingly, 59% of surveyed teens reported personally knowing classmates who use AI to cheat. The data arrives at a moment when most school districts still lack coherent policies on AI use, and when the tools students are accessing — including full reasoning models capable of producing sophisticated analytical writing — have outpaced the detection capabilities of most academic integrity software. The survey will likely accelerate policy debates that have so far moved slowly in K-12 education.

## Agent & Tool Use

### Block's AI-Driven Restructuring: A Case Study in Operational Agentic AI
**Category:** Industry News | **Source:** Reuters / The Verge

Jack Dorsey's public comments on the Block layoffs named three specific domains where AI agents are now handling work previously done by human employees: code review, customer support, and financial analysis. Each represents a different agentic profile — code review requires multi-file context and judgment about correctness and style; customer support requires real-time dialogue management and integration with backend systems; financial analysis requires structured data retrieval, calculation, and narrative generation. The fact that Block is replacing headcount rather than augmenting it in these areas signals that at least one major technology company has crossed the threshold from AI-assisted to AI-operated workflows in production.
