## Research Highlights

### Agentic Reasoning for Large Language Models: A Survey
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

This comprehensive survey organizes the rapidly growing field of agentic LLM reasoning into three distinct architectural layers. The foundational layer covers planning, tool use, and search; the self-evolving layer addresses feedback loops, memory mechanisms, and adaptation over time; and the collective multi-agent layer examines coordination strategies and knowledge sharing across agent networks. By providing a unified taxonomy, the paper gives researchers a clearer roadmap for understanding where current systems fall short and where the most promising open problems lie.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.12538)

### Towards Responsible and Explainable AI Agents with Consensus-Driven Reasoning
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv

This paper proposes a multi-agent architecture in which a consortium of heterogeneous LLM and VLM agents each independently generates outputs for a given task, and a dedicated reasoning agent then consolidates those outputs through consensus. The consolidation step enforces safety constraints, filters contradictory responses, and actively works to mitigate hallucination before a final answer is surfaced. The approach is notable for treating safety and explainability not as post-hoc filters but as structural properties baked into the reasoning pipeline itself.

[Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.21699)

## Agent & Tool Use

### Reasoning Agents and Nash Equilibria: Zero-Shot Strategic Behavior
**Category:** Research Paper | **Source:** arXiv / AI Research

New theoretical and empirical work presents evidence that off-the-shelf reasoning AI agents — models trained purely on chain-of-thought reasoning without explicit game-theoretic post-training — achieve Nash-like equilibrium play in strategic settings essentially zero-shot. The finding is surprising because Nash equilibrium behavior has historically required either explicit reward modeling or multi-agent self-play training. The result suggests that sufficiently capable reasoning models may internalize strategic rationality as a general emergent capability, with implications for how we model AI behavior in competitive and adversarial environments, including multi-agent deployments.
