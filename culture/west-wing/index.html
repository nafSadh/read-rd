The West Wing & Global Politics

Sorkin’s idealism, democratic backsliding, and two fictions about what happens when the folding chairs come out.

The West Wing presented American governance as a workplace drama where smart, exhausted people argued in good faith and the system basically worked. Two decades later, the system is under stress globally — executive aggrandizement, press erosion, the death of bipartisan reflex. This project surveys the gap between Sorkin’s Washington and the real one, then asks: if the old institutions are failing, what grows in the subtracted space?

The research covers 4 dimensions across 34 sources — idealism vs. realism, executive power, bipartisanship, and media/press — with a democratic backsliding slide deck drawing on V-Dem, Freedom House, IDEA, and RSF data. Two companion fictions explore direct democracy from opposite angles: one hopeful, one structural.
