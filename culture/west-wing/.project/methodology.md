# Methodology — West Wing & Global Politics Today

## Research Question
How does The West Wing's political worldview map onto the realities of global politics in 2026? Specifically: where do its assumptions about idealism, executive power, bipartisanship, and media now stand relative to the discourse?

## Scope & Dimensions
Four axes:
1. **Idealism vs Realism in Foreign Policy** — the show's domestic pragmatism vs foreign-policy hawkishness, read against contemporary IR debates
2. **Executive Power & Institutional Decay** — Bartlet's self-restraint vs the literature on executive aggrandizement and democratic backsliding
3. **Bipartisanship as Fantasy vs Aspiration** — the show's good-faith opposition vs affective polarization
4. **Media, Press & Democracy** — CJ's briefing room vs 2025-26 press freedom landscape

Deliberately excluded: casting/representation politics, Sorkin's personal controversies, comparison to other political dramas (House of Cards, Veep) except where directly relevant to discourse.

## Source Strategy
- Web search across scholarly, journalistic, and opinion sources
- Priority to named critics, political scientists, and media scholars
- Mixed timeframe: 2013-2026, with emphasis on post-2016 and post-2024 reevaluations
- Key anchor sources: Carnegie Endowment, Levitsky & Ziblatt, CJR, RSF, WaPo/Conversation 2026

## Reading & Synthesis Approach
Discourse survey: synthesizing what commentators, scholars, and critics are saying about the show's relevance (or irrelevance) to contemporary politics. Not a technical survey of the show's content itself.
