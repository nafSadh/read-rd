Misinformation Ate April Fools

When reality became indistinguishable from parody, the one day we agreed to lie together quietly died.

April Fools’ Day requires a shared baseline of truth to subvert. Misinformation, algorithmic context collapse, deepfakes, and post-truth politics have eroded that baseline — and with it, the cultural conditions for sanctioned play. Google stopped its pranks in 2020 and never came back. The Onion can’t outpace real headlines. This project surveys the intersection of trust erosion and humor collapse across 8 dimensions.
