# Cross-Section Synthesis — Attachment Theory

## Emerging Theme: The Science-to-Folk Pipeline

The most interesting finding across sections is not about attachment itself but about how a moderate scientific finding becomes pop culture dogma. The pipeline:

1. **Real observation** (Ainsworth): infants behave differently in separation-reunion scenarios
2. **Taxonomic leap** (Ainsworth → Main): behaviors become discrete "styles"
3. **Domain extension** (Hazan & Shaver): infant styles → adult romantic patterns
4. **Pop simplification** (Levine & Heller, Instagram): styles become identity labels
5. **Cultural function** (subreddits, TikTok): labels become diagnostic tools for evaluating partners

At each step, nuance is lost and certainty is gained. The dimensional becomes categorical. The contextual becomes global. The descriptive becomes prescriptive.

This pattern — real science → overextended framework → cultural folk theory — is shared with MBTI (Jungian types → 16 categories → personality identity) and to some extent the Big Five (though that one has held up better empirically).

## Key Unresolved Tension

The biggest unresolved question across sections: **Is there actually a stable "attachment prototype" that persists from infancy to adulthood, or is attachment fully context-dependent?**

Fraley's data supports a weak prototype (r≈.30 in childhood, r≈.50 in adulthood). But "weak prototype" is a long way from "your childhood determines your love life" — which is how the theory gets used in practice.
