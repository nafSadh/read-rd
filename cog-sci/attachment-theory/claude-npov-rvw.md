# NPOV & Rigor Review — Attachment Theory

**Date:** 2026-04-20
**Reviewer:** Claude (npov-cog1-r2)
**Files reviewed:** cog-sci/attachment-theory/survey-paper.html

## Summary
This survey maps attachment theory across six dimensions and deliberately adopts a "useful rather than true" framing, marshalling critics (Kagan, Keller, Mesman, Vicedo) alongside mainstream exponents. Prose is generally scholarly and hedged; most empirical claims carry inline citations with tooltip links. The biggest concern is framing bias: the paper is organized around a single critical perspective ("useful rather than true") and the abstract presents that perspective's conclusions — Strange Situation measures temperament, secure ideal encodes Western norms — as though they were settled. A secondary concern is a small cluster of uncited statistics (distribution percentages, AAI/ECR convergence strength) and the uncritical adoption of contested second-hand cross-cultural figures (52% N. Germany, 0% Japan).

## POV Issues
- `survey-paper.html#abstract` — "If this interpretation holds, attachment 'styles' could be substantially re-labeled temperamental traits." — conditional framing is fine; acceptable. No true first/second-person issues found here.
- `survey-paper.html#critique` — "If this interpretation holds, attachment 'styles' could be substantially re-labeled temperamental traits." — borderline: attributes the conditional to Kagan's position rather than asserting it; acceptable.
- `survey-paper.html#healing` — "If attachment patterns are not fixed, how do they change?" — rhetorical question. Minor persuasive framing; suggested rewrite: "The question of how attachment patterns change, given that they are not fixed, is addressed by the concept of earned security."
- No second-person "you/your" in exposition; no first-person "I/we/our" found.

## Logical Consistency Issues
- `survey-paper.html#critique` vs `survey-paper.html#synthesis` — S5 reports Kagan's prediction that attachment theory would become "a historical footnote" and notes it "has not materialized as anticipated." S7's "Attachment vs. Temperament" tension then states "Both factors likely contribute, though their relative proportions remain undetermined" — this is a reasonable synthesis but is itself an unsourced claim of consensus between positions neither side has actually ceded.
- `survey-paper.html#origins` vs `survey-paper.html#critique` — S1 presents the 52% N. Germany / 0% Japan figures as factual without caveats ("cross-cultural studies have revealed significant variations"); S5 repeats them, then S7 amplifies them in Tension 04. The same uncaveated figures are used to underwrite a strong conclusion ("suggest that the concept of 'secure' attachment may reflect Western cultural norms") — not a contradiction, but the repetition reinforces an unverified premise.

## Scholastic Rigor Issues
- `survey-paper.html#abstract` — "The 'secure' ideal encodes Western independence norms that produce 52% 'avoidant' classifications in northern Germany and 0% in Japan." — asserts a causal encoding ("encodes") rather than a correlation; the 0% Japan figure is widely cited but traces back to small single-site studies with contested classification criteria (Takahashi 1986, n=60). Suggested hedge: "Cross-cultural SSP studies have reported divergent distributions, e.g., elevated avoidant classifications in a northern German sample and near-zero avoidant classifications in a Japanese sample, which critics argue reflects culturally variable parenting norms rather than universal patterns."
- `survey-paper.html#abstract` — "A prominent critical perspective frames the theory as useful rather than true." — the phrase "useful rather than true" is a paraphrase presented in quotes without attribution to a specific source; if this is an authorial summary rather than a direct quotation, it should not carry quotation marks. Suggested fix: drop quotes or attribute to specific critic.
- `survey-paper.html#taxonomy` — "A notable issue is that the AAI and ECR exhibit only weak correlations with each other." — "weak" is undefined; no correlation value or citation given in the same sentence. The stat row then reports "Low" in the same vague register. Suggested hedge: provide numeric range with source (typical reported values r≈.09–.20 between AAI classification and self-report measures).
- `survey-paper.html#taxonomy` — "a phenomenon Fraley terms 'convergence failure.'" — "convergence failure" in quotes suggests a named Fraley construct; this phrasing is not standard in Fraley's published work, which more commonly uses "low cross-method convergence" or "modest agreement." If the term is a paraphrase, drop the quotes.
- `survey-paper.html#origins` — "Flaherty & Sadler (2011) report an approximate distribution of these patterns: 60–65% secure, 20–25% avoidant, and 10–15% anxious-resistant." — attributing classic SSP base rates to a single 2011 clinical-practice paper rather than to Ainsworth et al. (1978) or the van IJzendoorn meta-analyses is imprecise sourcing.
- `survey-paper.html#adult` — "Fraley et al. (2021), studying over 4,000 adults, found that 50% of significant life events triggered immediate shifts in attachment orientation, with 25% producing lasting change." — specific figures (50%, 25%) should be verifiable against the source; current phrasing presents them as round percentages without the reported effect size or statistical threshold used to define "shift." Suggested hedge: add the study's definition of shift and the measurement window.
- `survey-paper.html#neuro` — "PVT has attracted criticism from neuroscientists." — followed by sourcing via Wikipedia rather than the primary Grossman or Neuhuber-Berthoud papers. Methods section acknowledges this limitation; suggested fix: cite at least one primary critical paper (e.g., Grossman 2023, Neuhuber & Berthoud 2022) inline.
- `survey-paper.html#healing` — "Brom et al. (2017) provide strong RCT evidence for Somatic Experiencing, reporting that 15 sessions produced Cohen's d values of 0.94–1.26 for PTSD symptoms and 0.7 for depression." — "strong RCT evidence" is an editorial judgment on a single-trial result; SE evidence base is generally considered preliminary. Suggested hedge: "Brom et al. (2017) report a single RCT of Somatic Experiencing showing large effect sizes (d = 0.94–1.26 for PTSD, 0.7 for depression); this finding awaits independent replication."
- `survey-paper.html#healing` — "ICEEFT's research summary reports that Emotionally Focused Therapy improves outcomes for 70% of couples, with decreases in attachment avoidance predicting relationship satisfaction and fMRI evidence showing changes in threat response after treatment." — ICEEFT is the advocacy/training body for EFT; its research summary is not an independent source. Citing it for efficacy figures inside an empirical section without noting the conflict is a sourcing weakness. Suggested hedge: attribute the 70% figure to its origin (Johnson et al. 1999) or flag ICEEFT as the institutional sponsor.
- `survey-paper.html#healing` — "The American Journal of Psychotherapy (2016) documents a narrative pathway involving long-term psychodynamic therapy for avoidant personality disorder, reporting a 31% baseline remission rate." — "baseline remission rate" is ambiguous (pre-treatment? control arm? natural history?); figure cannot be evaluated without clarification.
- `survey-paper.html#synthesis` — "One contribution identified in the literature is the concept of earned security" — positive framing ("contribution") as fact; earned security is itself a contested construct (AAI-derived classification with known reliability issues). Suggested hedge: "One widely discussed concept in the literature is earned security."

## Tone Issues
- `survey-paper.html#hero` (paper-title) — "Useful Rather Than True" — the title endorses one critical position as the paper's thesis. Because this phrase is not attributed to an identifiable scholar (it is used colloquially in the critique literature) and the paper presents it as its own framing, the title itself carries authorial opinion. Neutral alternative: "Attachment Theory: Science, Pop Adaptation, and Critique" or similar descriptive title.
- `survey-paper.html#hero` (subtitle) — "what the science actually shows, where the pop psychology version diverges, and why a prominent critical perspective frames it as 'useful rather than true.'" — "actually shows" is mildly persuasive (implying the popular version is fake); "why" presupposes the critical frame is the correct lens. Neutral rewrite: "reviewing the empirical findings, noting divergences between academic and popular-psychology presentations, and summarizing the 'useful rather than true' critical perspective."
- `survey-paper.html#critique` — "the commodification of attachment 'styles' on social media, where they are used as identity labels, diagnostic tools for evaluating partners, and content for influencers" — "commodification" carries an editorial judgment; source (Refinery29) uses similar language but quoting the framing rather than asserting it would be more neutral.
- `survey-paper.html#critique` — "The Swaddle frames the cultural adoption as 'TikTokification' — the conversion of tentative scientific findings into certain identity claims." — "the conversion of tentative scientific findings into certain identity claims" reads as authorial endorsement of the Swaddle's framing. Suggested fix: close the quotation at "TikTokification" and note "a characterization The Swaddle uses to describe the simplification pipeline."
- `survey-paper.html#critique` — "UnHerd (2023) compares attachment styles culturally to horoscopes, noting their perceived truthfulness due to sufficient vagueness to fit various individuals, categorical nature to feel definitive, and explanatory power to satisfy narrative needs." — lists UnHerd's rhetorical points without explicit distancing; reads as paper endorsement. Suggested fix: add "UnHerd argues that..." to make attribution explicit throughout the list.
- `survey-paper.html#synthesis` — "At each step, nuance tends to diminish while perceived certainty increases. The dimensional becomes categorical. The contextual becomes global. The descriptive becomes prescriptive." — three terse declarative sentences in aphoristic register; stylistically elegant but editorial. Neutral rewrite: "Critics argue that at each step the representation becomes more categorical, more globally applicable, and more prescriptive than the underlying science warrants."

## Priority Fixes
1. Title and subtitle commit the paper to the "useful rather than true" thesis; this is the most consequential NPOV issue because it sets the interpretive frame for everything that follows. Retitle to a descriptive alternative or explicitly attribute the phrase.
2. Cross-cultural SSP figures (52% N. Germany / 0% Japan) are presented uncritically three times; these figures come from small single-site studies (Grossmann et al., Takahashi) whose generalizability is contested. Add hedging language and source the studies directly.
3. ICEEFT (trade association) and Wikipedia are cited for substantive empirical claims in healing and neuro sections; substitute primary sources (Johnson et al. for EFT efficacy; Grossman 2023 or Neuhuber & Berthoud 2022 for polyvagal critique).
4. Uncited or loosely-cited statistics — AAI/ECR correlation, EFT 70% improvement, SE Cohen's d — should carry inline numeric sources in the paragraph where they appear, not only in the reference list.
5. Rhetorical aphorisms ("The dimensional becomes categorical…") in Synthesis should be converted to attributed-claim form.

## Overall Assessment
**B** — The paper is well-structured, heavily cited, and its methods/limitations section already concedes several of the weaknesses flagged here (English-language bias, reliance on secondary sources for primary papers, polyvagal coverage via Wikipedia). Most prose is suitably hedged, cites are consistent, and logical structure is sound. The grade is held at B rather than raised because the paper commits itself at the title-and-abstract level to a single critical thesis ("useful rather than true") and treats that thesis's conclusions as the paper's own findings in several passages. For a survey, the critical perspective should be presented alongside the theory's mainstream defenses with more parity, not adopted as the organizing argument.
