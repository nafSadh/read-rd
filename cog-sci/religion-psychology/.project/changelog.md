**Decisions made:**
- Placed in `cog-sci/religion-psychology/` — this is cognitive science and empirical psychology, not theology
- Balanced framing: religion as natural phenomenon, neither defending nor attacking
- Each section addresses "what the evidence actually shows" with explicit attention to methodological limitations
- Scrupulosity and religious trauma given equal weight alongside mental health benefits
- The 2024 prosociality meta-analysis (r=.13, self-report bias) is a central finding for S4
