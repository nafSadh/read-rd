# S2 Notes — Psychology of Belief

## Core Claim
Cognitive biases that serve us well in everyday life — pattern recognition, teleological reasoning, dualism intuition, anthropomorphism — naturally incline the human mind toward religious conclusions.

## Key Findings
- **Willard-Norenzayan path model (2013):** Mentalizing, dualism, teleology, anthropomorphism independently predict belief. Dualism mediates mentalizing→belief link. Replicated cross-culturally (Czech/Slovak).
- **Dual-process theory:** Religious belief is a System 1 (fast, intuitive) product. System 2 (slow, analytical) can override but rarely does. CRT scores inversely correlate with religiosity.
- **Analytic atheist effect:** Atheists score 18.7% higher on composite analytic thinking measures (50K+ participants, 16 countries). BUT: effect varies dramatically across cultures. In some countries, disappears entirely.
- **Teleological thinking (Kelemen):** Children are "promiscuous teleologists." Adults under cognitive load revert. Even scientists endorse teleological statements under time pressure.
- **Motivated reasoning:** Confirmation bias, attribution bias, identity-protective cognition maintain beliefs once formed. Religious motivated reasoning has unusually high stakes (meaning, mortality, belonging).

## Critical Nuances
- Analytical thinking predicts questioning local norms, not atheism specifically
- Cultural context determines which cognitive potentials are actualized
- Many highly analytical people are deeply religious — the model explains variance, not determination
- The experiential dimension (mystical states, conversion) is poorly captured by the bias model
