# S6 Notes — Neuroscience of Religious Experience

## Newberg's Neurotheology
- SPECT imaging of prayer/meditation across traditions
- Prefrontal cortex ↑ (focused attention) during prayer
- Superior parietal lobe ↓ (self-other boundary dissolution) during mystical states
- Explicitly non-reductive: brain mediates spiritual experience, doesn't explain it away
- 2025 paper: practical clinical applications for integrative psychiatry

## The "God Spot" Debate — Resolved
- No single brain region for religious experience
- Distributed network model: prefrontal (intentionality), temporal (narrative/meaning), parietal (self-boundary), DMN (self-reference), ToM network (attributing intentions to God)
- Different religious activities activate different networks

## Psilocybin & Mystical Experience (Johns Hopkins)
- Griffiths 2006: psilocybin reliably occasions mystical experiences; 67% rated top-5 life experiences
- 14-month persistence; personality changes (Openness ↑)
- Cancer anxiety: substantial decreases in depression/anxiety
- 2018: psilocybin + meditation → enduring prosocial attitude changes
- Neural mechanism: claustrum suppressed → ego dissolution; DMN disrupted
- 2024 (Yaden): beyond mystical experience — emotional breakthrough, insight, connectedness
- $55M Hopkins research center

## Temporal Lobe Epilepsy
- 0.4–3.1% of partial epilepsy patients report ictal religious experiences
- Right temporal lobe predominance
- Hyperreligiosity rare, not typical for TLE
- Historical retrospective diagnoses (Dostoevsky, Paul) are methodologically suspect

## Meditation & Default Mode Network
- Experienced meditators: reduced DMN activity across all meditation types (Brewer 2011, PNAS)
- Increased DMN-executive control connectivity
- State-to-trait transition: long-term meditators show differences even at rest
- Critical: secular and religious meditators show SAME signatures → practice-dependent, not belief-dependent

## God Helmet (Persinger) — Failed Experiment
- Claimed weak magnetic fields induce "sensed presence"
- Double-blind replication failed: effect attributable to suggestion, not magnetic fields
- Scientific status: generally regarded as failed in the field

## What Brain Imaging Cannot Show
- Correlation ≠ causation (neural correlate ≠ neural explanation)
- The hard problem remains (why does parietal deactivation feel like unity?)
- Direction problem (does belief cause activity or vice versa?)
- Meaning question outside neuroscience's methodological reach
