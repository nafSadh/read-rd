# S5 Notes — Developmental Psychology of Religion

## Intuitive Theism (Kelemen)
- Children are "promiscuous teleologists" — purpose-based explanations for everything
- Peaks around age 5; never fully disappears
- Design stance as cognitive default → creationism cognitively natural, evolution cognitively unnatural
- Educational implication: science education must work against deep cognitive default

## Children's God Concepts
- Cross-cultural: youngest children distinguish God from humans
- Attribute supernatural *psychological* properties (omniscience) before supernatural *physical* properties
- Parental God concepts shape children's theology, moderated by executive functioning (Saide 2022)
- Active construction, not passive absorption — children use ToM, teleology, dualism to build theology

## Born Believers (Barrett)
- Developmental trajectory: agency detection (0-2) → ToM (2-4) → teleology (3-5) → dualism (4-6) → God concept differentiation (5-7) → abstract reasoning (7+)
- Cognitive infrastructure for religion in place before formal teaching
- Cultural elaboration builds on pre-existing cognitive foundations

## Deconversion
- 74% of young adult exiters left before age 17; 26% before teen years
- Multi-stage process: pre-exit strain → turning point → anomie → reconstruction
- Reasons: irrelevance, science conflict, moral disagreement, institutional failure
- Personality changes precede AND follow deconversion (Bleidorn 2024): openness ↑ before, stability ↓ after (then recovers)
- Polish adolescents (2025): peer integration + rejection of religious constraints
- Psychological cost: simultaneous loss of meaning system, social network, family, routine
- Grief response mimics bereavement

## Gen Z
- 34% unaffiliated — least religious American generation
- Earlier exit age than previous generations
- "No religion" ≠ "no spirituality" — many retain transcendent beliefs without institutional affiliation
- The religious impulse may be detaching from organized religion, not disappearing
