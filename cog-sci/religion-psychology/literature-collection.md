## S4: Moral Psychology & Religion

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| R1 | [Haidt: Moral Psychology and the Misunderstanding of Religion (Edge)](https://www.edge.org/conversation/jonathan_haidt-moral-psychology-and-the-misunderstanding-of-religion) | Five moral foundations; religion binds and blinds; New Atheists miss binding function; loyalty/authority/purity as religious foundations | 2007 |
| R2 | [Kelly & Kramer: Religiosity Predicts Prosociality — Meta-Analysis (2024)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38407059/) | 701 effects, 237 samples, 811,663 participants: r=.13 overall; r=.15 self-report vs. r=.06 behavioral; self-enhancement bias | 2024 |
| R3 | [Shariff et al.: Religious Priming Meta-Analysis (PSPR)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25673322/) | 93 studies, 11,653 participants: robust priming effects; does not affect non-religious participants; cultural transmission required | 2016 |
| R4 | [Chhabra et al.: Prime and Punishment — Religious Priming (2025)](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/00846724241309923) | Religious priming did NOT significantly enhance prosocial behavior; Bayesian analysis supports absence of effect | 2025 |
| R5 | [Norenzayan & Shariff: Prosociality and Religion (ScienceDirect)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2352250X20301780) | Religious prosociality is parochial: favors ingroups; supernatural monitoring hypothesis; reputation-based cooperation | 2021 |
| R6 | [PMC: Religious Priming — Ingroup vs. Outgroup Prosocial Behavior](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5114877/) | God priming enhanced ingroup cooperation; religion priming enhanced both in/outgroup but weaker for outgroup | 2016 |
| R7 | [Illinois PramLab: Religious Prosociality Chapter](https://labs.psychology.illinois.edu/pramlab/Papers/Religious_Prosociality_Chapter.pdf) | Self-reports suggest universal prosociality; behavioral measures suggest parochial prosociality; discrepancy is key finding | 2015 |
| R8 | [Wikipedia: Jonathan Haidt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jonathan_Haidt) | Moral foundations theory; Moral Psychology Handbook; The Righteous Mind; religion as group-binding mechanism | Updated 2025 |

**Assessment:** ADEQUATE — The 2024 meta-analysis is the centerpiece; Haidt's framework, priming research (including null 2025 result), and parochial prosociality all covered.
