# Synthesis Notes — Atrophy & Decision

## Core Thesis

Most decisions are not made from open possibility space — they are dictated by atrophy. What capacities, connections, habits, and options have degraded determines what gets chosen more than active preference does.

## Cross-Domain Pattern

Every domain surveyed in this project shows the same structural pattern:

1. An initial state of relative openness (infant synaptic density, beginner deliberation, adolescent identity exploration, large social networks, intact motivation)
2. A process of selective degradation driven by use/non-use, environmental stress, or developmental commitment
3. A residual state that constitutes the effective decision space
4. Decision outcomes that track the residual state more than the preferences or deliberations occurring within it

This pattern holds at timescales from milliseconds (decision fatigue within a session) to decades (developmental foreclosure, expert automaticity).

## Level-by-Level Notes

### Neural Level
- Synaptic pruning is the most structurally radical form — physically eliminates circuits
- LTD is the ongoing molecular substrate — daily gradual weakening of unused connections
- Stress-induced dendritic atrophy in PFC and hippocampus means adversity directly degrades deliberative hardware
- Critical/sensitive periods for some capacities (language, visual acuity) make the foreclosure permanent

### Behavioral Level
- Dual-process directionality: the arrow points from deliberation → automaticity, not the reverse
- The habit/goal-directed distinction maps onto striatal anatomy (dorsomedial vs. dorsolateral)
- Expert chunking is the extreme form: 50k+ patterns in chess grandmasters, deliberation essentially eliminated
- The optimization paradox: the best decision-making we celebrate is achieved by atrophying deliberation

### Cognitive Level
- Decision fatigue is the most reversible form — restores after rest/food
- The replication crisis around ego depletion weakens the "resource" metaphor but not the behavioral phenomenon
- Dweck's moderator (belief about willpower finitude) is interesting but may reflect within-session vs. chronic depletion differences

### Developmental Level
- Erikson's moratorium concept explicitly acknowledges that commitment forecloses exploration
- Path dependency from economics is the cleanest formal model: switching costs increase with commitment
- Identity foreclosure status (Marcia) is the most studied case of premature atrophy of alternatives

### Social Level
- Dunbar's number establishes a cognitive ceiling that cannot be exceeded by effort or technology
- The maintained network (not the nominal one) is the decision-relevant network
- Social media finding: nominal network expands, actual maintained relationships follow same atrophy dynamics

### Motivational Level
- Maier & Seligman 2016 revision is crucial: passivity is the DEFAULT, controllability must be actively learned and maintained
- This means motivational capacity is not acquired once and kept — it requires continuous reinforcement
- The atrophy metaphor is most literal here: the vmPFC override circuit that enables instrumental behavior must be actively maintained

### Philosophical Level
- Frankfurt's compatibilism maps cleanly: atrophy degrades the second-order reflective capacity constitutive of freedom
- This operationalizes freedom as a gradient rather than binary
- Neuroscientific challenges (frontal lobe damage) illustrate extreme cases where the capacity distinction is visible

## Key Tensions Summary

1. Constraint vs. efficiency — same process, different scales of analysis
2. Helplessness vs. rational updating — criterion problem: does passivity generalize beyond its origin domain?
3. Determinism vs. agency — thesis is not deterministic nihilism; compatible with graded compatibilism
4. Reversibility — domain-specific; early maintenance >> late restoration for most forms
5. Individual vs. structural — not alternatives; structure operates through individual atrophy pathways
6. Optimization paradox — expert atrophy is domain-optimal but domain-locked; novelty challenges undermine

## Implications for Practice

The maintenance imperative is the core practical conclusion:
- Best time to maintain a capacity is before it begins to atrophy
- The habit of deliberation itself requires maintenance (meta-level)
- Structural interventions should target atrophy pathways specifically
- Moral responsibility attributions should consider the conditions that shaped atrophy profiles

## Open Questions

- Can we quantify the proportion of decision variance explained by capacity state vs. deliberative quality?
- What is the minimal maintenance required to prevent critical atrophy thresholds?
- Is there a general theory of atrophy reversibility across domains?
- How do developmental timing effects interact with structural inequality?
