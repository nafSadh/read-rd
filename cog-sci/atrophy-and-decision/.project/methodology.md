# Methodology — Atrophy & Decision

## Research Approach

This project uses the paper-drive methodology: systematic cross-domain literature review organized around a single integrative thesis, producing a survey paper, a tensions paper, and a position paper.

## Thesis

Most decisions are not made from open possibility space — they are dictated by atrophy. What capacities, connections, habits, and options have degraded determines what gets chosen more than active preference does.

## Domains Surveyed

1. **Neural / Synaptic** — synaptic pruning, LTP/LTD, use-it-or-lose-it principles, skill decay
2. **Behavioral** — dual-process theory, habit formation, automaticity, expert chunking
3. **Cognitive** — decision fatigue, ego depletion, default bias under depletion
4. **Developmental** — Erikson's stages, identity foreclosure, path dependency
5. **Social** — Dunbar's number, social network atrophy, social capital
6. **Motivational** — learned helplessness, Seligman's revision, motivational architecture
7. **Philosophical** — Frankfurt's compatibilism, agency as capacity, free will debates
8. **Economic/Structural** — path dependence, option narrowing, cognitive lock-in

## Source Standards

- Peer-reviewed where available (PMC, PNAS, Nature, ScienceDirect)
- Encyclopedic sources (Wikipedia, SEP, NCBI) for orienting overviews
- Seminal primary sources (Baumeister 1998, Seligman/Maier 2016, Frankfurt)
- No fabricated statistics — all figures from cited sources
- Contested claims (ego depletion replication) represented from both sides
