# Literature Collection — Atrophy & Decision

## Neural Substrate: Synaptic Pruning & Skill Decay

- **Wikipedia: Synaptic Pruning** — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synaptic_pruning — Orienting overview of mechanisms (microglial engulfment, complement cascade, C1q/C3/C4), developmental timeline, and disorders associated with pruning dysregulation.

- **PMC: How Synaptic Pruning Shapes Neural Wiring (2020)** — https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7368197/ — Core concept review of pruning mechanisms and their role in circuit formation.

- **Arain et al. (2013). Maturation of the adolescent brain. PMC** — https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3982854/ — Documents adolescent neurodevelopment including pruning, myelination, and their cognitive implications.

- **Nabulsi, L. et al. (2024). Cognitive Control and Neural Activity during Human Development. Journal of Neuroscience** — https://www.jneurosci.org/content/44/26/e0373242024 — Shows that adolescent pruning improves working memory and executive function by reducing noise in PFC circuits.

- **Duan, Z.J. et al. (2022). Pruning recurrent neural networks replicates adolescent changes. PNAS** — https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2121331119 — Computational model confirming that pruning-like reduction in RNNs reproduces adolescent cognitive trajectories.

- **Wikipedia: Long-term Depression** — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Long-term_depression — Overview of LTD as the molecular mechanism of synaptic weakening.

- **Bhagya, V. & Bhatt, S. (2011). LTP and LTD: A Clinical Perspective. PMC** — https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3118435/ — Reviews the bidirectional plasticity mechanisms and their clinical implications.

- **NCBI: Long-Term Synaptic Depression** — https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK10899/ — Chapter on LTD mechanisms in Neuroscience textbook.

- **Siegel, D. Pruning, Myelination, and the Remodeling Adolescent Brain** — https://drdansiegel.com/pruning-myelination-and-the-remodeling-adolescent-brain/ — Accessible overview of adolescent brain remodeling by developmental psychiatrist Daniel Siegel.

- **Cleveland Clinic: Synaptic Pruning** — https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/articles/synaptic-pruning — Clinical overview of synaptic pruning and its implications.

## Habit & Deliberation

- **Wikipedia: Dual Process Theory** — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dual_process_theory — Comprehensive overview of Type 1/Type 2 distinctions, historical development, and contemporary debates.

- **The Decision Lab: System 1 and System 2 Thinking** — https://thedecisionlab.com/reference-guide/philosophy/system-1-and-system-2-thinking — Applied summary of Kahneman's framework.

- **PMC: Dual Process Theory and Default Mode Network (2018)** — https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6056761/ — Links dual-process theory to neural correlates including DMN involvement in fast thinking.

- **Skycak, J. Cognitive Science of Learning: Developing Automaticity** — https://www.justinmath.com/cognitive-science-of-learning-developing-automaticity/ — Educational framework for automatization theory.

- **Psychology Fanatic: Automatization Theory** — https://psychologyfanatic.com/automatization-theory/ — Overview of automatization theory and its implications.

- **Perception & Action: How Do We Become an Expert?** — https://perceptionaction.com/8-2/ — Covers chunk-based expertise acquisition, the role of working memory limits, and skill acquisition stages.

- **Macnamara, A. et al. (2024). The role of deliberation in intuitive decision-making in sports. International Review of Sport & Exercise Psychology** — https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/1750984X.2024.2422847 — Reviews deliberation's role in sports expertise, showing deliberation atrophies as expertise develops.

- **Logan, G.D. Beyond Automaticity: The Psychological Complexity of Skill** — https://neuroscience.gsu.edu/files/2022/03/Beyond-Automaticity-The-Psychological-Complexity-of-Skill.pdf — Documents that skills never fully automate; a residual deliberative window persists.

- **ScienceDirect: Automaticity Overview** — https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/psychology/automaticity — Overview of automaticity research and its theoretical implications.

## Decision Fatigue & Ego Depletion

- **Wikipedia: Decision Fatigue** — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decision_fatigue — Overview of decision fatigue research, mechanisms, and real-world examples.

- **Baumeister, R.F. et al. (1998). Ego Depletion. Journal of Personality and Social Psychology** — https://faculty.washington.edu/jdb/345/345%20Articles/Baumeister%20et%20al.%20(1998).pdf — Original ego depletion paper documenting willpower as a limited resource.

- **Friese, M. et al. (2024). Self-control and limited willpower. Current Opinion in Psychology** — https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2352250X24000952 — Current status of ego depletion theory, including replication debates and theoretical revisions.

- **PubMed: Ego depletion current status** — https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39278166/ — PubMed entry for the 2024 review.

- **GCBS: The Depleted Mind** — https://gc-bs.org/articles/the-depleted-mind-the-science-of-decision-fatigue-and-ego-depletion/ — Applied summary including the parole board study.

- **InsideBE: Decision Fatigue** — https://insidebe.com/articles/decision-fatigue/ — Comprehensive applied overview including Dweck's moderator findings.

- **Welcome to the Jungle: Ego Depletion** — https://www.welcometothejungle.com/en/articles/ego-depletion-decision-fatigue — Workplace applications of decision fatigue research.

## Social Network Atrophy

- **Wikipedia: Dunbar's Number** — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dunbar%27s_number — Overview of the social brain hypothesis, network tier structure, and debates.

- **Dunbar, R.I.M. (2025). Why friendship and loneliness affect our health. Annals of the New York Academy of Sciences** — https://nyaspubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/nyas.15309 — 2025 review linking social network quality to health outcomes.

- **Dunbar, R.I.M. (2016). Do online social media cut through constraints that limit offline social networks? Royal Society Open Science** — https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/10.1098/rsos.150292 — Empirical test showing online networks do not expand maintained relationships.

- **Tandfonline: Social Brain Hypothesis — Thirty Years On (2024)** — https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/03014460.2024.2359920 — Dunbar's 2024 review of the social brain hypothesis 30 years after the original proposal.

- **Medium: Social Network Theory by Dunbar** — https://medium.com/illumination/social-network-theory-41bf88d2ee98 — Accessible summary of Dunbar's social network tier model.

## Learned Helplessness

- **Wikipedia: Learned Helplessness** — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Learned_helplessness — Comprehensive overview of Seligman's research, neuroscience, and applications.

- **Maier, S.F. & Seligman, M.E.P. (2016). Learned Helplessness at Fifty. PMC** — https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4920136/ — Major 2016 revision reframing helplessness as failure to acquire controllability, with neuroscientific basis.

- **Seligman, M.E.P. Learned Helplessness. University of Pennsylvania** — https://ppc.sas.upenn.edu/sites/default/files/learnedhelplessness.pdf — Original formulation of learned helplessness theory.

- **Simply Psychology: Learned Helplessness** — https://www.simplypsychology.org/learned-helplessness.html — Accessible overview of theory, experiments, and applications.

- **Positive Psychology: Learned Helplessness** — https://positivepsychology.com/learned-helplessness-seligman-theory-depression-cure/ — Coverage of clinical implications and interventions.

- **Tandfonline: Learned Helplessness Revisited (2022)** — https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/02699931.2022.2141002 — 2022 review proposing biased goal evaluation as a key mechanism.
