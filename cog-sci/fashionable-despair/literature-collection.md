| S1-05 | [Academia.edu — Romantic Melancholy, Keats and Byron](https://www.academia.edu/2542711/Romantic_Melancholy_John_Keats_and_Lord_Byron) | Philosophical distinction between melancholy and depression; Byron's ironic detachment; Pfau on melancholy as excess of knowledge exceeding representation | 2013 |

| S1-07 | [One Alkaline Life — When Melancholy Was an Art](https://www.onealkalinelife.com/post/when-melancholy-was-an-art-the-beauty-and-burden-of-romanticized-sadness) | Renaissance/Romantic revering of melancholy as depth and intellect; Aristotle's argument that great minds are touched by melancholy; modern parallel with social media self-branding of sadness | 2025 |

## S2: The Medicalization Arc

| ID | Source | Key Points | Date |
|----|--------|-----------|------|
| S2-01 | [Psychology Today — Prozac Culture: How Did It Happen?](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/our-new-discontents/202402/prozac-culture-how-did-it-happen) | "Prozac culture" as therapeutic ethos; 1950s therapeutic revolution; DSM-III 1980 introduced hundreds of new disorders; medicalization of everyday distress; 1 in 6 adult women on antidepressants | 2024 |

| S2-04 | [Frances — DSM, Medicalization of Mental Illness (Professional Counselor)](https://tpcjournal.nbcc.org/dsm-psychotherapy-counseling-and-the-medicalization-of-mental-illness-a-commentary-from-allen-frances/) | DSM-5 diagnostic inflation; normal grief → MDD, tantrums → DMDD; drug company disease-mongering; Hippocrates: more important to know the person than the disease | 2022 |

| S2-06 | [Rhetoric of Depression — Listening to Prozac in a Pandemic (UF Press)](https://journals.upress.ufl.edu/rhm/article/view/1487) | Kramer's *Listening to Prozac* introduced "cosmetic pharmacology"; diagnosis as rhetorical/speech act shifts problems from social conditions to personal pathology | 2023 |

## S6: Who Gets to Be Sad

| ID | Source | Key Points | Date |

| S6-05 | [PMC — Social Determinants of Depression: Race, Gender, SES](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5742759/) | High income protective for White women but becomes risk factor for African American men; effects of race, gender, SES on depression are multiplicative not additive | 2017 |

| S6-07 | [PMC — Depression at Intersection of Race, Sex, Sexual Orientation](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10153310/) | 42 intersectional groups; prevalence ranges 3.4–31.4% (past year); sexual minority people warrant particular attention; structural mechanisms drive inequities | 2023 |
