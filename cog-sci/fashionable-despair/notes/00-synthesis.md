# Cross-Section Synthesis — Fashionable Despair

*Built incrementally as sections are completed. Finalized after overview deep-dive.*

## The Seven Structural Features

1. **Ficino's Template** — Reframe → Fashion → Market → Backlash → Repeat. Running since 1489, accelerating from centuries to months.
2. **The Diagnostic Ratchet** — DSM categories only expand. Constituencies resist contraction. Cultural ratchet (TikTok) faster than institutional (DSM).
3. **The Recovery Paradox** — When suffering = identity, recovery = existential threat. Serialization problem intensifies on platforms.
4. **The Attention Inequality** — Visibility inversely correlated with need. 48% white vs 31% Black vs 22% Asian treatment rates.
5. **The Privatization Engine** — Structural suffering → individual pathology. Poverty → "burnout." The therapeutic frame points inward.
6. **The Undecidable** — Destigmatization and romanticization structurally identical from outside. All proposed tests fail.
7. **After Fashion** — Post-fashionable framework: reconnect diagnosis to context, redistribute attention, deprivatize suffering.

## Where Sources Disagree

- **Net effect:** Diana effect (doubled treatment-seeking) vs BetterHelp (30% less likely to try therapy). Non-canceling.
- **Diagnostic validity:** Frances/Horwitz/Wakefield (overdiagnosis) vs S6 epidemiological data (genuine disparities). Both true for different populations.
- **Backlash legitimacy:** Freya India (therapeutic overreach) vs counter-backlash ("privilege talking").

## What Surprised

- The ratchet ratchets the critics too (Frances on DSM-IV→DSM-5).
- The attention inequality is regressive — awareness economy concentrates resources where need is lowest.
- The privatization engine is a market outcome, not a conspiracy.
