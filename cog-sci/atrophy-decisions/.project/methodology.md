# Methodology — Atrophy & Decisions

## Research Question

To what extent does atrophy — of skills, habits, relationships, motivation, cognitive capacity, and social options — determine the decisions people make across the lifespan?

The motivating thesis: most decisions are not actively chosen from a field of open possibilities but are constrained (or dictated) by what has *not yet degraded*. We choose from what remains, not from what we want.

## Scope & Dimensions

This survey covers seven dimensions of atrophy as they bear on decision-making:

- **S1: Cognitive Atrophy and Decision Defaults** — habit formation as degradation of deliberation, decision fatigue, cognitive load constraints
- **S2: Skill and Capability Atrophy** — use-it-or-lose-it neuroscience, competence constraints on available choices
- **S3: Social Network Atrophy** — shrinking social circles with age (SST), relationship decay, how social options constrain decisions
- **S4: Motivational Atrophy** — learned helplessness, anhedonia, apathy, hedonic adaptation, why people stop wanting things
- **S5: Identity Atrophy** — progressive narrowing of possible selves, commitment and path dependency
- **S6: Biological Substrate** — neuroplasticity windows, synaptic pruning, what "use it or lose it" means at the cellular level
- **S7: Counterpoint** — resilience, plasticity, and deliberate renewal (where atrophy can be reversed or resisted)

*Deliberately excluded:* Pathological atrophy (Alzheimer's, stroke, TBI) is used as evidence but is not the primary focus. Economic/structural constraints on decision-making are acknowledged but not the main lens. The project is not a clinical review.

## Source Strategy

Sources were found through targeted web searches across all seven dimensions simultaneously. Emphasis placed on:
- Peer-reviewed research (PubMed, PMC, major journals)
- Foundational theoretical works (Seligman, Carstensen, Markus & Nurius, Kahneman, Baumeister)
- Recent empirical studies (2020–2025 where available)
- Systematic reviews and meta-analyses for credibility

52 sources total, minimum 7 per section. Sources are organized in literature-collection.md.

## Reading & Synthesis Approach

**Discourse survey** (not technical/statistical review): emphasis on what the field says, where sources converge and diverge, and what is contested. No primary statistical analysis.

Reading depth: summary-level for most sources; full-text extraction for foundational papers and key empirical results.
