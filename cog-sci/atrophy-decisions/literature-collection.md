# Literature Collection — Atrophy & Decisions

> To what extent does atrophy — of skills, habits, relationships, motivation, cognitive capacity, and social options — determine the decisions people make across the lifespan?

---

## S1: Cognitive Atrophy and Decision Defaults

| ID | Source | Key Data / Claims | Date |
|----|--------|-------------------|------|
| S1-01 | Trends in Cognitive Sciences — "Leveraging cognitive neuroscience for making and breaking real-world habits" (Wood & Rünger) | Dual-system model: stimulus–response (S–R) system vs. goal-directed system. Habit expression = S–R dominates. Imbalance leads to compulsive behavior. | 2024 |
| S1-02 | Lakshminarasimhan et al., bioRxiv — "A computational principle of habit formation" | Computational model showing how repeated action in consistent contexts progressively degrades deliberation and automates response. | 2024 |
| S1-03 | Baumeister & Schweitzer, *Internal Medicine Journal* — "Self-control, limited willpower and decision fatigue in healthcare settings" | Ego depletion framework: prolonged decision-making degrades self-regulation. New work extends to chronic ego depletion and burnout. | 2023 |
| S1-04 | Baumeister et al., *Current Opinion in Psychology* — "Self-control and limited willpower: Current status of ego depletion theory" | Reviews 25 years of ego depletion research; replicability now well-established with stronger manipulations; extends to initiative and planning. | 2024 |
| S1-05 | Sweller, J. — "Cognitive Load Theory" (various) | Working memory has ~20s duration, 4–7 chunk capacity; effortful deliberation is acutely resource-limited; defaults emerge under load. | 1988–2020 |
| S1-06 | *Decision Fatigue — A Conceptual Analysis*, PMC (Pignatiello et al.) | Reviewed literature on decision fatigue: decision quality degrades over time; shifting to default options is the primary coping mechanism. | 2018 |
| S1-07 | Kahneman, D. — *Thinking, Fast and Slow* | System 1 / System 2 framework; habit as degraded deliberation; most decisions default to System 1 heuristics. | 2011 |
| S1-08 | Frontiers in Behavioral Neuroscience — Review on habit formation (2025) | Striatum takes over from PFC in habit formation; automaticity reduces cognitive load but also reduces deliberative control. | 2025 |

## S2: Skill and Capability Atrophy

| ID | Source | Key Data / Claims | Date |
|----|--------|-------------------|------|
| S2-01 | Tanaka, J.N. et al., *Human Performance* — "Factors That Influence Skill Decay and Retention: A Quantitative Review" | Review of 50+ studies; decay on complex tasks smaller than simple tasks; initial training quality and practice frequency are key moderators. | 1998 |
| S2-02 | Arthur et al. — "Skill Decay: The Science and Practice of Mitigating Skill Loss and Enhancing Retention" | Motor skills degrade; procedural knowledge more resistant than declarative; refresher training restores competence. | 2020 |
| S2-03 | *Science Advances* — "Age and cognitive skills: Use it or lose it" | Skills decline at older ages ONLY for those with below-average skill usage; age-skill profiles differ significantly by usage. | 2025 |
| S2-04 | Frontiers in Aging Neuroscience — "Brain plasticity and motor practice in cognitive aging" | Exposure to challenging tasks associated with structural brain neuroplasticity; lack of task engagement linked to neurocognitive impairment. | 2014 |
| S2-05 | Cognitive Research: Principles and Implications — "Does using AI accelerate skill decay without performers' awareness?" | AI-induced skill decay operates below performer's awareness; disuse at level of cognitive engagement, not task engagement. | 2024 |
| S2-06 | SKYbrary Aviation Safety — "Skill Fade" | Aviation context: "use it or lose it" applies to most acquired skills; procedural skills degrade fastest without recurrent practice. | Ongoing |
| S2-07 | *ScienceDirect* — "Competence retention in safety-critical professions: A systematic literature review" | Medical, military, aviation: skills degrade; competence not maintained without regular practice; consequences documented. | 2020 |

## S4: Motivational Atrophy and Learned Helplessness

| ID | Source | Key Data / Claims | Date |
|----|--------|-------------------|------|
| S4-01 | Seligman, M.E.P. et al. — "Learned helplessness: Theory and evidence" | Classic paper: uncontrollable outcomes produce motivational, cognitive, and emotional deficits; three-deficit model. | 1976 |
| S4-02 | PMC — "Learned Helplessness at Fifty: Insights from Neuroscience" | 50-year review: neural underpinnings (DRN serotonin, vmPFC circuits); trauma activates helplessness circuits. | 2016 |

| S4-04 | NIA — "Background Paper: Motivation and Aging" | Comprehensive overview; intrinsic motivation changes with age; hedonic regulation shifts; dopamine system changes. | Recent |
| S4-05 | PLOS One — "Assessing the relationship between learned helplessness and anhedonia" | LH and anhedonia highly correlated; both involve deficit in reward anticipation and motivation. | 2021 |
| S4-06 | Loewenstein et al. — "Hedonic Adaptation" | Hedonic treadmill: people adapt to gains but not fully to losses; aspirations rise faster than attainment, reducing motivation. | 2003 |
| S4-07 | *Psychology Today* — "Learned Helplessness" (clinical overview) | Behavioral manifestations: passivity, failure to seek alternatives, acceptance of suboptimal conditions; decisions by non-action. | Ongoing |
