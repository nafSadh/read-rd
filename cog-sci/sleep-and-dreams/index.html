Sleep & Dreams: The Third of Life We Don’t Understand

A survey of sleep science and dream research — from circadian biology and neural oscillations through dream theories, lucid dreaming, deprivation, and clinical frontiers.

We spend roughly a third of our lives asleep, yet the fundamental question — why — remains surprisingly contested. This project surveys sleep and dream science across 6 dimensions and 55+ sources: the evolutionary logic of sleep, the sleeping brain’s architecture, competing theories of dreaming, the science of lucid dreaming, what deprivation reveals, and clinical frontiers from narcolepsy to parasomnias.

Key Tensions

  • 01 Function of sleep: Single core function (synaptic homeostasis? glymphatic clearance?) vs. sleep as a multi-purpose biological state
  • 02 Why we dream: Epiphenomenon of neural noise vs. adaptive function (threat rehearsal, memory consolidation, emotion regulation)
  • 03 Sleep need: Universal 8-hour recommendation vs. genuine individual variation in sleep requirements
  • 04 Lucid dreaming status: Legitimate cognitive science vs. fringe topic still seeking mainstream acceptance
  • 05 Insomnia treatment: CBT-I as clear first-line vs. real-world access barriers driving continued pharmacology dependence
