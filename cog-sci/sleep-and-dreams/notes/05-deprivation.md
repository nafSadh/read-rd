# S5: Sleep Deprivation & Modern Life — Section Notes

## Core Argument
Sleep deprivation is a public health crisis hiding in plain sight. The cognitive effects are severe and well-documented, comparable to alcohol intoxication. Modern life systematically undermines sleep through blue light, caffeine culture, shift work, and the always-on economy. The $585B sleep industry represents both a response to the crisis and its commercialization.

## Randy Gardner's Experiment (1963-1964)
- 17-year-old stayed awake for 11 days 24 minutes (264.4 hours)
- Day 2: Hallucinations began (street sign as person)
- Day 4-5: Delusional episodes, paranoia
- Day 11: Expressionless, slurred speech, attention span near zero, failed serial subtraction
- Recovery: Slept 14h40min first night, returned to normal sleep within weeks
- **Long-term**: Gardner reported lifelong insomnia problems afterward
- Guinness Book stopped tracking the record due to health risks

## Cognitive Effects
- After 24h awake: cognitive impairment equivalent to 0.1% blood alcohol (legally drunk)
- Reaction time, attention, working memory, decision-making all severely impaired
- Emotional regulation deteriorates — amygdala hyperactive, prefrontal control reduced
- "Microsleeps" — brief involuntary sleep episodes, especially dangerous while driving

## Sleep-Related Disasters
- **Exxon Valdez** (1989): Third mate had slept 6h in previous 48h before oil spill
- **Chernobyl** (1986): Operators working through early morning hours when errors occurred
- **Challenger** (1986): Key decision-makers had slept <2h the night before launch decision
- **Three Mile Island** (1979): Shift change during critical early morning hours

## Blue Light & Screens
- Blue light (460-480nm) most potent suppressor of melatonin
- 2-3 hours of evening screen exposure can delay melatonin onset by ~90 minutes
- 60-80% of students report poor sleep linked to evening blue light exposure
- >4 hours daily device use predicts worse sleep outcomes
- **Nuance**: Blue-light-blocking glasses show mixed results in meta-analyses
- Daytime blue light exposure actually improves alertness and mood

## Caffeine
- Half-life ~5 hours (range 1.5-9.5h depending on genetics, smoking, oral contraceptives)
- Blocks adenosine receptors (Process S accumulation signal)
- Afternoon coffee (2pm) = half the caffeine still circulating at 7pm, quarter at midnight
- Does not eliminate sleep debt — masks symptoms while debt accumulates
- Paradox: caffeine-fueled productivity → sleep deprivation → more caffeine needed

## Shift Work
- Night-shift workers 3x more likely to develop shift-related sleep disorder
- Insomnia prevalence: 29-38% in shift workers vs 6% in general population
- Increased risk: cardiovascular disease, metabolic syndrome, cancer, depression
- Circadian disruption classified as "probable carcinogen" by IARC
- 15-20% of workforce in industrialized nations works non-standard schedules

## Teenager Chronotype
- Puberty triggers biological shift to later chronotype (evening preference)
- Driven by changes in melatonin secretion timing and circadian period
- Biological clock shifts 1-3 hours later during adolescence
- School start times (7-8am) directly conflict with adolescent biology
- American Academy of Pediatrics recommends starting no earlier than 8:30am

## The Sleep Industry ($585B)
- Mattresses, supplements, apps, wearables, sleep coaching, weighted blankets
- Sleep tech devices market: $24.85B (2024), projected $134.6B by 2034
- **Concern**: Orthosomnia — anxiety about optimizing sleep scores can worsen sleep
- Commodification of a basic biological need
