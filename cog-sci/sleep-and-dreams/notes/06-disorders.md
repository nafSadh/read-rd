# S6: Sleep Disorders & Frontiers — Section Notes

## Core Argument
Sleep disorders range from the extremely common (insomnia, sleep apnea) to the extraordinarily rare (fatal familial insomnia). Understanding them illuminates normal sleep function — what breaks reveals what was working. The frontier of sleep medicine is moving from treating disorders to engineering sleep: targeted memory reactivation and dream modification.

## Insomnia
- Most common sleep disorder: affects 10-30% of adults chronically
- **CBT-I** (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia): First-line treatment, more effective long-term than medication
  - Components: sleep restriction, stimulus control, cognitive restructuring, relaxation, sleep hygiene
  - 70-80% of patients show improvement
  - Now available as internet-delivered (digital CBT-I)
  - Culturally tailored versions showing promise (e.g., for Black women)
- **Problem**: Most patients receive pills instead of CBT-I due to access, cost, provider shortage

## Sleep Apnea
- Obstructive sleep apnea (OSA): airway collapses during sleep, repeated breathing interruptions
- 936 million adults affected globally
- Nearly 2x risk of cardiovascular disease, stroke, all-cause mortality
- Found in 40-80% of patients with hypertension, heart failure, coronary artery disease
- CPAP is standard treatment; Genio hypoglossal nerve stimulator FDA approved 2025
- Many patients undiagnosed — up to 80% of moderate-to-severe cases

## Narcolepsy & Hypocretin
- Narcolepsy Type 1: caused by autoimmune destruction of hypocretin/orexin-producing neurons in hypothalamus
- Hypocretin discovered 1998 (de Lecea) / orexin (Sakurai) — same molecule, named twice
- Symptoms: excessive daytime sleepiness, cataplexy (sudden muscle weakness triggered by emotion), sleep paralysis, hypnagogic hallucinations
- Breakthrough: Oveporexton (oral orexin receptor 2-selective agonist) showing promise in phase 2 trials
- Narcolepsy as "window into REM regulation" — cataplexy is inappropriate REM atonia during waking

## Fatal Familial Insomnia (FFI)
- Caused by mutation in PRNP gene (prion protein, D178N with 129M)
- Autosomal dominant — 50% chance of passing to children
- Progressive: insomnia → autonomic dysfunction → cognitive decline → death
- Average duration: 18 months from onset to death
- Thalamus (especially mediodorsal and anterior nuclei) degenerates
- Sporadic form exists without family history (sFI)
- Currently incurable — proves that sleep is not merely "nice to have" but necessary for life

## Parasomnias
- **Hybrid states**: mix of wake and sleep features
- Sleepwalking (NREM parasomnia): occurs during deep slow-wave sleep, no dream recall
- REM Behavior Disorder: failure of REM atonia, acting out dreams, often precursor to Parkinson's/Lewy Body
- Sleep eating, sleep driving, sleep texting — all documented
- Dream-reality confusion: emerging recognition as distinct phenomenon

## Targeted Memory Reactivation (TMR)
- Present sensory cues (sounds, odors) during sleep that were associated with learning during waking
- Cues reactivate associated memories, strengthening consolidation
- Rose scent during study + rose scent during deep sleep → better recall
- Moving from lab curiosity to practical application

## Dream Engineering
- Emerging field: deliberately influencing dream content
- Dormio device (MIT Media Lab): delivers audio cues during hypnagogia to guide dream themes
- Potential applications: creativity enhancement, nightmare reduction, skill rehearsal
- Ethical questions: Is it appropriate to engineer the last private space of consciousness?

## Open Questions
- Can TMR be scaled beyond laboratory settings?
- Will orexin receptor agonists change narcolepsy treatment paradigm?
- Is REM Behavior Disorder always a neurodegenerative prodrome, or can it be benign?
- Can dream engineering be therapeutic without disrupting sleep's natural functions?
