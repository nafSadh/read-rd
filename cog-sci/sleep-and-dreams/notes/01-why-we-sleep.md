# S1: Why We Sleep — Section Notes

## Core Argument
Sleep is universal, essential, and evolutionarily conserved — yet explaining why it exists remains one of biology's hardest problems. The two-process model (homeostatic + circadian) describes how sleep is regulated, but not why it evolved.

## Key Facts
- Sleep architecture: NREM stages 1-3 + REM, cycling every ~90 minutes
- NREM dominates first half of night (deep restoration), REM dominates second half (dreaming, integration)
- Process S: homeostatic sleep pressure (adenosine accumulation during waking)
- Process C: circadian rhythm (~24.2h internal clock, entrained by light)
- Every animal with a nervous system sleeps — even jellyfish show sleep-like states
- Sleep duration varies enormously: elephants <2h/day, bats/koalas ~20h/day

## The Evolutionary Puzzle
Sleep makes animals unconscious, immobile, and vulnerable to predation. It prevents foraging, mating, and offspring protection. Any behavior this costly must provide compensating benefits. Multiple theories:

1. **Energy conservation**: reduced metabolic rate saves calories
2. **Predation avoidance**: staying still in dangerous periods reduces detection
3. **Adaptive inactivity**: forced rest prevents maladaptive behavior at suboptimal times
4. **Restorative function**: tissue repair, immune function, waste clearance
5. **Cognitive function**: memory consolidation, synaptic homeostasis

No single theory explains all the data. Most researchers now accept sleep serves multiple functions.

## Walker's Framework
- Wake = reception (taking in information)
- NREM = reflection (consolidating and strengthening)
- REM = integration (connecting new learning with existing knowledge)

Note: Walker's popularization has been criticized (Guzey) for factual errors and overstatement. The framework is useful but should be held lightly.

## Open Questions
- Why do sleep needs change across lifespan? (Infants 16h, adults 7-9h, elderly 6-7h)
- Why does sleep deprivation kill (in rats) while voluntary sleep restriction seems tolerated?
- Is there a "core" function, or is sleep irreducibly multi-functional?
