# Methodology — Sleep & Dreams: A Survey of Sleep Science and Dream Research

## Research Question
What does modern science tell us about why we sleep, what happens in the sleeping brain, why we dream, and what goes wrong when sleep fails? This project surveys sleep science across neuroscience, psychology, evolutionary biology, and clinical medicine — treating sleep not as a single topic but as a convergence zone where some of the hardest questions in biology and consciousness meet.

## Scope & Dimensions
This survey covers 6 axes:
1. **Why We Sleep** — Sleep architecture, the two-process model, evolutionary puzzle
2. **The Sleeping Brain** — Memory consolidation, glymphatic system, synaptic homeostasis
3. **Dream Theories** — From Freud to predictive processing, the hard problem of dream consciousness
4. **Lucid Dreaming** — LaBerge's research, neuroscience of the hybrid state, therapeutic applications
5. **Sleep Deprivation & Modern Life** — Cognitive effects, blue light, shift work, the sleep industry
6. **Sleep Disorders & Frontiers** — Insomnia, apnea, narcolepsy, FFI, targeted memory reactivation

Deliberately excluded: pharmacology of specific sleep medications (beyond mentioning CBT-I as first-line), pediatric sleep disorders, sleep in non-human animals (except as evolutionary evidence), detailed polysomnography methodology.

## Source Strategy
- Web search for academic reviews, landmark experiments, and science journalism
- Priority: foundational texts (Walker, Hobson, LaBerge, Tononi & Cirelli), key discoveries (Nedergaard glymphatic, hypocretin/orexin), meta-analyses (CBT-I, lucid dreaming therapy)
- Historical experiments cited through secondary sources (Randy Gardner, Rechtschaffen rat studies)
- Pop science sources (Why We Sleep) cited with known criticisms noted (Guzey's critique of Walker)

## Reading & Synthesis Approach
Discourse survey: what are the established findings, where is the science still contested, and where does popular understanding diverge from the evidence?
