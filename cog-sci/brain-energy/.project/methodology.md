# Methodology — Brain Energy & Metabolic Psychiatry

## Research Question
What is the evidence for the metabolic theory of mental illness? This project surveys Palmer's framework, the mitochondrial dysfunction evidence, neuroinflammation pathways, ketogenic and metabolic interventions, and the clinical trial landscape -- treating metabolic psychiatry as an *emerging field* with both promising evidence and significant limitations.

## Scope & Dimensions
This survey covers 6 axes:
1. **The metabolic theory** -- Palmer's framework, paradigm shift from neurotransmitter to metabolic models
2. **Mitochondrial dysfunction** -- ATP production, electron transport chain, oxidative stress, brain energy demand
3. **Psychiatric applications** -- Depression, bipolar, schizophrenia, anxiety through the metabolic lens
4. **Neuroinflammation & gut-brain axis** -- NLRP3 inflammasome, microglia, cytokines, microbiome
5. **Interventions** -- Ketogenic diet, fasting, exercise, supplementation, BHB signaling
6. **Clinical evidence & future directions** -- RCTs, critiques, limitations, field trajectory

Deliberately excluded: epilepsy treatment (well-established, not novel), weight loss aspects of keto, detailed pharmacology of antipsychotics, non-psychiatric neurological applications.

## Source Strategy
- Web search for academic reviews, meta-analyses (PMC, PubMed), and clinical trial reports
- Priority: RCTs (JAMA Psychiatry 2025 TRD trial, Stanford pilot), systematic reviews, named researchers (Palmer, Sethi, Phelps)
- Critical perspectives included as first-class evidence, not afterthoughts
- Mechanistic studies (BHB/HDAC, NLRP3, mitochondrial complex I/IV) for biological plausibility
- Industry and institutional sources (Metabolic Mind, Harvard Brain Science Initiative) for field trajectory

## Reading & Synthesis Approach
Discourse survey: what claims does the metabolic theory make, what evidence supports them, where is the evidence strong vs. preliminary, and what are the legitimate criticisms?
