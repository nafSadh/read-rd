## Strengths

1. **The tensions paper is a genuine synthesis deliverable.** It goes beyond survey to produce original analytical structure. The "why both sides are right" format forces honest assessment of competing evidence. The conclusion distills the entire project into four words per dimension: biologically plausible, clinically preliminary, institutionally legitimate, commercially premature. This is the project's highest-quality output.

2. **Critical balance remains exemplary.** The project does not advocate for metabolic psychiatry. Verdict grids (Supported vs. Unproven) in the deep-dives, explicit critiques in S1 and S6, and the tensions paper's even-handed structure all enforce intellectual honesty. The inclusion of adherence challenges, equity concerns, and hype cycle risks alongside genuine scientific promise is well-calibrated.

3. **Source breadth and diversity.** 50 sources spanning meta-analyses, RCTs, landmark mechanistic studies, clinical commentary, institutional perspectives, and critical reviews. The tensions paper adds direct URLs for 25 references, improving verifiability for the synthesis layer.

4. **Overview now exists.** The highest-priority recommendation from round 1 (create or restore overview.dd.html) has been addressed. The index page no longer has a broken link.

5. **Template execution is consistent and polished.** All 6 deep-dives use the same three-panel interactive template. The tensions paper uses a different but equally polished template with sidebar navigation and hover footnotes. Both templates support light/dark themes.

## Weaknesses

1. **Project metadata is stale.** The todo.md shows S1-S6, index, and synthesis notes as unchecked despite all being complete. The methodology.md deliverables list is similarly outdated. The changelog has no entry for the tensions paper. This makes the project metadata unreliable as a record of what was done and when.

2. **Markdown notes remain thin.** Per-section notes (01-06) are still telegraphic bullet lists (e.g., S2 is 8 lines). The paper-drive methodology prescribes these as analytical working documents with cross-source synthesis. This weakness is unchanged from round 1.

3. **Incomplete citations persist.** The main literature-collection.md still has approximately 15 sources with vague attributions. While the tensions paper provides URLs for its references, the foundational citation document remains incomplete.

4. **S6 still lacks SVG diagrams.** The clinical evidence section is the only deep-dive without visual diagrams. An evidence hierarchy visualization or trial landscape timeline would strengthen this critical section.

5. **S3 still has 7 sections.** All other deep-dives have 8. The missing section creates a minor structural inconsistency.

6. **No cross-links between deep-dives.** The dd.html files are self-contained. Cross-references (e.g., S2 linking to S4 for the ROS-inflammation bridge, S5 linking to S2 for BHB mechanisms) would improve navigation.

7. **No slide deck.** The paper-drive workflow includes a presentation deliverable. The material is well-organized enough to support one.

8. **Unlinked audio file.** `overview.mp3` exists in the project directory but is not referenced from any HTML page. Its purpose and content are unclear.

9. **Font/template inconsistency between deep-dives and tensions paper.** The deep-dives use EB Garamond + Jost; the tensions paper uses Crimson Pro + Source Sans 3. This is a minor visual cohesion issue.

## Recommendations

### High Priority
1. **Update project metadata.** Check off completed items in todo.md and methodology.md. Add a changelog entry for the tensions paper and overview restoration. Accurate metadata is essential for project integrity.

2. **Complete citation data.** Add full author lists, titles, and DOIs/URLs for the ~15 sources in literature-collection.md that currently use vague publisher attributions. The tensions paper demonstrates this is feasible -- it provides URLs for all 25 of its references.

### Medium Priority
3. **Expand markdown notes.** Rewrite notes 01-06 to include cross-source synthesis, disagreements between sources, and analytical observations. These should be genuine working documents supporting the deep-dives, not just extraction shorthand.

4. **Add SVG diagrams to S6.** An evidence hierarchy pyramid or trial landscape timeline would strengthen the most critical section.

5. **Link the audio file.** Either add `overview.mp3` to the index page or overview.dd.html as a playable audio element, or remove it if it is not a project deliverable.

6. **Add cross-links between deep-dives.** The deep-dives reference overlapping concepts (ROS in S2 and S4, BHB in S2 and S5, insulin resistance in S3 and S5) but do not link to each other.

### Low Priority
7. **Normalize S3 to 8 sections.** Add a dedicated section or restructure to match the 8-section pattern.

8. **Build a slide deck.** The tensions paper provides a natural presentation structure: 4 tensions, each with evidence on both sides and resolution conditions.

9. **Harmonize typography.** Consider aligning the tensions paper's font stack with the deep-dives for visual cohesion across the project.
