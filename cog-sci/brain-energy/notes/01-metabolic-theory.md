# S1: The Metabolic Theory of Mental Illness

## Core Argument
Palmer proposes mental disorders are fundamentally metabolic disorders of the brain. Mitochondrial dysfunction reduces ATP, impairing neurotransmitter synthesis, ion channel function, synaptic plasticity. Reframes psychiatric symptoms as downstream of energy failure.

## Key Claims
1. Mental illness = metabolic dysfunction at cellular level
2. Mitochondria regulate neurotransmitters, hormones, gene expression, inflammation
3. Standard neurotransmitter model is incomplete -- describes symptoms not causes
4. Metabolic interventions can address root causes
5. Not anti-medication -- additional treatment dimension

## Palmer: Harvard/McLean, published "Brain Energy" 2022, treating with keto since ~2016

## Critiques
- Theory may be too broad/unfalsifiable
- Researcher-advocate overlap creates bias risk
- Most evidence from small open-label studies
- Popular discourse oversimplifies
