# Synthesis Notes — Brain Energy & Metabolic Psychiatry

## Central Thesis
Palmer's metabolic theory proposes psychiatric conditions emerge from cellular energy dysfunction. Mitochondrial impairment reduces ATP, triggering neurotransmitter deficits, neuroinflammation, and cognitive-emotional dysregulation.

## Key Tensions
1. **Paradigm shift vs. incremental addition** -- metabolic factors underappreciated but don't invalidate existing models
2. **Mechanistic strength vs. clinical evidence gaps** -- strong biology (Complex I/IV meta-analyses, BHB/HDAC), weak clinical evidence (one RCT, small pilots)
3. **Diet as medicine vs. diet as distraction** -- promising but adherence-challenged, not replacement for medication
4. **Emerging field vs. hype cycle** -- 75+ trials worldwide, Nature publication, but also supplement industry co-option

## Cross-Section Findings
1. Mitochondria as convergence point across disorders (meta-analyses)
2. Inflammation bridges metabolism and mood (NLRP3 pathway)
3. Insulin resistance precedes medication (drug-naive first-episode evidence)
4. BHB is more than fuel (HDAC inhibitor, NLRP3 inhibitor, epigenetic modifier)
5. Exercise works through same metabolic pathway
6. Gut-brain axis adds another entry point

## Evidence Hierarchy
- **Strong**: Mitochondrial deficits (meta-analyses), BHB/HDAC (Science 2013), neuroinflammation in depression
- **Moderate**: Ketogenic pilot trials, exercise-mitochondria pathway, gut-brain axis
- **Emerging**: JAMA Psychiatry RCT (2025), transdiagnostic metabolic approach
- **Preliminary**: Optimal protocols, responder prediction, long-term outcomes
