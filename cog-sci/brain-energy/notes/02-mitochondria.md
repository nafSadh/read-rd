# S2: Mitochondrial Dysfunction

## Core: Brain = 2% weight, 20% energy. Complex I/IV deficits across MDD, bipolar, SCZ (Hjelm 2018 meta, 125 studies).
## Pathways: Glycolysis (2 ATP) -> Citric Acid Cycle -> OXPHOS (26-28 ATP) = ~30-32 total
## Ketones: BHB as HDAC inhibitor (Science 2013), NLRP3 inhibitor, epigenetic modifier
## ROS cycle: Damaged mito -> more ROS -> more damage. Ketones produce fewer ROS.
## Acquired vs Genetic: Acquired (lifestyle) potentially reversible. 54% MDD in genetic mito disorders.
