# Methodology — Stoicism in Practice: Cognitive Science, Evidence, and Misuse

## Research Question
How does Stoicism fare when examined through the lens of cognitive science? What empirical evidence supports (or undermines) specific Stoic practices? Where does authentic, evidence-based Stoicism end and pop-cultural distortion begin?

This project treats modern Stoicism as a *living practice under scientific scrutiny* — neither dismissing it as self-help fad nor accepting it uncritically. The goal is to map the territory between ancient philosophy, clinical psychology, neuroscience, and social media culture.

## Scope & Dimensions
This survey covers 6 axes:
1. **The Science of Stoic Practice** — Empirical research on specific exercises (morning/evening reflection, negative visualization, view from above). Stoic Week data from Modern Stoicism org. SABS validation. What cognitive science says about why these work.
2. **Cognitive Mechanisms** — How Stoic practices map to cognitive reappraisal, emotion regulation, executive function. fMRI studies on reappraisal. Prefrontal-amygdala circuits. Gross & John's suppression vs. reappraisal distinction.
3. **Proper Use: Evidence-Based Stoicism** — What actually works according to research. CBT lineage (Ellis REBT -> Beck CT -> modern CBT). ACT parallels. Stoic journaling efficacy. Robertson's integration work.
4. **Misuse & Toxic Stoicism** — Emotional avoidance disguised as Stoic practice. "Broicism" and manosphere appropriation. Bypassing grief/trauma. When the dichotomy of control becomes victim-blaming. Reddit/social media Stoicism vs. the real thing.
5. **The Pop-Psychology Pipeline** — How Ryan Holiday's books/Daily Stoic simplified Stoicism. What gets lost in translation (physics, logic, cosmopolitan ethics). The Instagram-ification of ancient philosophy. Comparison to how mindfulness was commercialized (McMindfulness).
6. **Integration & Clinical Applications** — Donald Robertson's Stoicism-CBT bridge. Stoic-informed approaches to anxiety, anger, grief. Military resilience programs (Stockdale, CSF/MRT). Empirical Stoicism's future trajectory.

Deliberately excluded: detailed exposition of ancient Stoic metaphysics/physics (covered only as "what gets lost"), comparative religion (Stoicism vs Buddhism etc. as full topic), Stoic political philosophy, detailed history of the Stoa.

## Source Strategy
- Web search for academic reviews, empirical studies (PMC, PubMed, PsycNET), validated scales (SABS), and cultural criticism
- Priority: peer-reviewed research (Buhle et al. 2014 reappraisal meta-analysis, Gross & John 2003, LeBon et al. 2025 SABS), founding clinical texts (Ellis, Beck), and named practitioners (Robertson, Pigliucci)
- Pop sources (Daily Stoic, TikTok content, Holiday's books) cited as objects of analysis, not as evidence
- Modern Stoicism org data (Stoic Week reports) as primary empirical source for Stoic practice outcomes
- Military sources (Stockdale, CSF program) for real-world resilience applications

## Reading & Synthesis Approach
Discourse survey with empirical emphasis: what does the evidence actually say, where do practitioners and critics agree, where do they contradict? Special attention to the gap between what Stoicism *is* (philosophically) and what it *becomes* (culturally).
