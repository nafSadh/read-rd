# Notes — S2: Cognitive Mechanisms

## Core Framework: Reappraisal vs. Suppression
- Gross & John (2003): Two fundamentally different emotion regulation strategies
- **Cognitive reappraisal**: Changing how you think about stimulus BEFORE emotional response. What Stoics actually taught.
- **Expressive suppression**: Hiding emotional expression AFTER it's formed. What pop "stoicism" teaches.
- Reappraisers: more positive emotion, less negative, better relationships, greater well-being
- Suppressors: less positive emotion, MORE negative emotion, feelings of inauthenticity, worse social outcomes

## Neuroscience of Reappraisal
- Buhle et al. (2014) meta-analysis: 48 fMRI studies
  - Reappraisal consistently activates: dlPFC, vlPFC, dmPFC (cognitive control regions)
  - Reappraisal attenuates: bilateral amygdala
  - Does NOT activate vmPFC (separating it from extinction/habituation)
- Amygdala-PFC connectivity:
  - Convergent amygdala-vlPFC connectivity during reappraisal
  - Strength of amygdala-OFC/dmPFC coupling predicts success of emotion regulation
- TMS-fMRI causal evidence (2023):
  - Stimulating vlPFC enhances reappraisal performance
  - Increases vlPFC + vmPFC activity, decreases amygdala + insula
  - First causal (not just correlational) evidence for PFC role

## The Stoic Practice → Neural Mechanism Map
| Stoic Practice | Psychological Mechanism | Neural Circuit |
|---------------|----------------------|---------------|
| "It's not things that disturb us but our judgments" | Cognitive reappraisal | dlPFC/vlPFC → amygdala attenuation |
| Premeditatio malorum | Predictive processing, expectation adjustment | dmPFC, anterior insula |
| View from above | Psychological distancing, decentering | dmPFC, TPJ |
| Dichotomy of control | Attentional deployment, acceptance | ACC, vlPFC |
| Morning intention-setting | Goal-directed regulation, executive control | dlPFC, ACC |
| Evening reflection | Self-monitoring, error correction | dmPFC, ACC |

## Key Distinction from Pop Stoicism
- When someone says "just don't feel it" → suppression pathway → amygdala STILL fires, no PFC modulation
- When Epictetus says "examine your impressions" → reappraisal pathway → PFC actively modulates amygdala
- The cognitive WORK is the difference. You can't skip it.
