# Notes — S4: Misuse & Toxic Stoicism

## The Broicism Problem
- "Broicism" = bastardization of Stoicism cherry-picked for manosphere content
- Promoted by figures like Andrew Tate, "Sigma Male" accounts, RedPill communities
- Takes: emotional suppression, endurance, dominance. Leaves: virtue, compassion, cosmopolitanism, social duty
- TikTok: 257K videos, 2.5B views on #Stoicism — most lack precision, validity, reliability
- Professor of Greek philosophy: TikTok promotes "disinformation about stoicism"

## Emotional Suppression Disguised as Philosophy
- Key Gross & John finding: suppressors feel LESS positive emotion and MORE negative emotion
- "Man up" culture uses Stoic language to enforce suppression, not reappraisal
- PMC 2025 study: traditional masculinity norms (emotional stoicism, self-reliance, toughness) → risky coping behaviors, reduced help-seeking
- Teens conflate Stoicism with toxic positivity — "just don't let it affect you"
- Research: suppression linked to higher stress, anxiety, depression

## When Dichotomy of Control Becomes Victim-Blaming
- Proper use: Focus energy where you have agency (your response, your effort, your values)
- Misuse: "You can't control systemic racism/poverty/abuse, so stop complaining"
- Critics: too individualistic, ignores how social systems shape lives
- Tremblay (Modern Stoicism): addresses common misunderstandings
- Stoic response: Marcus Aurelius stressed social duty; dichotomy complements advocacy, doesn't replace it

## Bypassing Grief and Trauma
- Pop interpretation: "A Stoic doesn't grieve" — fundamentally wrong
- Seneca: grief "is wholly natural" for a period; wrote consolation letters
- Chrysippus: wrote texts of philosophical consolation
- Solomon & Nussbaum criticism: Stoicism treats emotions as pathos (pathological), should be minimized
- Real danger: using Stoic language to avoid processing genuine loss → complicated grief
