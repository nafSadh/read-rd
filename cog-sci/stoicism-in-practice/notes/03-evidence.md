# Notes — S3: Proper Use — Evidence-Based Stoicism

## The CBT Lineage
- **Epictetus** (1st century CE): "It is not things that disturb us, but our judgments about things"
- **Albert Ellis** (1956): REBT — "emotional problems are not caused by external events, but by our irrational beliefs about such events." Explicitly credited Stoics.
- **Aaron Beck** (1960s): Cognitive Therapy — quoted Marcus Aurelius in treatment manual. "The philosophical origins of cognitive therapy can be traced back to the Stoic philosophers."
- **Modern CBT**: Third-generation includes ACT, which integrates Stoic dichotomy of control + Buddhist acceptance

## ACT Parallels
- ACT "combines core insights from Buddhism, Stoicism, and Daoism"
- From Stoicism: dichotomy of control, values-based living
- Key difference: ACT says we CANNOT constantly control thoughts/emotions (vs. Stoic emphasis on cognitive mastery)
- Psychological flexibility (ACT) ≈ Stoic virtue of wisdom (adapting to what is)
- Values clarification (ACT) ≈ Stoic virtue ethics (living according to nature/reason)

## Robertson's Bridge Work
- "The Philosophy of Cognitive-Behavioural Therapy" (2010, 2nd ed. 2019) — book-length Stoicism-CBT mapping
- "Build Your Resilience" (2012) — Stoic resilience for general audience
- SMRT (Stoic Mindfulness and Resilience Training) — structured online program
- Key argument: Stoicism is "bigger and deeper than CBT" because it provides a philosophy of life, not just coping techniques
- This may explain why benefits could be more lasting — identity integration vs. technique application

## Journaling Efficacy
- Marcus Aurelius's Meditations = literally a Stoic journal
- Stoic journaling combines: morning intention-setting + evening self-examination
- Meta-analysis evidence: 68% of journaling interventions show effective outcomes
- Gratitude component: 6.86% higher life satisfaction, 7.76% lower anxiety
- Brief gratitude writing: significant stress reduction in healthcare workers

## What "Evidence-Based" Means Here
- Most evidence is for COMPONENTS of Stoic practice (reappraisal, gratitude, journaling) not for the WHOLE PACKAGE
- No RCT has tested "practice Stoicism for 12 weeks" vs. "practice CBT for 12 weeks"
- SABS validation is the first step toward this — now we can measure Stoic attitudes, we can correlate them with outcomes
- The evidence supports the mechanisms, but the specific Stoic packaging is understudied
