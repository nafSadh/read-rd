# Methodology — The Science of Humor

## Research Question
Why do things strike us as funny? This project surveys humor from every angle that has something credible to say about it — philosophy, neuroscience, evolutionary psychology, comedy craft, cultural anthropology, clinical research, and computational approaches. The goal is not to find one grand unified theory of funny (spoiler: there isn't one) but to map the territory well enough to see where the different explanations overlap, contradict, and complement each other.

## Scope & Dimensions
This survey covers 7 axes:
1. **Theories of Humor** — Incongruity (Kant, Schopenhauer), superiority (Hobbes, Plato), relief (Freud, Spencer), benign violation (McGraw & Warren), and why no single theory covers all cases
2. **The Neuroscience of Laughter** — fMRI studies, reward circuitry, gelotology, Duchenne vs non-Duchenne laughter, mirror neurons, contagious laughter
3. **Comedy Structure & Craft** — Rule of three, callbacks, timing, setup-punch mechanics, misdirection, writing rooms, the tight five, genre differences
4. **Dark Humor & Taboo** — Tendentious jokes, gallows humor as coping, moral violation theory, the Overton window, "too soon" dynamics
5. **Cultural Comedy** — Cross-cultural humor translation, British vs American vs Japanese vs Indian comedy, linguistic humor, memes as collective humor
6. **Humor, Health & Social Bonding** — Laughter yoga, health evidence, Dunbar's social grooming theory, humor in relationships
7. **AI & Computational Humor** — Machine humor generation, why humor is AI-hard, computational creativity, the Turing test of comedy

Deliberately excluded: humor therapy certification programs, children's humor development (except where relevant to theory), humor in advertising/marketing (except as examples), joke databases/taxonomies.

## Source Strategy
- Academic sources: psychology of humor journals, neuroscience reviews, meta-analyses
- Philosophy: primary texts and scholarly analysis of incongruity/superiority/relief theories
- Comedy craft: practitioner interviews, craft books, industry analysis
- Cultural studies: cross-cultural psychology, anthropological humor research
- Clinical: systematic reviews of humor and health outcomes
- AI/computational: NLP/computational creativity papers, LLM evaluation studies
- Target: 40-60 sources across all dimensions

## Reading & Synthesis Approach
Interdisciplinary survey: what does each field contribute to understanding humor, where do fields agree, and where does one field's findings challenge another's assumptions?
