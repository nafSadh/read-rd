02

The Prediction Problem

LLMs predict the most likely next token, but comedy requires the unlikely token that still makes sense[2]“ChatGPT is fun, but it is not funny!” (ArXiv, 2023) →. The punchline of a joke is, by definition, not what you expected. A well-calibrated language model has been optimized to produce exactly what you expected. These objectives are in direct tension.

The Fundamental Paradox

Making a model more accurate at prediction makes it less capable of genuine surprise — and surprise is the engine of comedy. Comedy is what happens when you predict correctly and then swerve.

Pre-LLM Computational Humor

Before LLMs, computational humor focused on narrow rule-based systems: JAPE (automatic pun generation), HAHAcronym (humorous acronym expansion), and similar approaches. These systems could produce passable wordplay but nothing broader. Puns are the one type of humor that is partially computable — requiring fewer simultaneous cognitive skills than multi-layered humor forms.

05

When AI Is Accidentally Funny

The most genuinely funny AI outputs tend to be unintentional. An image generator producing a dog with six legs. A chatbot confidently explaining something absurd. The humor comes from the incongruity between the machine’s confident delivery and its output’s absurdity — exactly the kind of violated expectation that humor theories describe.

AI as Foil

We laugh AT AI, not WITH it. The machine is the unwitting straight man in a comedy it doesn’t know it’s performing.

AI as Comedian

Intentional humor generation. Currently formulaic, predictable, lacking genuine surprise. The gap between foil and comedian reflects what current AI systems still lack in social and cognitive integration.

06

The Comedy Turing Test

Humor may be the truest benchmark for machine intelligence — not because it is the hardest task, but because it requires the broadest competence: understanding people, culture, expectations, and surprise simultaneously[5]Thomas Smith: Why Humor Is the Perfect Benchmark for GenAI (2023) →. No other single benchmark tests all four.

The Argument

If an AI can be genuinely, consistently, originally funny — not template-based puns, but humor that surprises even its creators — it has probably achieved something close to general intelligence.

07

Humor as Diagnostic

The failure of AI humor is not a curiosity — it is diagnostic[6]AI Humor Generation: Cognitive, Social and Creative Skills (2025) →. It tells us precisely what is missing from current AI: the ability to hold multiple frames simultaneously and choose the surprising one that still makes sense. A machine that can be genuinely funny has solved theory of mind, cultural understanding, creative subversion, and self-evaluation in one stroke.

The Key Insight

Until machines can be funny, the funniest thing about AI humor is that it is not funny. And that observation itself — the meta-humor of artificial intelligence failing at humor — may be the most revealing comedy of our era.
