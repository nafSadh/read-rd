# The Science of Humor — Literature Collection

> An interdisciplinary survey of why things are funny, from philosophy to fMRI to stand-up craft.

**Compiled:** March 25, 2026
**Scope:** Humor theory, neuroscience of laughter, comedy craft, dark humor, cultural comedy, humor & health, computational humor
**Method:** Web research across academic journals, philosophy texts, comedy practitioner resources, neuroscience reviews, cross-cultural studies, clinical meta-analyses, and AI/NLP research

## 1. Theories of Humor

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| TH-01 | **Theories of humor — Wikipedia** | Overview of incongruity, superiority, and relief theories. Kant: "the sudden transformation of a strained expectation into nothing." Schopenhauer: incongruity between concept and percept. Hobbes: "sudden glory." Plato: self-ignorance as comic. | Ongoing |
| TH-02 | **Humor — Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy** (Morreall) | Comprehensive philosophical treatment. Three traditional theories + modern developments. The "ha-ha"/"a-ha" connection to insight. Play theory of humor. Freedom from practical concern as prerequisite. | 2016 |
| TH-03 | **McGraw & Warren: "Benign Violations Are Funnier..."** (J. Personality & Social Psychology) | Benign violation theory (BVT). Something is humorous when it simultaneously violates expectations and seems benign. Psychological distance moderates: more distance makes violations seem more benign. | 2010, 2016 |
| TH-04 | **Hurley, Dennett & Adams: "Inside Jokes"** (MIT Press) | Humor as cognitive debugging — evolved to reward detection of false beliefs. The "ha-ha" as reward for catching errors in mental models. Sophisticated descendant of incongruity theory. Connects humor to insight and epistemic vigilance. | 2011 |
| TH-05 | **Warren & McGraw: "Differentiating what is humorous from what is not"** (J. Personality & Social Psychology) | Empirical tests of BVT vs alternative theories. Five studies showing violations perceived as simultaneously benign produce humor. Severity and distance interact. | 2016 |
| TH-06 | **PMC: "You Must Be Joking! Benign Violations, Power Asymmetry, and Humor"** (Frontiers in Psychology) | BVT extended to power dynamics. Power asymmetry affects what counts as "benign." Higher-power individuals enjoy more aggressive humor. Cultural context shapes violation perception. | 2019 |
| TH-07 | **Gervais & Wilson: "The evolution and functions of laughter and humor"** (Quarterly Review of Biology) | Synthetic evolutionary approach. Duchenne laughter as honest signal. "False alarm" theory — humor signals safety after perceived threat. Two-stage model: stimulus appraisal + social context. | 2005 |
| TH-08 | **Martin, R.A.: "The Psychology of Humor: An Integrative Approach"** | Textbook synthesis of humor research. Four humor styles: affiliative, self-enhancing, aggressive, self-defeating. HSQ (Humor Styles Questionnaire). Connects personality to humor use. | 2006 |

## 2. Neuroscience of Laughter

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| NS-01 | **Mobbs et al.: "Humor modulates the mesolimbic reward centers"** (Neuron) | Landmark fMRI study. Humor activates nucleus accumbens, ventral striatum, VTA. Degree of funniness positively correlated with BOLD signal in reward regions. Established humor-reward connection. | 2003 |
| NS-02 | **Harvard Medical School: "Humor, Laughter, and Those Aha Moments"** | Joke processing: setup activates left hemisphere prediction, punchline triggers right hemisphere reframe + reward circuit. Frontal lobe processes info, supplementary motor area directs laughter, nucleus accumbens assesses reward. | 2010 |
| NS-03 | **Wattendorf et al.: "Establishing the Roles of Dorsal and Ventral Striatum in Humor"** (J. Neuroscience) | Four-stage model: setup encoding, incongruity detection, resolution, appreciation. Dorsal striatum for comprehension, ventral striatum for appreciation. Dissociates understanding jokes from finding them funny. | 2023 |
| NS-04 | **Provine, R.R.: "Laughter as a Scientific Problem"** (J. Comparative Neurology) | "Sidewalk neuroscience" approach. People laugh 30x more in groups. Laughter primarily social, not cognitive. Speaker laughs more than audience. Laughter punctuates speech at phrase boundaries. | 2000 |
| NS-05 | **PMC: "Laughter as a Subject for Interdisciplinary Investigations"** | Comprehensive review bridging philosophy and neuroscience. Duchenne (genuine) vs non-Duchenne laughter. Neural pathways for voluntary vs involuntary laughter differ. Gelotology as field. | 2023 |
| NS-06 | **Scott, S.K. et al.: "The social life of laughter"** (Trends in Cognitive Sciences) | Contagious laughter mediated by mirror neuron system. Hearing laughter triggers premotor cortex. Social context modulates laughter perception. In-group vs out-group laughter processing differs. | 2014 |
| NS-07 | **Gervais & Wilson: "The evolution and functions of laughter"** (Quarterly Review of Biology) | Duchenne laughter as honest signal of cooperative intent. Non-Duchenne laughter as strategic social tool. Evolutionary continuity with great ape play signals. | 2005 |
| NS-08 | **Provine, R.R.: "Contagious laughter: Laughter is a sufficient stimulus"** (Bulletin Psychonomic Society) | Experimental demonstration that hearing laughter alone triggers laughter. No humor required — laughter is inherently contagious. TV laugh tracks exploit this mechanism. | 1992 |

## 3. Comedy Structure & Craft

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| CC-01 | **Rule of three — Wikipedia** | Establish, reinforce, surprise. Two items create pattern, third subverts. Comedy uses the minimum needed to set expectation before violating it. Latin: "omne trium perfectum." | Ongoing |
| CC-02 | **Chris Head: "Rule of Three, Callbacks & Anachronisms"** | Professional comedy teacher. Callback as linking device — unexpected return of earlier material. Anachronism as incongruity source. Pattern-breaking requires pattern-setting. | 2018 |
| CC-03 | **Ali Mehedi: "Comprehensive Guide to Standup Comedy Joke Writing"** | Setup-punch mechanics. Misdirection: lead audience toward wrong assumption. Act-outs: physical embodiment of joke. Taglines: additional punchlines building on original. The "tight five." | 2023 |
| CC-04 | **Greg Dean's Stand-Up Comedy** | Setup = premise. Punchline = reinterpretation. Timing: communicating faster than comprehension kills the laugh. Comic timing as controlled release of tension. | Ongoing |
| CC-05 | **Commonplace Fun Facts: "The Jack Benny Program"** | Jack Benny's "Your money or your life" — the most famous pause in comedy. Built career around silence. The audience completes the joke before the comedian speaks. Timing as the art of not-saying. | 2026 |
| CC-06 | **Ken Levine: "The Comedy Rule of 3's"** | TV writing room perspective. First joke introduces, second develops, third takes to extreme or reverses. Experienced writers sometimes break the rule deliberately. The rule describes audience expectation, not a formula. | 2017 |
| CC-07 | **The Script Lab: "The Rule of 3"** | Screenwriting application. Setup-elaboration-payoff in scene structure. Applies at joke, scene, and story level. Breaking the pattern works because the pattern was established. | 2020 |
| CC-08 | **Hothouse Literary Journal: "Literary Lessons from Stand-Up"** | Tension-release as comedy's fundamental mechanism. "Comedy is simply tension and release." Explaining a joke kills it because it bypasses the cognitive resolution that produces the reward. | 2020 |

## 4. Dark Humor & Taboo

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| DH-01 | **Freud: "Jokes and Their Relation to the Unconscious"** | Tendentious vs innocent jokes. Tendentious: hostile or obscene, disguise aggression/desire. Joke-work parallels dream-work (condensation, displacement). Social function: allows expression of forbidden impulses. | 1905 |
| DH-02 | **Freud: "Humour" (journal article)** | Humor as triumph of ego over adversity. Super-ego comforts ego. Gallows humor: criminal on Monday says "Well, this is a good beginning to the week." The mind asserts its invulnerability. | 1928 |
| DH-03 | **Willinger et al.: "Cognitive and emotional demands of black humour processing"** (Cognitive Processing) | 156 participants. Dark humor preference correlates with higher verbal and nonverbal IQ. Three groups: moderate IQ/moderate appreciation, high aggression/low appreciation, high IQ/low aggression/highest appreciation. Emotional instability decreases dark humor enjoyment. | 2017 |
| DH-04 | **PMC: "Sanity through Insanity: Dark Humor among US Veterans"** | Gallows humor as coping mechanism in military contexts. Facilitates reappraisal of traumatic experiences. Creates psychological distance from overwhelming intensity. Bond-building through shared taboo transgression. | 2024 |
| DH-05 | **PMC: "Understanding Association Between Humor and Emotional Distress"** | Light vs dark humor predict different outcomes. Dark humor negatively correlated with depression and anxiety in some populations. Mediating role of cognitive flexibility. | 2024 |
| DH-06 | **PDX Scholar: "Dark Humor in Trauma-and-Crisis-Centered" contexts** | Healthcare workers use dark humor for emotional regulation. Creates professional distance while maintaining empathy. Functions differently from civilian dark humor — occupational vs recreational. | 2023 |
| DH-07 | **McGraw & Warren: Moral violation and distance** | "Too soon" explained by BVT: temporal distance hasn't yet made the violation benign. Geographic, social, and temporal distance all moderate. Tragedy + time = comedy is empirically supported. | 2010-2016 |

## 5. Cultural Comedy

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| CU-01 | **Academia.edu: "Cross-Cultural Analysis of Humor in Stand-Up: US and Japan"** | American solo stand-up vs Japanese manzai duo. US comedians address audience directly; Japanese frame audience as overhearers. Manzai: boke (funny man) + tsukkomi (straight man). | 2015 |
| CU-02 | **Macquarie University: "Humor in Manzai Stand-up Comedy"** | Historical and comparative analysis. Manzai rooted in 1000-year-old tradition. Quick-exchange wordplay. Language itself as the mechanism of comedy. Distinct from Western joke structure. | 2018 |
| CU-03 | **Shifman, Limor: "Memes in Digital Culture"** (MIT Press) | Internet memes as participatory humor. Digital content units with common characteristics, created with awareness of each other. Memes reveal collective humor: what's shared, remixed, and transformed. | 2013 |
| CU-04 | **Day Translations: "The Role of Humor in Language — Cross-Cultural Analysis"** | Humor translation challenges. Puns nearly untranslatable. Cultural references require shared knowledge. Self-deprecating humor norms vary: British default, American selective, Japanese expected in certain contexts. | 2023 |
| CU-05 | **PoliLingua: "Challenges of Humor Translation Across Cultures"** | Linguistic humor and untranslatability. Wordplay depends on phonological/semantic overlap unique to each language. Humor localization vs translation. Some jokes simply cannot cross language barriers. | 2023 |
| CU-06 | **Woke Waves: "Laugh Lines: Cultural Humor Around the World"** | British dry wit (understatement, irony). American physical + observational. German Schadenfreude. Indian mimicry traditions. Australian self-deprecating humor as social leveling. | 2024 |
| CU-07 | **American Anthropologist: "Can anthropologists get humor?"** (Wiley) | Ethnographic thick description needed for humor analysis. Humor as window into unstated social norms. Cross-cultural humor study requires intimate local knowledge. | 2024 |

## 6. Humor, Health & Social Bonding

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| HH-01 | **PMC (Bennett et al.): "Humor and Laughter May Influence Health IV: Immune Function"** | Review: laughter decreases cortisol, increases lymphocyte blastogenesis. IgA production enhanced. Natural killer cell activity increases. Stress hormones reduced. | 2003 |
| HH-02 | **Norman Cousins: "Anatomy of an Illness"** | Self-treatment claim: 10 minutes of belly laughter = 2 hours pain-free sleep. Marx Brothers films + Candid Camera. Ankylosing spondylitis. Pioneer of mind-body connection in popular culture. Scientific basis: mixed/weak. | 1979 |
| HH-03 | **Laughter Yoga International (Kataria)** | Dr. Madan Kataria, Mumbai 1995. Three elements: laughter exercises, yogic breath-work, mindfulness. 40 foundation exercises. Started with 5 people in a park. Body doesn't distinguish real from simulated laughter. | 1995 |
| HH-04 | **ScienceDirect: "Efficacy of laughter-inducing interventions"** (Meta-analysis) | 45 studies, 2,547 participants. Significant positive effects on mental health (31 studies), physiological outcomes (14 studies), physical health (21 studies). Non-humorous laughter attains higher effect sizes. Overall evidence quality: low. | 2022 |
| HH-05 | **Springer: "Laughter Therapy: Life Satisfaction and Anxiety Control"** (Meta-analysis) | Significant effect on reducing anxiety (large effect size). Laughter therapy effective for depression, anxiety, and stress in cancer patients. But field "has yet to reach maturity." | 2025 |
| HH-06 | **Dunbar, R.I.M.: Laughter as social grooming** | Social laughter increases endogenous opioid release. Laughter functions as "grooming at a distance" — allows bonding with multiple individuals simultaneously. Extends Dunbar's number framework. | 2012 |
| HH-07 | **Bressler et al.: "Production and appreciation of humor as sexually selected traits"** | Humor as fitness indicator. Women prefer humor producers; men prefer humor appreciators. Sense of humor = #1 desired trait in partner across studies. Sexual selection drives humor evolution. | 2006 |
| HH-08 | **PMC: "Effects of Humor Production, Humor Receptivity, and Physical Attractiveness"** | Women preferred humor producers for all relationship types. Men preferred humor-receptive partners, especially for sexual relationships. Humor production signals intelligence and creativity. | 2015 |
| HH-09 | **Mayo Clinic Press: "The health benefits of humor"** | Clinical summary: laughter stimulates organs, activates stress response then soothes it, relieves pain via endorphins, improves immune function, increases personal satisfaction. | 2024 |
| HH-10 | **Michigan Today: "Laugh yourself healthy"** | 2023 meta-analysis: laughter interventions decreased cortisol by 32-37% compared to controls. Evidence for pain tolerance increase. Parasympathetic nervous system activation. | 2026 |

## 7. AI & Computational Humor

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| AI-01 | **ArXiv: "AI Humor Generation: Cognitive, Social and Creative Skills"** | Humor requires social skills (theory of mind, audience awareness), cognitive skills (reasoning, surprise), creative skills (novelty, subversion). No current AI system integrates all three. | 2025 |
| AI-02 | **ACM: "Can AI Take a Joke — Or Make One?"** (Creativity & Cognition) | Study of humor generation and recognition in LLMs. Users rate humor-trained LLM captions nearly on par with top human humor. But models remain formulaic; lack surprise. | 2025 |
| AI-03 | **ArXiv: "ChatGPT is fun, but it is not funny!"** | Humor remains challenging for LLMs even with fine-tuning. Original humor production extremely difficult. Models tend toward safe, predictable outputs. Joke structure recognition ≠ joke generation. | 2023 |
| AI-04 | **ArXiv: "HumorBench — Which LLMs Get the Joke?"** | On HumorBench-hard, no LLM exceeds 60% accuracy. Complex examples requiring cultural knowledge or multi-step reasoning stump all models. Humans find these intuitive. | 2025 |
| AI-05 | **Medium (Thomas Smith): "Why Humor Is the Perfect Benchmark for GenAI"** | Humor as Turing test: requires understanding people and their intents. Best LLMs succeed because they model human cognition, not because they follow formulas. Humor tests the depth of understanding. | 2023 |
| AI-06 | **Jing Conan Wang: "Why Is It So Hard to Create a Funny AI?"** | Two key ingredients: surprise and contradiction. Require understanding of how things logically work, then intentionally deviating. Many successful computational wordplay systems, but these don't extend to broader humor. | 2024 |
| AI-07 | **Finn Group: "Humor as benchmark for LLMs"** | Humor tests cultural knowledge, timing, audience modeling, and creative subversion simultaneously. No other benchmark requires all four. Progress in humor = progress toward general intelligence. | 2024 |
