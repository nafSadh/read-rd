The Science of Humor: Why We Laugh

Theories of humor, the neuroscience of laughter, comedy craft, dark humor, cultural variation, health effects, and the question of whether machines can be funny.

Humor is one of the few human universals that resists clean explanation. This project surveys the science of humor across 7 dimensions and 50+ sources — from incongruity theory and benign violation through the neural circuitry of laughter, the craft of comedy writing, dark humor and taboo, cultural comedy styles, humor’s health effects, and the frontier of computational humor and AI.

Key Tensions

  • 01 Theory unification: Incongruity, superiority, and relief theories each capture something real but no framework unifies them
  • 02 Humor and offense: The benign violation boundary is subjective and culturally contingent — one person’s comedy is another’s cruelty
  • 03 Craft vs. spontaneity: Professional comedy is highly structured, but the best humor often feels effortless and spontaneous
  • 04 AI humor: LLMs can generate joke structures but consistently fail at genuine funniness — what does this reveal about understanding?
