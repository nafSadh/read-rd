Sophie's World
A companion to the history of philosophy, from the Pre-Socratics to the present
Based on Jostein Gaarder's novel (1991) · Extended through 2026

In 1991, Jostein Gaarder published Sophie's World, a novel that made the entire sweep of Western philosophy accessible through the story of a 14-year-old Norwegian girl receiving mysterious letters about philosophy. The book became an international phenomenon, translated into 65 languages.

This companion extends that journey. Seven interactive deep-dives cover the philosophical terrain Gaarder mapped — the Pre-Socratics through existentialism — while also charting the 35 years of philosophical development that have unfolded since the book was published. Each section provides both what Gaarder taught and what he couldn't have known.

About This Project

This companion was built following the methodology established in the Agentic LLMs Survey: start from an existing work, read the primary source iteratively, take structured notes, supplement with web research, then synthesize into interactive deep-dives.

The source material was Gaarder's novel (pages 1–184 of the English translation), covering the Garden of Eden through the Middle Ages. Chapters beyond the PDF — Renaissance through existentialism — were reconstructed from Gaarder's known treatment supplemented by web research against the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy, and contemporary scholarship. The final section, "Philosophy After Sophie," covers 35 years of developments the book couldn't anticipate.

Each chapter of the PDF was extracted via PyMuPDF, read, and annotated with structured notes capturing: philosophers covered, key concepts, questions posed, what Gaarder does well, what he omits, and connections to later philosophy. These notes drove the deep-dive content. The interactive HTML format uses a three-panel layout (sidebar navigation, scrollable summaries, expandable detail panel) with inline SVG diagrams, light/dark theming, and responsive design.
