# S4: Physics Paradoxes

> Physics paradoxes are the most productive kind. Their resolutions reveal how reality actually works.

**Sources:** 8 (Wikipedia Schrodinger/Maxwell/Physical Paradox, SEP EPR, NatGeo, ScienceAlert, ScienceDaily, EditVerse)
**Adequacy:** Adequate.

---

## 1. Schrodinger's Cat (1935)

Designed by Schrodinger to critique the Copenhagen interpretation, not to support it. Cat in sealed box with radioactive trigger. QM says atom is in superposition until measured. Does the cat become a superposition of alive and dead? Schrodinger thought this was absurd. Three main interpretations: Copenhagen (measurement collapses the wave function), Many-Worlds (both outcomes occur in separate branches), Decoherence (environment rapidly entangles with quantum system, making superposition unobservable).

## 2. EPR Paradox (1935) and Bell's Theorem (1964)

Einstein, Podolsky, Rosen argued QM must be incomplete because it predicts "spooky action at a distance" — measuring one entangled particle instantly determines the state of the other. Bell (1964) proved no local hidden variable theory can reproduce QM predictions. Aspect (1982) and others experimentally confirmed Bell violations. 2022 Nobel Prize for experimental tests. Nature really is nonlocal (or we abandon other assumptions like realism).

## 3. Maxwell's Demon (1867)

Demon sorts fast/slow gas molecules between chambers, creating temperature difference without work. Seems to violate second law of thermodynamics. Resolution via information theory: Szilard (1929) showed measurement costs energy. Landauer (1961) proved erasing information increases entropy. Bennett (1982) completed the argument. Information is physical; entropy and information are deeply connected.

## 4. The Twin Paradox (1905)

One twin travels at near-light speed and returns younger. Apparent paradox: from each twin's reference frame, the other is moving. Shouldn't aging be symmetric? Resolution: the traveling twin accelerates (changes reference frame), breaking the symmetry. Only the stay-at-home twin remains in an inertial frame. Time dilation is real — GPS satellites must account for it.

## 5. The Grandfather Paradox

Travel back in time and prevent your grandfather from meeting your grandmother. You're never born. You never travel back. Your grandfather meets your grandmother. You're born. You travel back. Loop. Main resolutions: time travel is impossible (most physicists); the Novikov self-consistency principle (physics conspires to prevent paradoxes); branching timelines (many-worlds).

## 6. Olbers' Paradox (1823)

If the universe is infinite, eternal, and filled with stars, every line of sight hits a star eventually. The sky should be bright. It isn't. Resolution: the universe is finite in age (13.8 billion years), expanding (light redshifts), and stars have finite lifetimes. The dark sky is evidence for the Big Bang.

## 7. The Black Hole Information Paradox

Hawking (1975) showed black holes radiate thermally and eventually evaporate. But thermal radiation carries no information about what fell in. Where did the information go? QM says information is conserved. General relativity says it's lost. Still debated. Leading proposals: information is encoded in Hawking radiation (AdS/CFT supports this), or information is stored on the event horizon (holographic principle).

## 8. Physics Paradoxes as Discovery Engines

Pattern: a paradox reveals a tension between theory and intuition → investigation reveals that the theory is correct and intuition is wrong → the resolution teaches us something deep about reality. Schrodinger's Cat → QM is correct at macro scale (decoherence). EPR → nature is nonlocal. Maxwell's Demon → information is physical. Olbers → the universe had a beginning.
