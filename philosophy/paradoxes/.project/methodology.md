# Methodology — Paradoxes: From Zeno to Gödel

## Research Question
What is a paradox, why do paradoxes persist across 2,500 years of intellectual history, and what do they reveal about the limits of human reasoning? This project surveys paradoxes across philosophy, mathematics, physics, decision theory, identity, and visual art — treating them not as puzzles to be solved but as boundary markers of conceptual systems.

## Scope & Dimensions
This survey covers 7 domains:
1. **Ancient paradoxes** — Zeno (motion), Sorites (vagueness), Epimenides (self-reference). How these broke Greek mathematics and persisted until calculus and set theory.
2. **Logical paradoxes** — Liar, Russell's, Curry's, Grelling-Nelson, Berry's. The foundational crisis and Gödel's incompleteness theorems.
3. **Mathematical paradoxes** — Banach-Tarski, Gabriel's Horn, Hilbert's Hotel, Birthday, Monty Hall, Simpson's. The counterintuitive as mathematical fact.
4. **Physics paradoxes** — Schrödinger's Cat, EPR/Bell, Maxwell's Demon, Twin paradox, Grandfather paradox, Olbers', Information paradox. Paradoxes as engines of discovery.
5. **Decision & game theory paradoxes** — Prisoner's Dilemma, Newcomb's Problem, Trolley Problem, St. Petersburg, Braess's, Arrow's impossibility. Rationality undermining itself.
6. **Identity & persistence paradoxes** — Ship of Theseus, Teletransportation, Swampman, Chinese Room. Personal identity through time and what AI inherits.
7. **Visual & self-referential paradoxes** — Escher, Penrose, Magritte, Droste effect, Hofstadter's strange loops. Self-reference in art and logic.

Deliberately excluded: paradoxes in economics (Giffen goods, etc.), linguistic paradoxes beyond those with philosophical weight, recreational puzzle paradoxes without conceptual depth.

## Source Strategy
- Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy, and Wikipedia as foundational references
- Primary texts where accessible (Zeno via Aristotle, Russell's letter to Frege, Gödel 1931, Searle 1980, Parfit 1984)
- Academic surveys and encyclopedia entries for mathematical and physical paradoxes
- Quanta Magazine, Britannica, and other accessible explanatory sources
- Visual sources for Escher, Penrose, Magritte

## Reading & Synthesis Approach
Taxonomic survey: what makes each paradox paradoxical, how has each been addressed (if at all), and what structural family does each belong to? Cross-cutting themes: self-reference, infinity, vagueness, measurement, identity.
