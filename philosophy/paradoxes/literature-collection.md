## Tensions

| Claim | Position A | Position B |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Are Zeno's paradoxes solved? | Mathematicians: calculus and measure theory resolve them completely | Philosophers: mathematical resolution doesn't address the metaphysical puzzle of continuity |
| Is the Axiom of Choice acceptable? | Most mathematicians accept it as standard | Constructivists reject it; Banach-Tarski shows its "absurd" consequences |
| Does the Chinese Room refute strong AI? | Searle: syntax is never sufficient for semantics | Dennett, Hofstadter: the system as a whole understands; Searle's intuition misleads |
| Is Newcomb's Problem solved? | One-boxers: evidential decision theory supports taking one box | Two-boxers: causal decision theory demands taking both boxes |
| Are paradoxes failures or features? | Logical positivists: paradoxes reveal linguistic confusion | Gödel, Hofstadter: paradoxes reveal fundamental structural features of formal systems |
| Is personal identity real? | Parfit: there is no further fact; identity is conventional | Commonsense: of course you are the same person over time |
