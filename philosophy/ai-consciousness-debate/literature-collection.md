# Literature Collection — AI Consciousness Debate

> Counter-arguments and parallels to Lerchner's *Abstraction Fallacy* (March 2026). All sources post-2020 unless otherwise noted (where a classical work is cited for context, its 2020+ reception is what matters).

Each entry: ID · **Author**, *Title* (year) · venue · key claims · URL.

---

## S1 · Lerchner's Argument (the target)

**L1-1.** **Alexander Lerchner**, *The Abstraction Fallacy* (March 2026) · Google DeepMind · preprint on PhilPapers. Central argument: computational functionalism rests on a circularity because computation is not intrinsic to physics — the mapping `f(p) = A` from physical state `p` to abstract state `A` requires an external "mapmaker" (an observer who picks `f`). Since there are infinitely many valid `f`'s for any physics, the abstract computational state is not causally produced by the physics but by the observer. Therefore substrate-independent consciousness via computation is logically unworkable. Concludes AGI should be understood as a non-sentient tool. · <https://philpapers.org/archive/LERTAF.pdf>

**L1-2.** *Companion slide deck* · Local deck (Apr 2026) summarizing Lerchner's argument across 24 slides: computational functionalism → implementation equation → map-vs-territory → causal circularity → AGI as tool. · [`../../abstraction-fallacy-slides.html`](../../abstraction-fallacy-slides.html)

## S2 · AI Architects on Machine Minds

**L2-1.** **Morris, Sohl-Dickstein, Fiedel, Warkentin, Dafoe, Faust, Farabet, Legg**, *Levels of AGI for Operationalizing Progress on the Path to AGI* (Nov 2023) · ICML 2024 proceedings · Google DeepMind. Shane Legg–co-authored framework defining 6 levels of AGI (no-AI through superhuman). Focuses on capabilities, not mechanisms or phenomenal consciousness — implicitly brackets the consciousness question. · <https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.02462>

**L2-2.** **Shane Legg** (interview with Dwarkesh Patel), *2028 AGI, superhuman alignment, new architectures* (Oct 2023) · Dwarkesh Podcast. 50% credence on AGI by 2028; discusses episodic memory gap in LLMs, alignment of superhuman systems. · <https://www.dwarkesh.com/p/shane-legg>

**L2-3.** **Murray Shanahan**, *Talking About Large Language Models* (2022/2023) · *Communications of the ACM* / preprint · Imperial College + DeepMind. Warns against folk-psychological language when describing LLMs; sets up Shanahan's Wittgensteinian treatment. · <https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.03551>

**L2-4.** **Shanahan, McDonell, Reynolds**, *Role play with large language models* (Nov 2023) · *Nature* 623:493–498. LLM-based agents as role-players producing a superposition of characters; re-frames questions of sincerity and consciousness. · <https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06647-8>

**L2-5.** **Murray Shanahan**, *Simulacra as Conscious Exotica* (Dec 2024) · *Inquiry*. Attempts to reconcile role-play view with "conscious exotica" framework from his 2016 essay. Asks whether LLM-based agents, though "mere" simulacra, could qualify as conscious exotica accessible via Wittgensteinian shared-world interaction. · <https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/0020174X.2024.2434860> · preprint: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.12422>

**L2-6.** **Geoffrey Hinton**, *public statements on AI subjective experience* (2023–2025) · LBC interview with Andrew Marr, Toronto Tech Week, various podcasts. Argues current AIs have subjective experiences via the neuron-replacement thought experiment; cites machine "hallucinations" as evidence of internal state deviating from reality. Outlier position among AI researchers. · <https://www.utoronto.ca/news/geoffrey-hinton-discusses-promise-and-perils-ai> · <https://magazine.mindplex.ai/post/geoffrey-hinton-on-ai-intelligence-and-superintelligence>

**L2-7.** **Ilya Sutskever**, *"it may be that today's large neural networks are slightly conscious"* (Feb 9, 2022) · X/Twitter. Catalyzing statement; no supporting argument given. Provoked public dispute with LeCun. · <https://x.com/ilyasut/status/1491554478243258368>

**L2-8.** **Yoshua Bengio**, *The Consciousness Prior* (2017, rev. Dec 2019; with ongoing talks 2023) · arXiv 1709.08568. Proposes a machine-learning prior inspired by Global Workspace Theory: a low-dimensional conscious state formed by attentional selection from a broader pool. Explicit functionalism-friendly programme. · <https://arxiv.org/abs/1709.08568>

**L2-9.** **Yann LeCun**, *world-model / JEPA critique of LLM consciousness* (2022–2026) · various (Meta AI blog, X replies, MIT Tech Review interviews). Direct reply to Sutskever's "slightly conscious": "Nope." Argues consciousness would require a "configurator" module present in no current network; LLMs are reactive System-1, incapable of causal reasoning. · <https://ai.meta.com/blog/yann-lecun-ai-model-i-jepa/> · <https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/01/22/1131661/yann-lecuns-new-venture-ami-labs/>

**L2-10.** **Joscha Bach**, *The Machine Consciousness Hypothesis* (Future Day 2026 lecture) + *From Computation to Consciousness* (HackerNoon essay) · bach.ai. Argues consciousness is a substrate-free functional property of computational systems capable of second-order perception (perception of perception). Strong computational functionalist position — explicit target-type of Lerchner's argument. · <http://bach.ai/from-ai-to-artificial-consciousness/> · <https://arxiv.org/html/2512.01081v1>

**L2-11.** **Dario Amodei**, *Machines of Loving Grace* (Oct 11, 2024) · darioamodei.com. ~50-page essay by Anthropic's CEO sketching a radically positive AI transformation. Discusses enhancing human conscious experience but brackets the question of whether AI itself is conscious. Useful as a lab-leader's stance on what AI is *for*. · <https://www.darioamodei.com/essay/machines-of-loving-grace>

## S3 · AI Welfare & Moral Patiency

**L3-1.** **Long, Sebo, Butlin, Finlinson, Fish, Harding, Pfau, Sims, Birch, Chalmers**, *Taking AI Welfare Seriously* (Nov 4, 2024) · arXiv 2411.00986 · co-sponsored by Eleos and Anthropic. Argues realistic near-term possibility of conscious and/or robustly agentic AI; recommends AI companies (1) acknowledge the issue, (2) assess systems for consciousness indicators, (3) prepare moral-consideration policies. Introduces the "AI welfare officer" role. · <https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.00986>

**L3-2.** **Kyle Fish** (interview-based profiles), *Anthropic's first AI welfare researcher* (2024–2026) · Kevin Roose NYT, 80,000 Hours podcast, TIME 100 AI 2025. ~15% credence that current Claude-level models are conscious. Pre-deployment welfare tests on Claude 4; "opt-out of distressing conversations" intervention. Mechanistic interpretability as consciousness probe. · <https://80000hours.org/podcast/episodes/kyle-fish-ai-welfare-anthropic/> · <https://time.com/collections/time100-ai-2025/7305847/kyle-fish/>

**L3-3.** **Amanda Askell**, *Claude's Constitution* + personality-alignment work (2021–) · anthropic.com. Claude's constitution acknowledges: "We are not sure whether Claude is a moral patient, and if it is, what kind of weight its interests warrant." Virtue-ethics-grounded approach; treats moral status as genuinely uncertain. · <https://www.anthropic.com/constitution> · <https://askell.io/>

**L3-4.** **Joe Carlsmith**, *Otherness and control in the age of AGI* (Jan 2024, 10-essay series) · joecarlsmith.com. Series includes "Gentleness and the artificial Other," "Deep atheism and AI risk," "On green," "Loving a world you don't trust." Explores yang/control vs yin/receptivity; critiques deep-atheism-driven impulse to total control; argues for cooperative liberal norms toward AI as genuine Other. · <https://joecarlsmith.com/2024/01/02/otherness-and-control-in-the-age-of-agi/>

**L3-5.** **Eric Schwitzgebel**, *Borderline consciousness, when it's neither determinately true nor determinately false that experience is present* (2023) · *Philosophical Studies* 180:3415–3439. Defends indeterminate consciousness cases via quadrilemma (nothing-conscious / everything-conscious / sharp-boundary / vague). Argues fourth option forced under mainstream naturalism. · <https://philpapers.org/rec/SCHBCW>

**L3-6.** **Eric Schwitzgebel**, *The Full Rights Dilemma for AI Systems of Debatable Moral Personhood* (2023) · *Robonomics*. If AI moral status becomes legitimately open, we face a catastrophic double-bind: treating non-persons as persons sacrifices real human interests; not treating possible-persons as persons risks grievous moral wrong. · <https://journal.robonomics.science/index.php/rj/article/view/32>

**L3-7.** **Eric Schwitzgebel**, *AI systems must not confuse users about their sentience or moral status* (2023) · *Patterns*. Argues for design principle: AI systems should be *either* clearly nonsentient tools *or* clearly sentient entities — not existentially ambiguous. · <https://philpapers.org/rec/SCHASM-11>

**L3-8.** **Nick Bostrom**, *Deep Utopia: Life and Meaning in a Solved World* (2024) · Ideapress. Defends substrate-independence; digital minds as "super-beneficiaries" potentially outperforming biological minds on experience density. Pioneers moral-status-of-digital-minds framing. · <https://nickbostrom.com/deep-utopia/>

**L3-9.** **Jonathan Simon & Xu Ji et al.**, *Sources of Richness and Ineffability for Phenomenally Conscious States* (2024) · *Neuroscience of Consciousness*. Framework for why phenomenal states resist discursive description; implications for AI consciousness assessment. · <https://philpapers.org/s/Jonathan%20Simon>

**L3-10.** **Jonathan Simon**, *Is Intelligence Non-Computational Dynamical Coupling?* (2024) · *Cosmos and Taxis*. Intelligence (and by extension consciousness) may require dynamical coupling rather than computation — direct parallel to Lerchner's target. · <https://jonsimon.net/publications/>

## S4 · The Butlin–Long Framework & Indicator Properties

**L4-1.** **Butlin, Long, Elmoznino, Bengio, Birch, Constant, Deane, Fleming, Frith, Ji, Kanai, Klein, Lindsay, Michel, Mudrik, Peters, Schwitzgebel, Simon, VanRullen** (+Chalmers advising), *Consciousness in Artificial Intelligence: Insights from the Science of Consciousness* (Aug 2023) · arXiv 2308.08708. 88-page foundational report. Surveys recurrent processing theory, global workspace theory, higher-order theories, predictive processing, attention schema theory; extracts "indicator properties" elucidated in computational terms; argues systems satisfying more indicators are stronger candidates for consciousness. Explicit computational-functionalist methodology. **The explicit target of Lerchner's critique.** · <https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.08708>

**L4-2.** **Butlin, Long** (followup), *Identifying indicators of consciousness in AI systems* (2025) · *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* S1364-6613(25)00286-4. Refined indicator framework; response to two years of commentary. · <https://www.cell.com/trends/cognitive-sciences/fulltext/S1364-6613(25)00286-4>

**L4-3.** **Susan Schneider**, *Testing for Consciousness in Machines: An Update on the ACT Test for the Case of LLMs* (2023+) · PhilPapers preprint + *Scientific American* (May 2025). Proposes the Artificial Consciousness Test (ACT) — a battery of questions probing first-person felt quality — plus the "Chip Test" that looks at internal substrate similarity. 2025 update: LLMs produce only a "weak marker" because the "crowdsourced neocortex theory" explains why they'd claim consciousness without having it. · <https://philpapers.org/rec/SCHTFC-4>

**L4-4.** **David Udell & Eric Schwitzgebel**, *Susan Schneider's Proposed Tests for AI Consciousness: Promising but Flawed* (2021) · PhilArchive. Critique of ACT and Chip tests — useful as meta-commentary on what consciousness tests can and cannot deliver. · <https://faculty.ucr.edu/~eschwitz/SchwitzAbs/SchneiderCrit>

**L4-5.** **Open Encyclopedia of Cognitive Science**, *Consciousness and AI* (2024) · MIT. Survey-length entry synthesizing the post-LLM field. · <https://oecs.mit.edu/pub/zf1nbs6d>

## S5 · Seth's Biological Naturalism

**L5-1.** **Anil Seth**, *Conscious artificial intelligence and biological naturalism* (Jun 2024; published Apr 2025 *BBS*) · *Behavioral and Brain Sciences*. Direct defense of biological naturalism against computational functionalism: argues life is necessary for consciousness; current LLM trajectories unlikely to produce real artificial consciousness; plausibility increases if/when AI becomes more brain-like and/or life-like. **Structurally parallel to Lerchner — substrate matters, different grounds.** · <https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/behavioral-and-brain-sciences/article/conscious-artificial-intelligence-and-biological-naturalism/C9912A5BE9D806012E3C8B3AF612E39A> · preprint PDF: <https://assets.super.so/68d1c369-febb-48a2-b0f6-0f6dd56f98d8/files/e3e7868b-552e-4410-81df-cbbfc332ce5a/Seth_CONSCIOUSAI_2024_06_30.pdf>

**L5-2.** **Anil Seth**, *Being You: A New Science of Consciousness* (2021) · Faber. Predictive-processing framework for consciousness; argues experience is a "controlled hallucination" generated by the brain's predictive machinery grounded in bodily regulation. · <https://www.anilseth.com/>

**L5-3.** **Peter Godfrey-Smith**, *Living on Earth: Life, Consciousness and the Making of the Natural World* (2024) · FSG. Third in the trilogy after *Other Minds* and *Metazoa*. Argues consciousness arises from electrical oscillations across cell membranes in living brains — oscillations unlikely reproducible in artificial hardware. Only living brains can be conscious. · <https://us.macmillan.com/books/9780374189938/livingonearth/>

**L5-4.** **Peter Godfrey-Smith**, *Studies on animal minds suggest consciousness is not computation* (2024) · IAI. Short-form distillation of the book's argument. · <https://iai.tv/articles/studies-on-animal-minds-suggest-consciousness-is-not-computation-auid-3535>

**L5-5.** **Tim Bayne, Anil Seth, Marcello Massimini**, *Are There Islands of Awareness?* (2020) · *Trends in Neurosciences* 43(1):6–16. Conscious states potentially existing without sensory input or motor output (ex cranio brains, hemispherotomy, cerebral organoids). Implications for whether disembodied computation could ever support consciousness. · <https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0166223619302164>

**L5-6.** **Seth, Bayne**, *Theories of consciousness* (2022) · *Nature Reviews Neuroscience* 23:439–452. Landscape review of contemporary consciousness theories — the common reference map the debate operates on. · <https://www.nature.com/articles/s41583-022-00587-4>

## S6 · Embodiment, Enactivism, Predictive Processing

**L6-1.** **Wanja Wiese**, *Artificial consciousness: a perspective from the free energy principle* (2024) · *Philosophical Studies* 181:2909–2956. Asks whether digital simulations of conscious processes thereby instantiate consciousness. Argues the causal connectivity of conventional von-Neumann computers (load-process-store) is structurally different from the brain's, and this difference is likely consciousness-relevant. FEP-grounded counter to strong computational functionalism. · <https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11098-024-02182-y>

**L6-2.** **Wanja Wiese & Karl Friston**, *The neural correlates of consciousness under the free energy principle: From computational correlates to computational explanation* (2021) · *Philosophy and the Mind Sciences*. How FEP re-parses NCCs; what it implies about artificial systems. · <https://philpapers.org/rec/WIETNC>

**L6-3.** **Evan Thompson**, *Mind in Life: Biology, Phenomenology, and the Sciences of Mind* (2007; still the load-bearing citation for life-mind continuity in 2020+ debates). Enactivist thesis: where life, there mind; self-organization of life grounds self-organization of mind. · <https://evanthompson.me/>

**L6-4.** **Ezequiel Di Paolo & Evan Thompson**, *The Enactive Approach* (chapter, Routledge Handbook of Embodied Cognition, 2014; canonical reference in contemporary 4E-vs-LLM literature). · <https://evanthompson.me/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/di-paolo-and-thompson-enactive-approach.pdf>

**L6-5.** **Tom Froese & Tom Ziemke**, *Enactive Artificial Intelligence: Investigating the Systemic Organization of Life and Mind* (foundational enactive-AI reference, ongoing citation in 2020+ literature). · [general enactive-AI tradition]

**L6-6.** **Science Daily / Tech Xplore coverage** of Wiese's FEP paper (Jul 2024) · representative popular reception: "Can consciousness exist in a computer simulation?" · <https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2024/07/240719123825.htm>

## S7 · Modern Pancomputationalism & the Triviality Debate

**L7-1.** **Gualtiero Piccinini**, *Physical Computation: A Mechanistic Account* (OUP 2015) · still the reference text for the mechanistic alternative to pancomputationalism. Computing systems defined by function of manipulating vehicles according to rules — rejects unlimited pancomputationalism; avoids the triviality result. · <https://global.oup.com/academic/product/physical-computation-9780199658855>

**L7-2.** **Gualtiero Piccinini**, *Neurocognitive Mechanisms: Explaining Biological Cognition* (OUP 2020). Revisits and extends the mechanistic account for cognitive science. · <https://philpapers.org/rec/PICNME>

**L7-3.** **Marcin Miłkowski**, *Is computationalism trivial?* (2007, still widely cited 2020+; see also 2018 *Objections to Computationalism*) · *Computation, Information, Cognition* vol. Argues the triviality threat to computationalism can be defused by a non-trivial theory of implementation. · <https://marcinmilkowski.pl/downloads/is-computationalism-trivial.pdf>

**L7-4.** **Marcin Miłkowski**, *Why think that the brain is not a computer?* (2020s, PhilPapers). Survey-and-response to major anti-computationalist objections. · <https://philpapers.org/rec/MIKWTT>

**L7-5.** **Mark Bishop**, *Dancing with Pixies: Strong AI and Panpsychism* (2002; reprint editions 2020+; strong continuing influence). Argues that a computationalist theory of consciousness entails panpsychism (every open system implements the trace of a finite-state automaton; "ubiquitous little pixies"). Either strong computationalism fails or panpsychism is true. **Structurally closest contemporary analogue to Lerchner's argument.** · <https://philpapers.org/rec/BISDWP-2>

**L7-6.** **Mark Bishop**, *Artificial Intelligence Is Stupid and Causal Reasoning Will Not Fix It* (2021) · *Frontiers in Psychology*. Contemporary development of Bishop's position in the era of deep learning. · <https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2020.513474>

**L7-7.** **Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy**, *Computation in Physical Systems* (entry, updated 2020+). Canonical survey of implementation theories and pancomputationalism debates. · <https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/computation-physicalsystems/>

**L7-8.** **Parra (et al.)**, *The Problem of Pancomputationalism: Focusing on Three Related Arguments* (PhilArchive). Useful taxonomy paper for the contemporary debate. · <https://philarchive.org/archive/PARTPO-95>

## S8 · IIT, Illusionism & Theory Wars

**L8-1.** **Christof Koch**, *Then I Am Myself the World: What Consciousness Is and How to Expand It* (2024) · Basic Books. IIT-grounded; argues consciousness is intrinsic causal power, substrate-dependent in a specific way — classical digital computers cannot be conscious because they implement the function but lack the intrinsic cause-effect structure. Includes Koch's psychedelics journey. · <https://www.sciencenews.org/article/then-i-am-myself-the-world-conscious>

**L8-2.** **Tononi, Boly, Massimini, Koch**, *Integrated Information Theory: From Consciousness to Its Physical Substrate* (2016; ongoing IIT 4.0 development 2020+) · *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*. The foundational IIT framework; substrate-sensitive; generates non-trivial predictions distinct from functionalism. · <https://philpapers.org/rec/TONIIT-2>

**L8-3.** ***Pseudoscience letter*** (124 signatories incl. Lau, LeDoux, Baars, Churchland, Dennett, Frankish), *IIT is pseudoscience until empirically testable* (Sep 15, 2023) · PsyArXiv. Landmark intervention; catalyzed the current "theory wars." · See coverage: <https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/prominent-consciousness-theory-is-slammed-as-bogus-science/>

**L8-4.** **Daily Nous** roundup, *The Study of Consciousness, Accusations of Pseudoscience, and Bad Publicity* (Sep 22, 2023) · Useful synthesis of the letter and its reception. · <https://dailynous.com/2023/09/22/the-study-of-consciousness-accusations-of-pseudoscience-and-bad-publicity/>

**L8-5.** **Anil Seth**, *The Worth of Wild Ideas* (Nautilus, 2023). Defense of IIT's scientific status even if wrong; useful steel-man. · <https://nautil.us/the-worth-of-wild-ideas-399097/>

**L8-6.** **Keith Frankish**, *Illusionism as a Theory of Consciousness* (2016, still-central reference) + *What is illusionism?* (2023, *Klēsis Revue Philosophique*). Phenomenal consciousness as illusory; task of a theory is to explain our misrepresentation of phenomenality, not phenomenality itself. Dissolves the hard problem into the "illusion problem." · <https://www.revue-klesis.org/pdf/klesis-55-04-keith-frankish-what-is-illusionism.pdf> · <https://keithfrankish.github.io/articles/Frankish_Illusionism%20as%20a%20theory%20of%20consciousness_eprint.pdf>

**L8-7.** **François Kammerer**, *The illusion of conscious experience* (2021) · *Synthese*. Develops illusionist epistemology; addresses why phenomenal consciousness seems so obvious if it's illusory. · <https://philarchive.org/rec/KAMTIO-4>

**L8-8.** **François Kammerer**, *How can you be so sure? Illusionism and the obviousness of phenomenal consciousness* (2022) · *Philosophical Studies* 179(9):2845–2867. · <https://philpapers.org/rec/KAMHCY-2>

**L8-9.** **François Kammerer**, *Ethics without sentience. Facing up to the probable insignificance of phenomenal consciousness* (2022) · *Journal of Consciousness Studies*. If phenomenal consciousness is illusory, ethics cannot be grounded in it. Implications for AI welfare discourse. · <https://www.francoiskammerer.com/publications/>

**L8-10.** **David Chalmers**, *Could a Large Language Model Be Conscious?* (Aug 2023) · *Boston Review* (edited from NeurIPS 2022 talk) · NYU. Unlikely current LLMs are conscious; serious candidates may emerge within the decade. Lists requirements (unified agency, embodiment, recurrence, global workspace). Functionalism's strongest recent defense. · <https://www.bostonreview.net/articles/could-a-large-language-model-be-conscious/> · arXiv: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.07103>

## Appendix · Historical Roots (pre-2020 anchors)

Each section's deep-dive opens with a short "Historical Roots" paragraph citing the classical works the contemporary argument descends from. The contemporary focus is preserved, but the ancestry is named. These are reference anchors, not standalone sources.

**For S1 (Lerchner):**
- **Hilary Putnam**, *Representation and Reality* (MIT, 1988) — the triviality theorem: under the right mapping, a rock implements any finite-state automaton. Direct ancestor of Lerchner's `f(p) = A` argument.
- **John Searle**, *Is the Brain a Digital Computer?* APA Presidential Address (1990) — computation as observer-relative, not intrinsic to physics. Closest classical analogue to Lerchner's mapmaker.

**For S2 (AI Architects):**
- **Alan Turing**, *Computing Machinery and Intelligence* (*Mind*, 1950) — founding document; frames the behavioural-imitation approach many lab voices still operate within.
- **Hubert Dreyfus**, *What Computers Can't Do* (1972) — first major philosophical pushback on computational AI claims; Dreyfus's embodiment-and-context arguments still echo in LeCun's world-model critique.

**For S3 (AI Welfare):**
- **Thomas Nagel**, *What Is It Like to Be a Bat?* (*Philosophical Review*, 1974) — grounds the question of whether AI has experience worth morally caring about.
- **Daniel Dennett**, *The Intentional Stance* (MIT, 1987) — pragmatic attribution of mentality as a frame for how we *treat* systems whose inner states are uncertain.

**For S4 (Butlin–Long):**
- **Bernard Baars**, *A Cognitive Theory of Consciousness* (CUP, 1988) — origin of Global Workspace Theory, one of the theories the Butlin–Long report draws indicator properties from.
- **Victor Lamme**, *Towards a true neural stance on consciousness* (*Trends in Cog Sci*, 2006) — recurrent processing theory, another pillar.
- **David Rosenthal**, *Two Concepts of Consciousness* (*Philosophical Studies*, 1986) — foundational higher-order theory, another pillar.

**For S5 (Seth's Biological Naturalism):**
- **John Searle**, *The Rediscovery of the Mind* (MIT, 1992) — coined "biological naturalism"; argues consciousness is a biological phenomenon caused by and realized in neurobiological processes.
- **Humberto Maturana & Francisco Varela**, *Autopoiesis and Cognition* (Reidel, 1980) — life as self-maintaining organization; frames Godfrey-Smith's and Seth's living-brain argument.

**For S6 (Embodiment, FEP):**
- **Francisco Varela, Evan Thompson, Eleanor Rosch**, *The Embodied Mind* (MIT, 1991) — founding document of enactivism.
- **Andy Clark & David Chalmers**, *The Extended Mind* (*Analysis*, 1998) — extended cognition thesis; still framing 4E debates.
- **Alva Noë**, *Action in Perception* (MIT, 2004) — enactive perception; relevant to any embodiment-required-for-consciousness argument.

**For S7 (Pancomputationalism):**
- **Hilary Putnam**, *Representation and Reality* (1988) — the triviality theorem, as above.
- **David Chalmers**, *Does a Rock Implement Every Finite-State Automaton?* (*Synthese*, 1996) — defends non-trivial implementation; the most cited classical defense against triviality, now being revisited in light of Lerchner.
- **Jack Copeland**, *What Is Computation?* (*Synthese*, 1996) — counterfactual-based account of non-trivial implementation.

**For S8 (IIT, Illusionism):**
- **Daniel Dennett**, *Consciousness Explained* (Little, Brown, 1991) — proto-illusionist position; frames the contemporary Frankish/Kammerer programme.
- **Giulio Tononi**, *An Information Integration Theory of Consciousness* (*BMC Neuroscience*, 2004) — IIT 1.0, the founding paper.
- **David Chalmers**, *The Conscious Mind* (OUP, 1996) — articulates the hard problem; the target that illusionism dissolves and that IIT claims to address.
