# Methodology — AI Consciousness Debate (Counter-Arguments & Parallels to Lerchner's Abstraction Fallacy)

## Research Question

What counter-arguments exist in the contemporary (2020–2026) literature against Alexander Lerchner's *The Abstraction Fallacy* (March 2026), and what recent arguments share its structure or target computational functionalism in parallel ways? The survey maps the live debate over machine consciousness that has intensified since large language models entered mainstream discourse.

## Scope & Dimensions

Eight sections, all drawing primarily on post-2020 sources:

1. **S1: Lerchner's Argument** — the target of the survey; map/territory framing, mapmaker dependency, causal circularity.
2. **S2: AI Architects on Machine Minds** — what the people building these systems think. Legg, Shanahan, Hinton, Sutskever, Bengio, LeCun, Bach, Amodei.
3. **S3: AI Welfare & Moral Patiency** — Fish, Long, Askell, Carlsmith, Schwitzgebel, Simon, Bostrom. Whether practical consciousness-hedging follows from functionalist assumptions.
4. **S4: The Butlin–Long Framework** — "Consciousness in AI" (2023) and the indicator-properties approach Lerchner explicitly targets.
5. **S5: Seth's Biological Naturalism** — Seth 2024 "Conscious artificial intelligence and biological naturalism"; Godfrey-Smith; the substrate-matters revival.
6. **S6: Embodiment, Enactivism, Predictive Processing** — Thompson, Wiese & Friston, 4E cognition, life-mind continuity.
7. **S7: Modern Pancomputationalism** — Piccinini, Miłkowski, Bishop; the contemporary triviality debate closest in form to Lerchner's mapmaker argument.
8. **S8: IIT, Illusionism & Theory Wars** — Tononi/Koch 2024; the 2023 IIT "pseudoscience" letter; Frankish/Kammerer illusionism.

### Historical grounding
Each section's deep-dive opens with a short "Historical Roots" paragraph naming 1–3 classical ancestors (Putnam 1988, Searle 1990/1992, Chalmers 1996, Varela 1991, Baars 1988, etc.). The contemporary focus is preserved — classical works are referenced as anchors, not surveyed in their own right. The `Historical Roots` appendix of the literature collection lists all 24 anchors organized per-section.

### Explicitly excluded
- Full surveys of classical 1970s–1990s arguments as standalone sections (Searle's Chinese Room, Putnam's original triviality, Block's original qualia papers are mentioned inline where relevant but not treated in depth).
- Pure cognitive science / neuroscience work that does not touch the functionalism/substrate question.
- AI alignment and control problems unrelated to phenomenal consciousness.

## Source Strategy

Primary sources are drawn from:

- **Peer-reviewed philosophy journals**: *Journal of Consciousness Studies*, *Philosophical Studies*, *Behavioral and Brain Sciences*, *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*.
- **Working papers & preprints**: arXiv (philosophy/cs.AI), PhilArchive, PhilPapers.
- **AI-lab essays & position papers**: Anthropic "Core Views," DeepMind AGI levels paper, Dario Amodei *Machines of Loving Grace*.
- **Long-form essays & interviews**: Dwarkesh Patel podcast (Shane Legg, Hinton), *Boston Review* (Chalmers on LLMs), *Aeon*, *The Gradient*.
- **Academic monographs (post-2020)**: Seth *Being You* (2021), Koch *Then I Am Myself the World* (2024), Simon *Artificial Consciousness* (2024), Godfrey-Smith *Living on Earth* (2024).

Citation tracking: starting from Lerchner's reference list and the Butlin–Long report's bibliography, expanding outward through cited-by chains on PhilPapers and Google Scholar.

## Reading & Synthesis Approach

Hybrid: primarily a **technical survey** (reading primary texts at depth) with **discourse framing** (showing how positions respond to each other). Each section's notes synthesize by sub-topic rather than paper-by-paper; cross-section tensions are captured in `notes/00-synthesis.md`.

### Reading depth tiers

- **Tier 1 (full read)**: Lerchner 2026, Butlin–Long 2023, Seth 2024, Chalmers 2023, Piccinini 2015/2020, Tononi/Koch 2024.
- **Tier 2 (targeted read)**: Shanahan LLM papers, Bengio Consciousness Prior, Amodei essay, Carlsmith essays, "Taking AI Welfare Seriously."
- **Tier 3 (key-claim extraction)**: Interviews, blog essays, shorter position pieces.
