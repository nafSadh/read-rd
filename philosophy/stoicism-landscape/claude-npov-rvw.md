## Issues Found

### POV (Point of View)

- **P-01 (strength):** The survey explicitly structures its final two sections around critiques and unresolved tensions. Section 06 presents five serious criticisms (emotional suppression, political quietism, slavery silence, pop dilution, gender skew) with citations. The synthesis lists five "fractures" in current Stoic discourse. This is exemplary NPOV practice -- the survey does not advocate for Stoicism.
- **P-02 (minor):** The overview summary for Section 04 (Modern Revival) includes specific sales figures (10M+ Holiday books) and Stoic Week results (15% fewer negative emotions). These are presented as facts, not endorsements. The same section notes Silicon Valley adoption criticism. Balanced.
- **P-03 (negligible):** The phrase "Stoicism is arguably more widely practiced today than at any point since the Roman Empire" is hedged with "arguably." Acceptable.

### RIGOR

- **R-01 (minor):** The Stoic Week statistics (15% reduction in negative emotions, 14% rise in life satisfaction) are cited to Modern Stoicism / Royal Holloway research. These are self-reported measures from a self-selected population without control groups. The text does not note this methodological limitation. **No fix applied** -- the data is accurately cited; methodological caveats are a depth concern rather than an NPOV issue.
- **R-02 (minor):** "Chrysippus wrote 705 books" is cited to the Stoic App Blog rather than a primary source (Diogenes Laertius 7.180). The claim is standard in the literature; the citation could be stronger.
- **R-03 (negligible):** Seneca's biography correctly notes the tension between his wealth and his philosophy, and the text acknowledges "Stoic defenders note that wealth is a 'preferred indifferent.'" Fair treatment.

### BIAS

- **B-01 (minor):** The survey gives significantly more space to sympathetic treatment of Stoicism (sections 1-5) than to critiques (section 6). This is a structural choice common to landscape surveys (present the tradition, then critiques), not bias per se. The critiques section is substantive and well-cited.
- **B-02 (minor):** The CBT-Stoicism connection is described as a "documented historical lineage" rather than "influence" or "analogy." This is the stronger claim and is supported by the cited sources (Ellis explicitly credited Epictetus). Accurate.
- **B-03 (moderate):** The survey's treatment of Nussbaum's critique quotes her as arguing Stoicism leads to "shallow and dehumanizing psychology" and then notes that "Pigliucci says she attacks a straw-man." Both positions are presented, but the Nussbaum position gets less elaboration. The synthesis section partially addresses this.

### TONE

- **T-01 (negligible):** Academic register maintained throughout. No hagiographic tone toward any Stoic figure. Seneca's hypocrisy is noted; Marcus Aurelius is described factually.
- **T-02 (negligible):** The phrase "pop-Stoicism in its Silicon Valley variant markets emotional repression as masculine strength, co-opted by the 'manosphere'" is strong language but is attributed to cited critical sources, not to the survey voice.

---

## Fixes Applied

None. The survey maintains strong NPOV through its structural commitment to a dedicated critiques section and a synthesis that foregrounds unresolved tensions.

---

## Remaining Concerns

1. **Stoic Week methodology:** A brief note about self-selection and lack of control groups would strengthen rigor without undermining the data.
2. **Nussbaum elaboration:** The emotional philosophy critique deserves slightly more space to match the depth of the sympathetic treatment.
3. **Primary source citations:** A few claims (Chrysippus's 705 books, Epictetus's popularity vs. Plato) cite secondary/tertiary sources where classical primary sources exist.

## Pass 2

### Issues Found and Fixed

#### POV -- Second-Person Address (4 fixes)

- **P2-01:** `overview.dd.html` callout: "Ethics tells *you* how to live, but *you* need physics..." -- second-person address draws reader into the Stoic framework rather than describing it neutrally. **Fixed:** changed to "Ethics addresses how to live, but physics is needed..."
- **P2-02:** `overview.dd.html` detail 03 (Core Doctrines): "*you* cannot possess one without the others" -- second person. **Fixed:** changed to "*one* cannot possess one without the others."
- **P2-03:** `overview.dd.html` summary 06 (Critiques): "acceptance-of-what-*you*-can't-control" -- second person embedded in compound adjective. **Fixed:** changed to "acceptance-of-what-*one*-can't-control."
- **P2-04:** `s6-critiques.dd.html` summary 01: "Stoicism teaches *you* to suppress *your* emotions" -- second person in describing the objection. **Fixed:** changed to "Stoicism teaches *adherents* to suppress *their* emotions."

#### Tone -- Editorial/Advocacy Language (5 fixes)

- **P2-05:** `overview.dd.html` summary 07 (Tensions): "The most valuable findings in any philosophical survey are the contradictions" -- editorial value judgment presented as universal truth. **Fixed:** softened to "The contradictions in a philosophical survey are often its most revealing findings."
- **P2-06:** `overview.dd.html` detail 06 (Critiques): "Any tradition that has endured for 2,300 years has accumulated serious and substantive criticism. The best critiques... are not dismissals but engagements" -- editorial characterization that implicitly praises the critiques' quality. **Fixed:** neutralized to "A tradition that has endured... The critiques examined here engage with Stoicism on its own terms rather than dismissing it."
- **P2-07:** `overview.dd.html` detail 07 (Tensions): "The most productive tensions... are not between Stoics and their critics but between equally serious interpreters" -- editorial superlative framing. **Fixed:** changed to "Notable tensions... exist not only between Stoics and their critics but between equally serious interpreters."
- **P2-08:** `s1-ancient-foundations.dd.html` detail 03 (Chrysippus callout): "The loss of this corpus is one of the great intellectual tragedies of antiquity" -- advocacy/editorial language ("tragedies"). **Fixed:** changed to "The scale of this textual loss is among the largest in the history of ancient philosophy."
- **P2-09:** `synthesis.dd.html` conclusion: "The five fractures... are not weaknesses -- they are signs of intellectual vitality. A tradition without live disagreements is a dead tradition." -- editorial advocacy for the value of disagreement. **Fixed:** changed to "The five fractures... reflect ongoing scholarly debate. A tradition with live disagreements is one that continues to attract serious engagement."

#### Scholastic Rigor -- Attribution and Precision (4 fixes)

- **P2-10:** `overview.dd.html` detail 05 (CBT): "CBT without philosophical grounding becomes mere symptom management" -- Robertson's argument presented as the survey's own conclusion. **Fixed:** attributed to Robertson ("Robertson argues... and that CBT without philosophical grounding becomes mere symptom management").
- **P2-11:** `s5-stoicism-cbt.dd.html` detail 05 (What CBT Drops): same unattributed Robertson claim. **Fixed:** attributed with "Robertson contends that..."
- **P2-12:** `synthesis.dd.html` section 05 (CBT Connection): same unattributed Robertson claim. **Fixed:** attributed with "Robertson argues... that..."
- **P2-13:** `s5-stoicism-cbt.dd.html` summaries and detail 01: CBT described as "modern science" -- CBT is clinical practice, not science per se. **Fixed:** changed both instances to "modern clinical practice."

#### Minor Evaluative Language (1 fix)

- **P2-14:** `s1-ancient-foundations.dd.html` consensus list: "Chrysippus's contributions to logic were *genuinely* original" -- the intensifier "genuinely" is unnecessary editorial emphasis when the text already argues for originality in context. **Fixed:** removed "genuinely."

#### Items Noted as Clean (from Pass 1 remaining concerns)

- **Stoic Week methodology:** s4-modern-revival.dd.html detail 03 already contains an "Evidence Caveat" callout noting self-selection bias and lack of RCT methodology. This was added between passes. Satisfactory.
- **s2-roman-stoics.dd.html** detail 04 (Marcus Aurelius): "Their power derives from their intimacy" -- editorial claim about textual quality. **Fixed:** changed to "Scholars attribute much of their enduring appeal to their intimacy."

### Summary

Pass 2 applied **15 fixes** across 5 files. The main patterns were:
1. Residual second-person address (4 instances) pulling the reader into the Stoic worldview rather than describing it
2. Editorial superlatives and value judgments in survey voice (5 instances)
3. Robertson's CBT critique presented as the survey's own conclusion rather than attributed (3 instances)
4. Minor precision issues (science vs. clinical practice; unnecessary intensifier) (3 instances)

No logical contradictions or unsupported factual claims were found. The survey's structural NPOV (dedicated critiques section, synthesis foregrounding fractures) remains strong. The fixes tighten the neutral register without altering substantive content.
