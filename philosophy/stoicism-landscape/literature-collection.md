# Stoicism: Ancient Foundations to Modern Revival — Literature Collection

> A survey of Stoic philosophy across its 2,300-year history: from the Painted Porch to Silicon Valley, from Chrysippus's logic to cognitive behavioral therapy.

**Compiled:** March 25, 2026
**Scope:** Ancient foundations, Roman Stoicism, core doctrines, modern revival, Stoicism & CBT, critiques & limitations
**Method:** Web research across academic encyclopedias, philosophical analyses, popular treatments, psychological research, critical essays

## 1. Ancient Foundations

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| AF-01 | **Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy: "Stoicism"** | Comprehensive entry covering all three parts of Stoic philosophy (logic, physics, ethics). Zeno founded the school c. 300 BCE at the Stoa Poikile. Chrysippus systematized the doctrine to the point of being called "the second founder." The three-part division was the Stoics' own. | Rev. 2023 |
| AF-02 | **Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy: "Stoicism"** | Detailed treatment of Early, Middle, and Late Stoa. Zeno studied under Cynic Crates and was influenced by Plato's Academy and the Megarian School. Cleanthes succeeded Zeno as scholarch. Only fragments survive from the Early Stoa — complete works only from the Late (Roman) period. | Ongoing |
| AF-03 | **Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy: "Stoic Ethics"** | Virtue as the sole good (monistic theory). Preferred and dispreferred indifferents. The oikeiosis doctrine (self-appropriation). Hierocles' concentric circles of concern. Cosmopolitanism as extension of rational kinship. | Ongoing |
| AF-04 | **Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy: "Ancient Logic"** | Chrysippus developed propositional logic distinct from Aristotle's term logic. Five indemonstrable syllogisms as axioms. Four inference rules (themata). Over 300 books on logic. Achievement not recognized until 20th century propositional calculus. | Rev. 2020 |
| AF-05 | **Wikipedia: "Stoic Logic"** | Chrysippus's propositional logic was the first formal system of propositional logic in Western philosophy. Topics included speech act theory, sentential connectives, modal logic, tense logic, epistemic logic, the Liar paradox, and the Sorites. | Ongoing |
| AF-06 | **Wikipedia: "Stoic Physics"** | Two principles: active (logos/God/Nature) and passive (matter). Pneuma as the vital breath pervading all things in four gradations (hexis, physis, psyche, logos). Deterministic causality but compatibilist free will. Conflagration (ekpyrosis) and eternal recurrence. | Ongoing |
| AF-07 | **Massimo Pigliucci: "From Ancient to New Stoicism: I — Stoic Physics"** (Figs in Winter) | Stoic physics as materialist monism. Everything that exists is corporeal. Only bodies can act or be acted upon. Pneuma gradations explain the unity of inanimate objects, growth of plants, sensation of animals, and reason of humans. Modern update: replace logos with laws of nature. | 2020 |
| AF-08 | **Massimo Pigliucci: "From Ancient to New Stoicism: II — Stoic Logic"** (Figs in Winter) | Stoic logic included epistemology, rhetoric, grammar, and formal logic proper. Phantasia (impression), synkatathesis (assent), katalepsis (cognitive grasp). The Stoic sage never errs in assent — a normative ideal, not a description. | 2020 |
| AF-09 | **The Stoic App Blog: "Chrysippus: The Second Founder Who Systematized Stoicism"** | Chrysippus wrote 705 books (none survive complete). Defended Stoicism against Academic Skeptics. Developed the theory of lekta (sayables). Without Chrysippus, "there would have been no Stoa" (Diogenes Laertius). | 2024 |
| AF-10 | **Parkland College: "Zeno of Citium's Philosophy of Stoicism"** (student thesis) | Zeno's biography: Phoenician merchant from Cyprus, shipwrecked near Athens, studied under Crates the Cynic. Founded school c. 301 BCE. Taught that virtue is the only good and lives according to nature. | 2020 |

## 2. The Roman Stoics

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| RS-01 | **Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy: "Epictetus"** | Born c. 50 CE as a slave in Hierapolis, Phrygia. Studied under Musonius Rufus. Freed after Nero's death (68 CE). Banished from Rome under Domitian (93 CE). Founded school at Nicopolis. Wrote nothing himself — Arrian transcribed the Discourses and compiled the Enchiridion. | Rev. 2023 |
| RS-02 | **Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy: "Marcus Aurelius"** | Emperor 161-180 CE. The Meditations were private spiritual exercises, never intended for publication. Written in Greek during military campaigns. Influenced by Epictetus, whom he cites. Represents the culmination of Roman Stoicism in practice — philosophy as a way of life at the highest level of political power. | Rev. 2022 |
| RS-03 | **Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy: "Epictetus"** | Epictetus was "completely free of eclecticism" and showed closer connection to Chrysippus's original Stoic doctrine than other Late Stoics. The Discourses cover 4 books (of possibly 8 original). The Enchiridion is a practical handbook, not a summary. | Ongoing |
| RS-04 | **Britannica: "Epictetus"** | His master Epaphroditus was Nero's secretary. According to Origen, Epictetus was more popular in his own time than Plato. His philosophy influenced Marcus Aurelius directly. The Enchiridion became a monastic manual in the Middle Ages. | Ongoing |
| RS-05 | **Study.com: "Roman Stoicism — Overview, Beliefs & Virtues"** | Roman Stoicism focused on practical ethics over theoretical physics and logic. Emphasized duty (officium), endurance, inner freedom amid external constraints. Seneca served as tutor and advisor to Nero. Marcus Aurelius was the philosopher-king Plato envisioned. | Ongoing |
| RS-06 | **WCU Thesis: "The Evolution of Stoicism"** | Greek Stoicism: technical treatises, formal logic, addressed to fellow philosophers. Roman Stoicism: epistles, handbooks, moral essays, meditations. Shift from system-building to soul-therapy. Only Roman texts survive complete. | 2019 |
| RS-07 | **Mises Institute: "Wisdom of the Stoics"** | Seneca's Letters to Lucilius: 124 letters covering friendship, grief, anger, the shortness of life, the proper use of time, the consolation of philosophy. Practical moral philosophy in epistolary form. In the 17th century Seneca was the best-known Stoic; his reputation declined in the 19th century. | Ongoing |
| RS-08 | **Gregory Sadler: "Reading Recommendations for Studying Stoicism"** | The "Big Three" (Seneca, Epictetus, Marcus Aurelius) are the entry points for virtually all modern readers. Scholarly reception has shifted: mid-Victorians favored Marcus Aurelius under Matthew Arnold's influence; Epictetus gained ground in the 20th century. | 2024 |

## 3. Core Doctrines

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| CD-01 | **Philosophy Break: "The Dichotomy of Control"** | Epictetus, Enchiridion 1: "Some things are within our power, while others are not." Within our power: opinion, motivation, desire, aversion. Not within our power: body, property, reputation, office. Foundation of Stoic practical ethics. | 2023 |
| CD-02 | **The Living Philosophy: "Stoicism's Dichotomy of Control"** | The dichotomy is not binary acceptance/rejection but a framework for directing effort. Preferred indifferents (health, wealth) are worth pursuing — they just aren't constitutive of the good life. Trichotomy of control (Irvine's modification): things we control, things we partially control, things we don't. | 2023 |
| CD-03 | **Orion Philosophy: "A Guide to the 4 Stoic Virtues"** | The four cardinal virtues: wisdom (sophia/phronesis), courage (andreia), justice (dikaiosyne), temperance (sophrosyne). Virtue is a unified disposition — you can't have one without the others. The virtuous person acts appropriately in all circumstances. | 2024 |
| CD-04 | **Daily Stoic: "The 9 Core Stoic Beliefs"** | Key beliefs: virtue is the highest good; focus on what you can control; treat others well; take the obstacle as the way; be mindful of time; live according to nature; practice negative visualization; memento mori; amor fati. | Ongoing |
| CD-05 | **Routledge Encyclopedia of Philosophy: "Stoicism — The Indifferents"** | Formal classification: good (virtue), bad (vice), indifferent (everything else). Indifferents subdivided: preferred (health, wealth, reputation — have positive "selective value"), dispreferred (disease, poverty, disgrace), strictly indifferent (number of hairs). Selection among indifferents is "appropriate action" (kathekon). | Ongoing |
| CD-06 | **Stoic Ethics and Political Citizenship (College of Stoic Philosophers)** | Stoic cosmopolitanism: we are citizens of the cosmos before any particular city. Hierocles' concentric circles: self, family, extended family, neighbors, fellow citizens, humanity. The task is to "draw the circles toward the center." | 2023 |
| CD-07 | **Massimo Pigliucci: "From Ancient to New Stoicism: III — Stoic Ethics"** (Figs in Winter) | Ethics as the culmination of the Stoic system: physics tells us how the world works, logic gives us tools to reason about it, ethics tells us how to live in it. The sage (sophos) is an ideal — a normative standard, not a realistic expectation. Progress toward virtue (prokope) is the realistic goal. | 2020 |
| CD-08 | **Stoicism University: "Stoicism Ethics: A Guide to Living Virtuously"** | Oikeiosis (self-appropriation/affinity): the natural impulse to care for oneself extends to others through reason. Basis for both virtue ethics and cosmopolitanism. Connects individual psychology to social ethics. | 2024 |

## 4. The Modern Revival

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| MR-01 | **Modern Stoicism Organization** (modernstoicism.com) | Founded 2012. Hosts Stoicon (largest global Stoic gathering), Stoic Week, SMRT (Stoicism Mindfulness & Resilience Training) 4-week course. Team includes academics (Massimo Pigliucci, John Sellars, Christopher Gill) and therapists (Tim LeBon, Donald Robertson). | Ongoing |
| MR-02 | **Ryan Holiday — Wikipedia + Daily Stoic** | Holiday's books have sold over 10 million copies in 40+ languages. The Obstacle Is the Way (2014): 230K+ copies by 2016, adopted by NFL teams (Patriots, Seahawks), Chicago Cubs. The Daily Stoic: 2M+ English copies. #1 NYT bestseller series on Stoic Virtues. Daily Stoic email list reaches millions. | Ongoing |
| MR-03 | **William Irvine: A Guide to the Good Life** (OUP, 2009) | First systematic modern guide to practicing Stoicism. Introduced "negative visualization" as key technique. Proposed trichotomy of control (modifying Epictetus's dichotomy). Best-selling popular introduction. Criticized by scholars for departing from ancient sources (Robertson's review). | 2009 |
| MR-04 | **Massimo Pigliucci: How to Be a Stoic** (2017) + Blog of the APA Interview | Philosophy professor (CUNY) who publicly adopted Stoicism. Blog "How to Be a Stoic" (now "Figs in Winter"). Argues for updating Stoic metaphysics while keeping ethics. Distinguishes academic Stoicism from pop Stoicism. Key advocate for philosophical rigor in the movement. | 2017 |
| MR-05 | **Donald Robertson — Wikipedia + Personal Site** | Scottish CBT therapist. Founded Modern Stoicism organization. The Philosophy of Cognitive-Behavioural Therapy (2010): key academic work linking Stoicism and CBT. How to Think Like a Roman Emperor (2019): #1 philosophy bestseller, translated into 18 languages. President of Plato's Academy Centre in Greece. | Ongoing |
| MR-06 | **Stoic Week Research Results** (Modern Stoicism + Royal Holloway) | Since 2012, ~40,000 participants. Consistent results: 15% reduction in negative emotions, 14% increase in life satisfaction after one week. SABS (Stoic Attitudes and Behaviours Scale): 7 dimensions. High SABS linked to greater resilience, lower anger, higher satisfaction. 94% would recommend Stoicism to others. | 2012-2025 |
| MR-07 | **Quartz: "Silicon Valley tech workers are using an ancient philosophy designed for Greek slaves as a life hack"** | Tim Ferriss promoted Holiday's books. Holiday spoke at Google, chatted with Jack Dorsey, Kevin Rose, Brad Feld. Stoicism as "operating system for high-stress environments." Tech adoption accelerated mainstream interest. | 2017 |
| MR-08 | **Pigliucci: "Silicon Valley Style Stoicism"** (Medium) | Critique: SV Stoicism emphasizes wealth as indifferent but ignores virtue as the only good. Focuses on individual resilience for financial gain, not character development. Ignores community and justice — antithetical to Zeno's cosmopolitanism. | 2019 |
| MR-09 | **Philosophy for Life: "The Big, Messy Tent of Modern Stoicism"** (Jules Evans) | Multiple factions: academic philosophers, CBT therapists, self-help authors, military/sports coaches, Silicon Valley optimizers. Tension between scholarly fidelity and popular accessibility. Question: can Stoicism survive without its metaphysics? | 2015 |
| MR-10 | **Academia.edu: "Stoicism for the 21st Century: How Did We Get There and What to Make of It"** (Pigliucci) | Academic paper tracing the revival from Pierre Hadot's "philosophy as a way of life" through the founding of Modern Stoicism org to the pop-Stoicism explosion. Proposes "Stoicism 2.0" that updates physics and logic while preserving ethics. | 2024 |

## 5. Stoicism & CBT

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| CB-01 | **Donald Robertson: "Stoic Philosophy as a Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy"** (Medium/Personal Site) | Stoicism as "the clearest example of a system of psychotherapy in ancient Greek or Roman philosophy." Ellis and Beck both explicitly acknowledged Stoic origins. Cognitive distancing, decatastrophizing, and value clarification all have Stoic precedents. | 2019 |
| CB-02 | **The Walled Garden: "Albert Ellis: Stoicism as the Root of CBT"** | Ellis read Epictetus, Seneca, and Marcus Aurelius in youth. REBT (1955-57) was the first form of cognitive therapy. Core principle: "It's not things that upset us but our judgements about them" — direct from Epictetus, Enchiridion 5. Ellis called this the "ABC model" (Activating event, Beliefs, Consequences). | 2023 |
| CB-03 | **Jules Evans: "RIP Aaron Beck, Founder of CBT and Reviver of the Stoics' Cognitive Approach"** (Medium) | Beck's original treatment manual for depression: "The philosophical origins of cognitive therapy can be traced back to the Stoic philosophers." Beck's influence from Stoicism was mainly indirect through exposure to Ellis's REBT writings. Cognitive model: automatic thoughts, cognitive distortions, core beliefs. | 2021 |
| CB-04 | **Big Think: "The Ancient Roots of Psychotherapy Matter Now"** | Modern CBT techniques map directly to Stoic practices: cognitive restructuring ↔ Stoic examination of impressions; behavioral activation ↔ Stoic exercises (askesis); Socratic questioning ↔ Stoic dialectic. The connection is not metaphorical but historically direct. | 2023 |
| CB-05 | **PMC (NIH): "The Western Origins of Mindfulness Therapy in Ancient Rome"** | Peer-reviewed article linking Stoic practices (prosoche/attention, melete/meditation, askesis/exercise) to modern mindfulness-based cognitive therapy. Marcus Aurelius's Meditations as a form of journaling therapy. Seneca's evening review as precursor to CBT homework. | 2023 |
| CB-06 | **Springer: "The Lineage of Positive Psychology and CBT: How Stoicism Inspired Modern Psychotherapy"** | Academic paper tracing influence chain: Stoicism → REBT (Ellis, 1955) → CT (Beck, 1960s) → CBT (integration, 1980s) → Third-wave CBT (ACT, DBT, MBCT). Stoic virtues parallel character strengths in positive psychology (Seligman/Peterson). | 2024 |
| CB-07 | **VA Counseling: "Stoicism and CBT: Is Therapy a Philosophical Pursuit?"** | Clinical perspective: both Stoicism and CBT teach that emotional disturbance comes from interpretations, not events. Key difference: CBT is time-limited treatment; Stoicism is a lifelong philosophical practice. CBT borrows techniques; Stoicism provides a comprehensive worldview. | 2024 |
| CB-08 | **Robertson: The Philosophy of Cognitive-Behavioural Therapy** (Karnac, 2010) | The definitive academic work on the Stoicism-CBT connection. Documents 18 specific parallels between Stoic practices and CBT techniques. Argues that CBT practitioners benefit from understanding Stoic philosophy as the "missing foundation" of their practice. | 2010 |
| CB-09 | **Psychology Today: "The Science Behind Stoicism"** | Evidence review: Stoic practices associated with reduced anxiety, depression, and anger; increased life satisfaction and resilience. SABS predicts wellbeing outcomes. Stoic journaling (Marcus Aurelius style) shows therapeutic benefits comparable to standard CBT journaling. | 2025 |

## 6. Critiques & Limitations

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| CL-01 | **Martha Nussbaum: "The Therapy of Desire" + "Upheavals of Thought"** (via Daily Stoic interview + multiple secondary) | Emotions are cognitive judgments (agrees with Stoics) but should NOT be extirpated. Grief, anger, love are forms of moral judgment — ways of valuing what matters. Stoic apatheia leads to "shallow and dehumanizing psychology." Vulnerability is essential to human flourishing. However: Nussbaum respects and uses the Stoic tradition selectively. | 1994/2001 |
| CL-02 | **Nussbaum interview (Philosophy for Life / History of Emotions Blog)** | "My upheavals come from attachment to particular people or politics, and that's the part where I reject Stoicism." Important human goods (love, friendship, political participation, bodily integrity) necessarily expose us to risk and loss. Stoic invulnerability neglects what people reasonably value. | 2012 |
| CL-03 | **America Magazine: "Martha Nussbaum Respects the Cynic-Stoic Tradition — But She's Ready to Correct It"** | Nussbaum's critique of Stoic cosmopolitanism: right idea (universal human dignity) but flawed execution (neglects material aid, institutional reform). Correct the Stoics, don't abandon them. The Stoics themselves defended equality of sexes — feminist critique is more nuanced than simple rejection. | 2019 |
| CL-04 | **New Statesman: "The Problem with Stoicism"** | Stoicism thrived during slavery and women's oppression because it helped people endure being oppressed. Encourages quietism and distraction while upholding the status quo. "There's something self-serving in the wealthiest segment of American society practicing a philosophy that enables detached acceptance of the world's injustices." | 2007 |
| CL-05 | **Philosophy Now: "The Stoic's Lacuna"** | Ancient Stoics were "quite silent about slavery" — no Stoic questioned the institution itself. Advocated treating slaves humanely but accepted the social structure. Tension between cosmopolitan ideals and lived practice. | 2021 |
| CL-06 | **Aeon: "When Stoicism Is a Political Not Just a Personal Virtue"** | Counter-argument: Stoicism has a strong tradition of political engagement. Cato and Cicero stood up to Caesar; Seneca confronted Nero; the Stoic Opposition under the Flavians. Characterizing Stoicism as quietist ignores this history. But the tension remains. | 2019 |
| CL-07 | **Donald Robertson: "Did Stoicism Condemn Slavery?"** | Careful historical analysis. Stoics like Seneca and Chrysippus argued slaves are human beings deserving dignity. Chrysippus may have opposed slavery theoretically. But no Stoic called for abolition. The gap between principle and practice is real. | 2017 |
| CL-08 | **Forward Pathway: "The Modern Revival of Stoicism — Critical Reflections"** | Silicon Valley Stoicism as "naïve Stoic Ideology." Emotional repression marketed as masculine strength. Gender bias: modern pop-Stoicism's audience is overwhelmingly male. "Manosphere" co-optation. Risk of using Stoicism to justify emotional unavailability. | 2024 |
| CL-09 | **American Conservative: "Silicon-Valley Stoicism Can Point to the Real Stuff"** | Sympathetic critique: pop Stoicism is shallow but serves as a gateway to deeper engagement. Most people who start with Ryan Holiday eventually read Epictetus. The danger is stopping at the self-help level. The real Stoicism demands much more than resilience — it demands virtue. | 2019 |
| CL-10 | **Pigliucci: Reply to Nussbaum on Stoicism** (Medium) | Defends the Stoic position on emotions: apatheia is not emotional suppression but freedom from destructive passions. The Stoics recognized positive emotions (eupatheiai): joy, wish, caution. Nussbaum attacks a straw-man version of Stoic psychology. | 2019 |

## 7. Cross-Cutting & Historical

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| XC-01 | **Stoic Simple: "What Influence Did Stoicism Have on Early Christianity?"** | Early Church Fathers (Justin Martyr, Clement of Alexandria, Origen) drew on Stoic concepts. Pauline theology mirrors Stoic natural law (Romans 2:14-15). "Logos" in John 1:1 resonates with Stoic logos. Stoic influence on Christianity often exceeds Platonist influence. | 2024 |
| XC-02 | **Stoic Handbook: "Stoicism and Christianity: Exploring the Logos and Historical Connections"** | Shared concepts: divine providence, moral conscience, universal brotherhood, natural law. Key differences: Stoic materialism vs Christian dualism; Stoic eternal recurrence vs linear time; Stoic sage vs Christian saint. Epictetus's Enchiridion adapted as monastic text. | 2024 |
| XC-03 | **Wikipedia: "Stoicism" — Reception and Influence** | Stoic natural law theory influenced Roman law (Cicero, Justinian's Digest). Renaissance recovery: Justus Lipsius's Neo-Stoicism (1584). Influence on Descartes, Spinoza, Kant. Adam Smith's Theory of Moral Sentiments draws on Stoic sympathy. Tom Wolfe's A Man in Full (1998) features Epictetus. | Ongoing |
