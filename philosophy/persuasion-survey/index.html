SURVEY · PHILOSOPHY OF PERSUASION

The Architecture of Influence

A survey of persuasion from Aristotle to AI — Rhetoric, Exploitation, Defense, Automation, Governance
This survey traces the architecture of human persuasion across 2,350 years, from Aristotle's rhetorical proofs through cognitive psychology to machine-generated influence. It examines how persuasion works, how it fails, how we defend against it, and what happens when machines automate the process. Five deep dives: the grammar of persuasion itself, the dark psychology of exploitation, the counter-technologies of human resilience, the emerging landscape of AI-driven influence, and the governance frameworks emerging to regulate it all.
