# The Architecture of Influence: A Survey of Persuasion from Aristotle to AI

**Proposal v1** — March 24, 2026

---

## Thesis

Persuasion is humanity's oldest technology and AI's newest weapon. This survey traces the full arc — from Aristotle's *Rhetoric* through dark psychology and cognitive bias exploitation, to the emergence of LLM-powered influence systems that have achieved human-level or super-human persuasiveness. We organize this landscape into a five-part taxonomy: **Rhetoric, Exploit, Defend, Automate, Govern** — arguing that the ancient struggle between persuader and defender has entered a fundamentally new phase where the persuader is no longer human.

## Section Breakdown

### Section 1: RHETORIC — The Grammar of Persuasion

**Core argument:** Every act of persuasion, ancient or AI-generated, operates through the same three channels Aristotle identified: credibility (ethos), emotion (pathos), and reasoning (logos). Understanding this grammar is prerequisite to understanding either exploitation or defense.

**Key topics:**
- Aristotle's *Rhetoric* and the three appeals (ethos, pathos, logos)
- Plato vs. Aristotle: rhetoric as flattery vs. rhetoric as art
- Roman rhetoric: Cicero's five canons (inventio, dispositio, elocutio, memoria, actio)
- The Sophists and the amorality of persuasive technique
- Cialdini's six principles as modern rhetorical categories
- Kahneman's System 1/System 2 as the cognitive substrate

**Key literature:**
- Aristotle, *Rhetoric* (c. 350 BCE)
- Plato, *Gorgias* and *Phaedrus*
- Cialdini, *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion* (1984, 7th ed. 2021)
- Kahneman, *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (2011)
- Petty & Cacioppo, Elaboration Likelihood Model (1986)
- Chaiken, Heuristic-Systematic Model (1980)

### Section 2: EXPLOIT — Weaponizing the Grammar

**Core argument:** The same principles that make persuasion effective make manipulation possible. Dark psychology is not a separate domain — it's the deliberate exploitation of cognitive architecture that evolved for cooperation.

**Key topics:**
- The Dark Triad/Tetrad (Narcissism, Machiavellianism, Psychopathy + Sadism)
- Cognitive biases as attack surface: anchoring, framing, availability, sunk cost
- Manipulation tactics: gaslighting, love-bombing, DARVO, coercive control
- Social engineering: pretexting, phishing, authority exploitation
- Cambridge Analytica and large-scale psychological manipulation
- Propaganda techniques: from Bernays to modern information warfare

**Key literature:**
- Paulhus & Williams, "The Dark Triad of personality" (2002)
- Tversky & Kahneman, "Judgment under Uncertainty: Heuristics and Biases" (1974)
- Cialdini, *Influence* (weaponized perspective)
- Bernays, *Propaganda* (1928)
- Stark, *Coercive Control* (2007)
- Hadnagy, *Social Engineering: The Science of Human Hacking* (2018)

### Section 3: DEFEND — Counter-Technologies of the Mind

**Core argument:** Defenses against persuasion have a 2,300-year history, from Stoic *praemeditatio malorum* to modern psychological inoculation. The most effective defenses don't resist persuasion — they inoculate against manipulation by building metacognitive awareness.

**Key topics:**
- Stoicism as original cognitive resilience training
  - Epictetus: "It's not things that disturb us, but our judgments about things"
  - Marcus Aurelius: *Meditations* as anti-manipulation manual
  - Praemeditatio malorum → CBT's cognitive restructuring
- CBT as formalized Stoic defense
  - Aaron Beck's cognitive model
  - REBT and rational-emotive imagery
  - Robertson, *Philosophy of CBT: Stoic Philosophy as Rational & Cognitive Psychotherapy*
- Psychological inoculation theory
  - McGuire (1961): weakened dose of misinformation + refutation → resistance
  - Prebunking at scale: Google/Jigsaw YouTube campaigns
  - Roozenbeek et al. (2022): inoculation on social media (Science Advances)
- Media literacy and critical thinking education
  - Meta-analysis: 54 RCTs, 10,941 participants (Nature Human Behaviour 2025)

**Key literature:**
- Epictetus, *Discourses* and *Enchiridion*
- Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations*
- Robertson, *Philosophy of CBT* (2010, 2nd ed. 2019)
- McGuire, "Resistance to persuasion" (1961)
- Roozenbeek et al., "Psychological inoculation improves resilience" (Science Advances, 2022)
- van der Linden, *Foolproof* (2023)
- Nature Human Behaviour meta-analysis on debiasing education (2025)

### Section 4: AUTOMATE — The Machine Persuader

**Core argument:** LLMs represent a discontinuity in the history of persuasion: for the first time, persuasion can be fully automated, personalized, interactive, and deployed at scale simultaneously. This is not just "faster propaganda" — it's a new category.

**Key topics:**
- LLM persuasion capabilities
  - Salvi et al. (2024): LLMs match or exceed human persuasiveness in RCTs
  - Bai et al. (2025): LLM messages as effective as human-crafted (Nature Comms)
  - Hackenburg et al. (2025): 77,000 participants, conversational AI shifts political attitudes (Science)
  - Post-training boosts persuasiveness 51%, but decreases factual accuracy
  - Meta-analysis: overall effect d = 0.22–0.37 across 17 studies
- Personalization paradox
  - Microtargeting not significantly more effective than generic messages (PNAS)
  - Model size shows diminishing returns for single-message persuasion
  - Interactive dialogue dramatically more persuasive than static messages
- Deepfakes and synthetic media
  - Arup $25M deepfake video call fraud (January 2024)
  - Voice cloning, face-swap technology, synthetic text
- Prompt injection as social engineering
  - Connection to MCP security vulnerabilities (from your agentic LLM survey)
  - AI agents as new attack surface for manipulation

**Key literature:**
- Salvi et al., "On the Conversational Persuasiveness of LLMs" (arXiv, 2024)
- Bai et al., "LLM-generated messages can persuade humans" (Nature Comms, 2025)
- Hackenburg et al., "Levers of political persuasion with conversational AI" (Science, 2025)
- Durmus et al., "Persuasion with LLMs: A Survey" (arXiv, 2024)
- Goldstein et al., "Generative Language Models and Automated Influence Operations" (arXiv, 2024)
- PNAS, "Scaling language model size yields diminishing returns" (2025)
- Nature Scientific Reports meta-analysis on LLM persuasion (2025)

### Section 5: GOVERN — Rules for the Persuasion Machine

**Core argument:** The governance response to automated persuasion is fragmented, reactive, and struggling to keep pace. Three approaches are emerging: transparency mandates (EU AI Act), criminal prohibitions (TAKE IT DOWN Act), and technical defenses (watermarking, detection). None alone is sufficient.

**Key topics:**
- EU AI Act Article 50: transparency obligations (enforceable August 2026)
  - Code of Practice on AI-Generated Content (December 2025 draft)
  - Labeling, watermarking, metadata requirements
- U.S. legislative landscape
  - TAKE IT DOWN Act (Public Law 119-12, May 2025)
  - DEFIANCE Act (Senate, January 2026)
  - 46 states, 169 laws since 2022, 146 bills in 2025 alone
- International approaches
  - China: mandatory labeling of all AI-generated content
  - Japan: criminal penalties for non-consensual AI images
- Technical defenses
  - C2PA content provenance standard
  - AI detection tools (Reality Defender, etc.)
  - Watermarking limitations
- Ethical frameworks
  - When is persuasion legitimate? (health campaigns, education)
  - Consent and disclosure as necessary but insufficient
  - The asymmetry problem: defense is harder than attack

**Key literature:**
- EU AI Act, Article 50 (2024)
- TAKE IT DOWN Act, Public Law 119-12 (2025)
- DEFIANCE Act (2026)
- C2PA Technical Specification
- IAPP, "Artificial illusion: Global governance challenges of deepfake technology"
- Colorado AI Act (enforcement February 2026)
