PHILOSOPHY · READING COMPANION
Sophie's World Reader
An interactive deep-dive companion for Jostein Gaarder's novel — exploring 2,500 years of Western philosophy from the Pre-Socratics through the Middle Ages
Based on Jostein Gaarder's novel (1991) · Pages 3–177

This reader follows Sophie Amundsen through the first half of her philosophical journey — from the mysterious letters in the mailbox through the Pre-Socratics, the Athenian golden age, Plato's Forms, Aristotle's system, the Hellenistic schools, and the Medieval synthesis of faith and reason. Each section maps directly to chapters of the novel, providing both a reading guide and an interactive deep-dive into the philosophy.

About This Project

This reader follows the methodology established in the Agentic LLMs Survey: start from an existing work, read the primary source iteratively, take structured notes, supplement with web research, then synthesize into interactive deep-dives.

The source material was Gaarder's novel (pages 3–177 of the English translation), covering the Garden of Eden through the Middle Ages. Each chapter was extracted via PyMuPDF, read, and annotated with structured notes capturing: philosophers covered, key concepts, questions posed, what Gaarder does well, what he omits, and connections to later philosophy. These notes drove the deep-dive content.
