# Sophie's World Reader — Interactive Philosophy Companion

> A deep-dive reading companion for Jostein Gaarder's *Sophie's World* (pages 1–177), covering the history of Western philosophy from the Pre-Socratics through the Middle Ages.

## What This Is

An interactive HTML reading companion that illuminates the philosophical ideas in *Sophie's World*, with three-panel deep-dive layouts featuring sidebar navigation, concept summaries, and expandable detail panels with SVG diagrams.

## Structure

Each section has:
- **Markdown notes** (`notes/*.md`) — structured analysis per section
- **Deep-dive HTML** (`*.dd.html`) — interactive three-panel layout with SVG diagrams

## Source

*Sophie's World* by Jostein Gaarder (1991), pages 1–177.

This companion covers approximately 2,500 years of Western philosophy as presented through the novel's unique frame: a mysterious philosopher teaching a 14-year-old girl through letters, while a deeper meta-narrative (the Hilde mystery) gradually questions the nature of reality itself.
