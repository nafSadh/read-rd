SYNTHESIS18

What the Evidence Shows

The Core Finding

International climate finance is experiencing a "convergent retreat" — simultaneous contraction across all major channels — masked by headline commitments that are growing on paper. The system rewards accounting over action, lending over granting, and mitigation over adaptation.

The most promising developments are at the periphery: debt-for-climate swaps, the FRLD's governance model, South-South experimentation, and domestic policies creating irreversible clean energy constituencies.

The central question is whether these peripheral innovations can scale fast enough to compensate for the systemic retreat — or whether the gap between what physics demands and what politics delivers will continue to widen.
