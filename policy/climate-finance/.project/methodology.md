# Methodology — Shifting Trends in Climate Finance

## Research Question
How are the geopolitics and public funding mechanisms of international climate finance shifting — who's paying, who's receiving, what's the architecture, and where are the tensions?

## Scope & Dimensions
8 axes covering the full landscape of international climate finance from a geopolitics and public funding lens:

1. **Geopolitics of Climate Finance** — US, China, EU, Global South dynamics
2. **The $100B Pledge → NCQG** — The broken promise and the contested Baku outcome
3. **Multilateral Funds & MDB Reform** — GCF, World Bank, IMF, MDB capital adequacy
4. **Bilateral & Development Finance** — ODA flows, conditionality, debt-for-climate swaps
5. **Loss & Damage Finance** — The new fund, operationalization, liability framing
6. **Carbon Markets & Article 6** — VCM crisis, compliance markets, sovereignty
7. **Domestic Public Spending** — IRA, EU Green Deal, China, India
8. **Adaptation vs. Mitigation Gap** — Persistent funding imbalance, political dynamics

**Deliberately excluded:** Private finance flows (PE/VC, green bonds, corporate pledges) except where they intersect public architecture. Technology transfer. Sector-specific finance (energy, transport). Sub-national climate budgets.

## Source Strategy
- **Primary:** UNFCCC/COP documents, OECD climate finance reports, World Bank/IMF publications, IPCC finance chapters
- **Think tanks:** Brookings, WRI, ODI, E3G, Carbon Brief, Climate Policy Initiative
- **Institutional:** Green Climate Fund reports, Adaptation Fund, Loss & Damage Fund
- **Journalism:** Reuters, Bloomberg Green, Carbon Brief, The Guardian
- **Data:** OECD DAC stats, Climate Finance Tracker
- Web search sweep across all 8 dimensions, followed by targeted gap-filling

## Reading & Synthesis Approach
Discourse survey — what institutions, governments, analysts, and critics are saying about the state of play. Data-grounded where possible (dollar figures, pledge tracking, flow data). Not a technical/academic literature review.
