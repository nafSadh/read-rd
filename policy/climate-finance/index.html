Shifting Trends in Climate Finance

Geopolitics, public funding, and the evolving architecture of international climate finance — who pays, who decides, and what’s changing.

The global climate finance architecture is in flux. The $100 billion pledge was never met on time, the new $300 billion target from COP29 in Baku is already contested, the U.S. is withdrawing again, China refuses to be classified as a donor, and the Global South is leveraging new coalitions. This project surveys 8 dimensions of international climate finance — from geopolitics and the broken pledge through multilateral reform, loss & damage, carbon markets, and the adaptation gap.
