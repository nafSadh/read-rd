The Cosmos: From Quantum Foam to Cosmic Web

A survey of the universe at every scale — dark matter, dark energy, the Fermi paradox, the multiverse, and the frontiers of observational cosmology.

The universe is 13.8 billion years old, 93 billion light-years across, and composed mostly of things we cannot see or explain. This project surveys cosmology across 7 dimensions and 55 sources — from the cosmic distance ladder through the Big Bang and CMB, dark matter evidence and detection, dark energy and the fate of the universe, the Fermi paradox, multiverse theories and the anthropic principle, and the observational frontiers from JWST to DESI to Vera Rubin.

Key Tensions

  • 01 Dark matter identity: Decades of searches have found nothing — WIMPs, axions, and primordial black holes all remain viable but undetected
  • 02 Hubble tension: Local and early-universe measurements of the expansion rate disagree at 5 sigma — systematic error or new physics?
  • 03 Dark energy evolution: DESI data hints that dark energy changes over time, which would overturn the simplest cosmological model
  • 04 Multiverse falsifiability: Is the multiverse a scientific theory or an unfalsifiable philosophical framework?
  • 05 The silence: The Fermi paradox has 75+ proposed solutions but no consensus — is the universe empty, dangerous, or merely large?
  • 06 Inflation’s smoking gun: Cosmic inflation is universally invoked but its direct evidence (B-mode polarization) remains undetected — unfalsifiable?
