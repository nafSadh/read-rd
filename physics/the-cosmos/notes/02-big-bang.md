# S2: The Big Bang & Cosmic Microwave Background

> The first 380,000 years: from quantum fluctuations to the universe's baby photo, and how inflation solved problems we didn't know we had.

**Sources:** 7 (Wikipedia CMB, PDG Review 2024, ESA Planck, NASA WMAP, Symmetry Magazine inflation, Wikipedia inflation, Wang 2024)
**Adequacy:** Strong. CMB physics, inflation theory, and observational constraints well covered.

---

## 1. The Hot Big Bang

The universe began 13.8 billion years ago in a state of extreme density and temperature. The evidence is overwhelming:

1. **Hubble expansion:** Galaxies are receding, and the rate increases with distance.
2. **CMB radiation:** A thermal glow at 2.725 K filling all space -- the cooled remnant of the primordial fireball.
3. **Light element abundances:** The observed ratios of hydrogen (75%), helium (25%), and trace lithium match predictions of nucleosynthesis at T ~ 10^9 K, a few minutes after the Bang.

The Big Bang is not an explosion in space but an expansion of space itself. There is no center and no edge -- every point in the universe was once at the same "place."

## 2. Nucleosynthesis: The First Three Minutes

In the first few minutes, the universe was hot enough for nuclear fusion. Protons and neutrons combined into:
- Hydrogen-1 (protium): ~75% by mass
- Helium-4: ~25% by mass
- Deuterium: ~0.01%
- Helium-3: trace
- Lithium-7: trace

The predicted abundances match observations to high precision -- a powerful confirmation of Big Bang cosmology. However, there is a "lithium problem": observed primordial lithium is 3-5x lower than predicted, which remains unexplained.

## 3. Recombination and the CMB

At 380,000 years (z ~ 1100), the universe cooled to ~3,000 K, allowing protons and electrons to combine into neutral hydrogen. Photons decoupled from matter and streamed freely -- these photons are the CMB we observe today, redshifted from ~3,000 K to 2.725 K by cosmic expansion.

**COBE (1989):** First space-based CMB measurement. Confirmed perfect blackbody spectrum. Detected anisotropies at 1 part in 100,000. George Smoot: "It's like looking at God." Nobel Prize 2006.

**WMAP (2001-2010):** Precision cosmology. Measured power spectrum of anisotropies. Determined universe is flat (Omega_total = 1.0 +/- 0.02). Measured baryon density, dark matter density, dark energy density.

**Planck (2009-2013):** Finest CMB map ever. Six-parameter Lambda-CDM model fits data with extraordinary precision. Age: 13.799 +/- 0.021 Gyr. Composition: 4.9% baryons, 26.8% dark matter, 68.3% dark energy.

## 4. Anisotropies: The Seeds of Everything

The CMB anisotropies -- tiny temperature variations of ~30 microkelvin -- are the seeds from which all cosmic structure grew. The pattern encodes:

- **Acoustic oscillations:** Before decoupling, photons and baryons formed a coupled fluid. Sound waves created peaks and troughs in the power spectrum. The first peak at ~1 degree confirms flat geometry.
- **Baryon density:** Ratio of odd to even peaks.
- **Dark matter density:** Overall envelope of the power spectrum.
- **Spectral index:** Nearly scale-invariant (n_s = 0.965), as inflation predicts.

## 5. Inflation: The First 10^-32 Seconds

Two problems plagued the standard Big Bang:

**Horizon problem:** Opposite sides of the CMB sky (separated by 93 Bly) have the same temperature to 1 part in 100,000. But they were never in causal contact -- how did they "know" to equilibrate?

**Flatness problem:** Why is the universe so precisely flat? Any initial curvature would have been amplified by expansion. The universe appears fine-tuned to Omega = 1.

**Alan Guth's solution (1980):** A brief period of exponential expansion (inflation) from ~10^-36 to ~10^-32 seconds. A patch smaller than a proton inflated to larger than the observable universe, homogenizing temperature and flattening geometry. Quantum fluctuations during inflation were stretched to cosmological scales, becoming the seeds of structure.

## 6. Evidence for Inflation

**Supporting:**
- Nearly scale-invariant power spectrum (n_s = 0.9649 +/- 0.0042, Planck+BICEP/Keck)
- Gaussian perturbations (as predicted by simplest inflation models)
- Flat universe (Omega_k consistent with 0)
- Superhorizon correlations (perturbations larger than the horizon at decoupling)

**Missing:**
- Primordial gravitational waves (B-mode polarization). Tensor-to-scalar ratio r < 0.036 (2021 upper bound). BICEP2's 2014 claim of r ~ 0.2 was retracted (dust contamination).
- Non-Gaussianity: Planck found no significant signal, constraining some inflation models.

**Upcoming:**
- LiteBIRD (JAXA, launch ~2032): designed to measure r down to 0.001.
- CMB-S4 (ground-based): similar sensitivity.

## 7. The Problems with Inflation

Critics (Steinhardt, Turok, Ijjas) argue:
- Inflation requires its own fine-tuning (initial conditions, potential shape).
- "Eternal inflation" is generic -- most inflationary models predict a multiverse, making the theory unfalsifiable.
- No model-independent prediction: hundreds of inflation models exist, most consistent with current data.

Alternatives (bouncing cosmologies, cyclic models, VSL theories) exist but are less developed.

## 8. What Sources Agree On

1. The CMB is real, thermal, and precisely characterized.
2. Lambda-CDM fits the data remarkably well with only 6 parameters.
3. Inflation is the leading explanation for flatness, horizon problem, and seed perturbations.
4. Primordial nucleosynthesis predictions are confirmed for H and He.
5. Next-generation CMB experiments may detect primordial gravitational waves.

## 9. Where Sources Disagree

1. **Inflation's status:** Established paradigm or unfalsifiable framework?
2. **Lithium problem:** Observational error, stellar depletion, or new physics?
3. **Eternal inflation:** Feature (predicts multiverse) or bug (loses predictivity)?
