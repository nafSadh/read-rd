# S8: Economics & Infrastructure

> $500B+ hyperscaler capex. $252B corporate AI investment. AI revenue growing 9× — but the gap between investment and realized returns defines the bubble question.

**Sources:** 5 (Goldman Sachs, Gartner, IBM, LLM pricing, Understanding AI)

## Key Findings
- Goldman: $500B+ hyperscaler capex 2026. Seven biggest tech companies: 30%+ of S&P 500 cap, ~25% of earnings.
- Investment: $252B corporate AI in 2024 (Stanford, up 26% YoY). US: $109B. GenAI specifically: $33.9B (+18.7%).
- Revenue: Epoch reports AI company revenues grew 9× in 2023-2024. OpenAI targeting $15B in 2026. Anthropic $4.7B→$7B ARR.
- Pricing: GPT-4-level: ~$30→<$1/M tokens. ~10×/year deflation.
- Infrastructure: gigawatt-scale clusters operational 2026. $58B productivity software market disrupted.
- Bubble debate: S&P Global — 42% of companies abandoned most AI initiatives in 2024. But revenue is real and growing.

## Agreements
1. Investment is massive and accelerating. 2. Revenue is real but concentrated. 3. Cost deflation is enabling new use cases. 4. Power is the binding constraint.

## Disagreements
1. Is $500B capex rational or a bubble? 2. When does revenue match investment? 3. Will the productivity software disruption be as large as Gartner predicts ($58B)?
