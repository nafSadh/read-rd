# S6: Public Perception & Trust

> 66% use AI regularly. Only 46% willing to trust it. 56% report making mistakes due to AI. The trust-use gap defines how the public relates to AI in 2026.

**Sources:** 5 (Pew Research, KPMG/Melbourne 48K study, Ipsos AI Monitor, U of Queensland, Germany study)

## Key Findings
- KPMG (48K people, 47 countries): 66% use AI regularly, 83% believe in wide benefits, only 46% willing to trust. 66% don't evaluate AI accuracy. 56% made mistakes due to AI.
- Ipsos: 52% excited vs 53% nervous (near-split). Excitement correlates with economic optimism (Thailand, Indonesia highest; Canada, Netherlands lowest).
- Trust increased since 2020 in AU/UK/US/CA/DE — but NO change in perceived adequacy of regulations.
- Familiarity drives trust: more experience → more trust (Germany study). The trust-use gap may close from the bottom up.
- Stanford AI Index: countries with HIGHEST AI investment are also the MOST skeptical.

## Agreements
1. People use AI far more than they trust it. 2. Excitement and anxiety coexist — not opposite poles. 3. Economic context shapes perception more than technology quality. 4. Regulation perceived as inadequate everywhere.

## Disagreements
1. Is the trust gap closing? Queensland says trust increased. KPMG says 56% make mistakes. Both can be true.
2. Will familiarity resolve distrust? Germany study says yes. Counter: familiarity with errors could deepen distrust.
