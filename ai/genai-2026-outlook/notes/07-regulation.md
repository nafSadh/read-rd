# S7: Regulation, Governance & Sovereignty

> The EU has a comprehensive framework. The US has a patchwork. The rest of the world is watching. Enforcement begins in 2026 but readiness is low.

**Sources:** 6 (MIT Tech Review, Gartner, Stanford HAI, KPMG, OneTrust, Lexology/Wilson Sonsini)

## Key Findings
- EU AI Act: phased implementation. Aug 2026: high-risk AI systems + transparency (Article 50) become enforceable. Penalties: €35M or 7% turnover. EC considering 1-year delay for high-risk systems (industry pressure).
- US: no federal AI law. Trump Dec 2025 EO signals federal consolidation but doesn't preempt states. 45+ states with AI bills. Colorado AI Act (June 2026), California Transparency Act (Jan 2026). DOJ may challenge "onerous" state laws.
- Legal frontier: liability for chatbot harm (teen suicide case → OpenAI trial Nov 2026), defamation by AI, "death by AI" claims >2,000 by end 2026 (Gartner).
- AI sovereignty: 35% of countries locked into region-specific platforms by 2027 (Gartner).
- 70% of 48K people want AI regulation (KPMG). But no improvement in perceived adequacy since 2020.

## Agreements
1. Regulation is accelerating globally. 2. EU leads comprehensiveness; US leads fragmentation. 3. Sovereignty is rising. 4. Enforcement will test frameworks.

## Disagreements
1. Will EU AI Act be a global standard (like GDPR) or a competitive burden? 2. Can the US patchwork cohere into anything workable?
