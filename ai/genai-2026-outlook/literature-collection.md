## 3. Employment & Labor Market

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| E-01 | **Goldman Sachs Research (Briggs & Dong)** | 2.5% of US employment at risk if current AI use cases expanded. 6-7% at risk with wide AI adoption. Unemployment +0.5pp during transition. **Young tech workers disproportionately affected**: 20-30 year olds in tech-exposed occupations: unemployment up ~3pp since start of 2025. Only 9.3% of companies report using GenAI. Historically, tech disruption effects are temporary (2 years). | Aug 2025 |
| E-02 | **Yale Budget Lab** | **"No discernible disruption" to broader labor market 33 months after ChatGPT.** No clear growth in AI exposure among unemployed. Historical precedent: computers took a decade to transform workflows. | 2026 |
| E-03 | **Dallas Fed (Economic Letter)** | AI simultaneously aiding AND replacing workers. Codified knowledge (book learning) automatable, tacit knowledge (experience) complemented. Entry-level workers substituted, experienced workers augmented. **Wages rising in AI-exposed occupations with high experience premiums.** Career ladder model broken — entry-level development becoming "cost-ineffective." | Feb 2026 |
| E-04 | **Brookings Institution** | 37.1M highly AI-exposed workers. Of those, **70% (26.5M) have high adaptive capacity** (high pay, financial buffers, diverse skills). Most vulnerable: 6.1M clerical/admin workers with LOW adaptive capacity. Office clerks (2.5M), secretaries (1.7M), receptionists (965K). | Feb 2026 |
| E-05 | **BLS Employment Projections** | AI approached same as other technologies. Displacement "tends to take longer than technologists expect." Many affected occupations still see employment growth historically. Methods not designed to capture "extremely rapid technological change." | 2025 |
| E-06 | **TechCrunch: "Investors predict AI coming for labor in 2026"** | MIT: 11.7% of jobs already automatable. VCs predict 2026 budget shifts from labor to AI. "AI will become the scapegoat for executives looking to cover past mistakes." Agents shifting from productivity tools to work automation. | Dec 2025 |
| E-07 | **World Economic Forum** | 85-92M jobs displaced globally by 2030. 97-170M new roles created (net positive). 59% of workers need reskilling by 2030. 41% of employers foresee workforce reductions where AI handles tasks. | 2025 |
| E-08 | **National University compilation** | 40% of employers expect to reduce workforce. 14% of workers say they've already been displaced. 79% of employed women in jobs at high automation risk (vs 58% men). Workers 18-24 are 129% more likely to worry about AI job loss. 49% of Gen Z believe AI reduced value of college education. | 2025-2026 |

## 4. Public Perception & Trust

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| P-01 | **Pew Research Center: "Key findings about Americans and AI"** | 13 findings across 5 years of surveys. Public "cautious but somewhat open." AI part of everyday life for many Americans — work, school, healthcare. | Mar 2026 |
| P-02 | **KPMG/University of Melbourne Global Study** | **48,000 people across 47 countries.** 66% use AI regularly. 83% believe AI will deliver wide benefits. **Only 46% willing to trust AI systems.** 70% believe national/international regulation needed. 66% rely on AI output without evaluating accuracy. **56% report making mistakes due to AI.** | May 2025 |
| P-03 | **Ipsos AI Monitor 2025** (30 countries) | 52% excited about AI products vs 53% nervous. **54% trust AI not to discriminate more than humans (45%).** 53% say AI has already changed their lives. 67% expect AI to change lives in next 3-5 years. Excitement correlated with economic optimism (Thailand, Indonesia, Malaysia highest). | Aug 2025 |
| P-04 | **University of Queensland: Trust in AI** | 17-country study. Trust increased since 2020 in AU/UK/US/CA/DE. But NO change in perceived adequacy of regulations. No change in confidence in entities to govern AI. Fear and worry dominant in Western countries. Only 42% of Gen X/Millennials trust AI in Australia (vs 25% older). | Jul 2025 |
| P-05 | **Germany study (ScienceDirect)** | Perceived value primarily shaped by perceived benefits (not risks). Risk plays significant but secondary role. Individual differences (age, gender, familiarity) influence perceptions. Greater familiarity → more trust. | Aug 2025 |
