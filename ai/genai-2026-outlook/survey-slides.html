The Central Paradox

  • 88% deploy AI somewhere (PwC)
  • 78% use in at least one function (McKinsey)
  • But only 39% report EBIT impact
  • Only 12% achieved both revenue + cost gains
  • 42% of initiatives abandoned (S&P Global)

The technology has never been better. The gap between capability and value has never been wider.

S1

LLMs & Foundation Models

8 sources · Commoditization, cost collapse, compute ceiling

Model Commoditization

  • SWE-bench: 4% → 72% in one year
  • Cost: 280× inference deflation in 18 months
  • Top-10 gap: narrowed to 5.4% on Chatbot Arena
  • Efficiency: NVIDIA argues 7B models are 10-30× more efficient for most tasks
  • Open-source: Qwen 8.85M downloads — Chinese models closing the gap

The model itself is becoming the least interesting part of the stack.

S3

AI Agents

10 sources · Hype, production reality, integration bottleneck

Agent Production Reality

  • 40% of projects canceled by 2027 (Gartner)
  • ≤10 steps before human needed in 68% of cases
  • 85% custom-built (reject generic frameworks)
  • 74% evaluated by humans, not benchmarks
  • 1000+ MCP servers, but <4% RSA focus = opportunity

The failure mode is integration, not the LLM. Brittle connectors and legacy systems kill projects.

S4

Workplace Adoption

10 sources · The adoption-value gap

The Adoption-Value Gap

  • McKinsey: 78% use AI → only 39% EBIT impact
  • Deloitte: 66% productivity → only 20% revenue
  • PwC: 88% deploy → only 12% both gains
  • Coding: strongest use case (+67% PRs/day)
  • Skills: #1 barrier (Deloitte, 3,235 leaders)

Leadership commitment (3× differentiator) matters more than technology choice.

S5

Employment & Labor

8 sources · Career ladder, vulnerability, no broad disruption yet

The Layered Story

  • Surface: "No discernible disruption" 33 months post-ChatGPT (Yale)
  • Undertow: Young tech workers unemployment +3pp (Goldman)
  • Structure: Career ladder breaking — codified knowledge substituted, tacit augmented (Dallas Fed)
  • Vulnerable: 6.1M clerical workers with low adaptive capacity (Brookings)
  • Gender: 79% of employed women in high-risk jobs vs 58% men

Trust, Regulation, Economics

Public perception · EU vs US · The bubble question

Three More Dimensions

  • Trust: 66% use AI, 46% trust it, 56% made mistakes (KPMG, 48K people)
  • EU: AI Act core enforcement August 2026. Penalties: €35M / 7% turnover
  • US: No federal law. 45+ state patchwork. Trump EO signals preemption
  • Capex: $500B+ hyperscaler investment. 9× AI company revenue growth
  • Bubble? 42% project abandonment AND $15B OpenAI revenue target — both true
T1-T6

Six Tensions

Where credible sources reach opposite conclusions

The Six Contradictions

  • T1 Adoption vs. value: 88% deploy, 12% get both gains
  • T2 No disruption vs. undertow: Aggregate fine, young tech workers hurting
  • T3 Use vs. trust: 66% use, 46% trust — unprecedented gap
  • T4 Scale vs. efficiency: 9 GW clusters vs 7B models that are 10-30× cheaper
  • T5 Bubble vs. boom: Both sides cite valid evidence simultaneously
  • T6 Autonomy timelines: 18 months vs 5 years vs 10 years — 6× spread

These tensions, not consensus points, reveal the true state of the field.

Synthesis

  • GenAI 2026 is in an installation phase — massive capital deployment, real capability, enterprise returns concentrated in a minority
  • The technology-adoption question is answered: everyone has AI
  • The organizational-transformation question is wide open
  • 78% adopted minus 12% full value = 66% of organizations in limbo

AI is simultaneously underhyped (capabilities are extraordinary) and overhyped (enterprise returns are modest). Both are true.
