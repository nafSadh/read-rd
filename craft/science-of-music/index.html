The Science of Music: Sound, Brain, and Emotion

From the physics of vibrating strings through harmony, rhythm, emotion, memory, cultural variation, and the future of music — what science reveals about humanity’s universal art form.

Music is simultaneously a physical phenomenon, a cognitive process, and a cultural practice. This project surveys the science of music across 7 planned dimensions and 50+ sources — from acoustics and the harmonic series through consonance perception, rhythm and entrainment, how music triggers emotion, music and memory, cross-cultural universals and differences, and the technological future of music creation.

Key Tensions

  • 01 Nature vs. culture: Some musical features (octave equivalence, beat perception) appear universal; others (scales, harmony rules) are culturally constructed
  • 02 Consonance theories: Helmholtz’s beating explanation vs. frequency-ratio simplicity vs. learned familiarity — no consensus after 150 years
  • 03 Music and emotion: Does music genuinely induce emotions or merely represent them? The felt vs. perceived distinction remains debated
  • 04 AI composition: AI can generate convincing music, but whether this constitutes creativity or sophisticated pattern matching is unresolved
