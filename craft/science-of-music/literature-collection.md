# Literature Collection — The Science of Music

An interdisciplinary deep-dive into why music works the way it does: from acoustics to emotion to culture.

---

## S1: The Physics of Sound (8 sources)

1. **HyperPhysics — Fourier Analysis and Synthesis** (Georgia State University)
   - URL: http://hyperphysics.phy-astr.gsu.edu/hbase/Audio/fourier.html
   - Key data: Fourier decomposition of complex waveforms into sine components; relationship between harmonic content and timbre
   - Reference acoustics textbook resource used in university physics courses

2. **Joseph Romano — PHYS 1406: Physics of Sound & Music** (Lecture Notes, Fall 2022)
   - URL: https://josephromano.github.io/PHYS1406/notes/notes.pdf
   - Key data: Complete undergraduate treatment of frequency, amplitude, wavelength, speed of sound (343 m/s at 20C), standing waves, resonance, overtone series
   - Covers both the physics and perception sides

3. **Sabine's Formula & The Birth of Modern Architectural Acoustics** (Thermaxx Jackets)
   - URL: https://www.thermaxxjackets.com/news/sabine-modern-architectural/
   - Key data: Wallace Clement Sabine derived reverberation time formula (1898); RT60 = 0.161 V/A; optimal concert hall RT 2.0-2.25s; lecture hall ~1.0s

4. **Optimizing Acoustic Design for Dual-Function Concert and Speech Halls** (Scientific Reports, 2025)
   - URL: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-025-96139-8
   - Key data: Modern computational approaches to Sabine's equation; absorption coefficients 0-1 range; computer modeling of hall acoustics

5. **The Applications of Fourier Series Harmonics in Musical Tones** (ResearchGate, 2024)
   - URL: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/386131153
   - Key data: FFT analysis of instrument timbres; real-time frequency band adjustment; harmonic vs inharmonic partials

6. **The Historical Connection of Fourier Analysis to Music** (University of Montana)
   - URL: https://scholarworks.umt.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1389&context=tme
   - Key data: Historical link between Fourier's mathematical work and acoustic analysis; from Euler to modern DSP

7. **Harmonic Series (Music)** (Wikipedia)
   - URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harmonic_series_(music)
   - Key data: Sequence of frequencies f, 2f, 3f, 4f...; relationship to instrument design; natural harmonics on strings/brass

8. **Overtones in Physics** (Vedantu)
   - URL: https://www.vedantu.com/physics/overtones
   - Key data: Distinction between harmonics and overtones; open vs closed tube harmonics; node/antinode patterns

## S2: Harmony & the Math of Consonance (8 sources)

1. **Pythagorean Tuning** (Wikipedia)
   - URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pythagorean_tuning
   - Key data: Tuning based on pure 3:2 fifths; Pythagorean comma (23.46 cents); wolf fifth problem

2. **Just Intonation** (Wikipedia)
   - URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Just_intonation
   - Key data: All intervals as whole-number ratios; pure consonance vs limited modulation; 5-limit vs 7-limit systems

3. **Musical Temperament** (Wikipedia)
   - URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Musical_temperament
   - Key data: Overview of meantone, well temperament, equal temperament; historical evolution; compromise between purity and flexibility

4. **Pythagorean Tuning** (John Carlos Baez, Azimuth, 2023)
   - URL: https://johncarlosbaez.wordpress.com/2023/10/07/pythagorean-tuning/
   - Key data: Mathematical derivation of the Pythagorean comma; 3^12 vs 2^19; group theory perspective on tuning

5. **The Pythagorean Musical Scale: The Math Behind Its Importance and Imperfection** (Vocal Media)
   - URL: https://vocal.media/fyi/the-pythagorean-musical-scale-the-math-behind-its-importance-and-imperfection
   - Key data: Blacksmith's hammer myth; frequency ratios 2:1 (octave), 3:2 (fifth), 4:3 (fourth)

6. **The Well-Tempered Clavier** (Wikipedia)
   - URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Well-Tempered_Clavier
   - Key data: Bach's WTC as demonstration of all 24 keys; "well-tempered" vs "equal-tempered" distinction; political statement about tuning freedom

7. **Exploring the Many Tunings of Balinese Gamelan** (Computer Music Journal, MIT Press, 2024)
   - URL: https://direct.mit.edu/comj/article/47/2/21/124238
   - Key data: Gamelan slendro (5-note near-equidistant) and pelog (7-note); intentional beating from paired instruments; no pure octave concept

8. **Crystals of Sound: Applying Physics of Phase Transitions to Musical Intonation** (Empirical Musicology Review)
   - URL: https://emusicology.org/article/id/4597/
   - Key data: Computational approaches to consonance/dissonance; Helmholtz beating frequency theory; Plomp-Levelt critical bandwidth model

## S3: Rhythm, Time & the Brain (7 sources)

1. **Specific Contributions of Basal Ganglia and Cerebellum to Neural Tracking of Rhythm** (Grahn et al., Cortex, 2017)
   - URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28910668/
   - Key data: Basal ganglia for beat finding/internal rhythm generation; cerebellum for temporally precise encoding; double dissociation in patient studies

2. **Human Brain Basis of Musical Rhythm Perception** (Grahn & Rowe, Brain Connectivity, 2013)
   - URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4101486/
   - Key data: Supplementary motor area (SMA) active for complex rhythms; distinct substrates for meter, tempo, pattern

3. **Identifying a Brain Network for Musical Rhythm** (Neuroimaging Meta-analysis, 2022)
   - URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9195154/
   - Key data: Meta-analysis identifying rhythm network: SMA, premotor cortex, basal ganglia, cerebellum, auditory cortex

4. **From Sound to Movement: Mapping Neural Mechanisms of Auditory-Motor Entrainment** (Brain Sciences, 2024)
   - URL: https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3425/14/11/1063
   - Key data: Neural oscillation entrainment to rhythmic stimuli; predictive timing mechanisms; audiomotor coupling

5. **Neural Entrainment to Polyrhythms: Musicians vs Non-musicians** (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2017)
   - URL: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnins.2017.00208
   - Key data: Musicians show stronger neural entrainment to polyrhythmic patterns; training-dependent plasticity

6. **Rhythm in Sub-Saharan Africa** (Wikipedia)
   - URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhythm_in_Sub-Saharan_Africa
   - Key data: 3:2 cross-rhythm as foundation of West African polyrhythm; divisive vs additive rhythm; clave pattern origins

7. **Braiding Rhythms: Bell Patterns in West African and Afro-Caribbean Music** (Smithsonian Folkways)
   - URL: https://folkways.si.edu/braiding-rhythms-the-role-of-bell-patterns-in-west-african-and-afro-caribbean-music
   - Key data: Timeline/bell patterns as organizing principle; 12-pulse and 16-pulse cycles; structural role of rhythmic asymmetry

## S4: The Neuroscience of Musical Emotion (8 sources)

1. **From Everyday Emotions to Aesthetic Emotions: Toward a Unified Theory of Musical Emotions** (Juslin, Physics of Life Reviews, 2013)
   - URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23769678/
   - Key data: BRECVEMA framework — 8 mechanisms for music-induced emotion; from brainstem reflexes to aesthetic judgment

2. **Emotional Responses to Music: The Need to Consider Underlying Mechanisms** (Juslin & Vastfjall, BBS, 2008)
   - URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18826699/
   - Key data: Original 6-mechanism model (later expanded to 8); critique of treating "emotion" as monolithic

3. **Anatomically Distinct Dopamine Release During Anticipation and Experience of Peak Emotion to Music** (Salimpoor et al., Nature Neuroscience, 2011)
   - URL: https://www.nature.com/articles/nn.2726
   - Key data: Caudate active during anticipation; nucleus accumbens during peak pleasure; PET + fMRI combined; "chills" as marker of peak response

4. **Dopamine Modulates the Reward Experiences Elicited by Music** (Ferreri et al., PNAS, 2019)
   - URL: https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1811878116
   - Key data: Causal evidence: levodopa enhanced musical pleasure, risperidone reduced it; first pharmacological manipulation study

5. **Why Is Sad Music Pleasurable? A Possible Role for Prolactin** (Huron, Musicae Scientiae, 2011)
   - URL: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1029864911401171
   - Key data: Prolactin hypothesis — sad music triggers consolation hormone; evolutionary mismatch between stimulus and response

6. **Enjoying Sad Music: A Test of the Prolactin Theory** (Ladinig et al., Musicae Scientiae, 2021)
   - URL: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1029864919890900
   - Key data: Empirical test found NO significant prolactin increase from sad music; theory not supported by evidence

7. **Uncertainty and Surprise Jointly Predict Musical Pleasure and Amygdala Activity** (Cheung et al., Current Biology, 2019)
   - URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0960982219312588
   - Key data: Interaction of uncertainty + surprise predicts pleasure; amygdala and hippocampus reflect expectation dynamics; nucleus accumbens reflects uncertainty

8. **The BRECVEMA Model and Theories of Embodied Cognition** (Jan, PhD Thesis, Glasgow, 2024)
   - URL: https://theses.gla.ac.uk/84234/
   - Key data: Practice-based test of BRECVEMA; experimental music theatre composition portfolio; embodied cognition extension

## S5: Music & Memory (7 sources)

1. **Involuntary Musical Imagery as a Component of Ordinary Music Cognition** (Liikkanen & Jakubowski, Psychonomic Bulletin & Review, 2020)
   - URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7704448/
   - Key data: INMI (earworms) in 90%+ of population; correlation with musical engagement; auditory cortex activation mirrors real listening

2. **Tunes Stuck in Your Brain: INMI Frequency Correlates with Cortical Structure** (ScienceDirect, 2015)
   - URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1053810015000975
   - Key data: Individual differences in earworm frequency correlate with cortical thickness in auditory and prefrontal regions

3. **Earworms: The Soundtrack of a Wandering Mind** (Emory Arts on the Brain, 2025)
   - URL: https://scholarblogs.emory.edu/artsbrain/2025/04/25/earworms-the-soundtrack-of-a-wandering-mind/
   - Key data: Phonological loop and auditory cortex activation; brain can't distinguish imagined from real sound

4. **Music, Emotion, and Cognition After Brain Damage** (Sarkamo et al., Cortex, 2014)
   - URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24794920/
   - Key data: Preserved musical memory in Alzheimer's; procedural memory pathways resist neurodegeneration; SMA relatively spared in early/mid Alzheimer's; music therapy reduces agitation

5. **Music-Evoked Autobiographical Memories: The Reminiscence Bump** (Schulkind et al., Memory & Cognition, 1999)
   - URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10546197/
   - Key data: Music from ages 15-25 disproportionately recalled; overlaps with identity formation period; hormonal and neuroplasticity explanations; cross-cultural consistency

6. **Memory for Music: Effect of Melody on Recall of Text** (Wallace, Journal of Experimental Psychology: Learning, Memory, and Cognition, 1994)
   - URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8151279/
   - Key data: Melody serves as retrieval scaffold for verbal information; sung material improves word recall; temporal structure constrains search space; foundational study in musical mnemonics

7. **Involuntary and Voluntary Recall of Musical Memories** (Memory & Cognition, Springer, 2018)
   - URL: https://link.springer.com/article/10.3758/s13421-018-0792-x
   - Key data: Temporal accuracy differences between voluntary and involuntary musical memory recall; emotional response patterns

## S6: Music Across Cultures (8 sources)

1. **Universality in Song** (Mehr & Singh, 2018; Mehr et al., Science, 2019)
   - URL: https://www.parsingscience.org/2018/02/20/mehr-and-singh/
   - Key data: Natural History of Song project; every documented culture has music; song types (lullabies, dance, healing) share acoustic features cross-culturally

2. **Infants Relax in Response to Unfamiliar Foreign Lullabies** (Mehr et al., Nature Human Behaviour, 2020)
   - URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33077883/
   - Key data: N=144 infants; relaxation response to foreign lullabies vs non-lullabies; heart rate + pupillometry + EDA; consistent across first year of life

3. **Lullabies and Universality: An Ethnographic Review** (Aubinet, Music Perception, 2024)
   - URL: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/10693971241272406
   - Key data: Systematic ethnographic review of lullaby universality; shared features: descending contour, narrow pitch range, repetitive structure, slow tempo

4. **Music, Language, and the Brain** (Patel, Oxford University Press, 2008)
   - URL: https://global.oup.com/academic/product/music-language-and-the-brain-9780195123753
   - Key data: SSIRH hypothesis — shared neural resources for syntactic processing in music and language; challenges Pinker's "cheesecake" position

5. **Origins of Music in Credible Signaling** (Mehr & Krasnow, BBS, 2021)
   - URL: https://mehr.nz/pdf/2021_MehrEtAl_BBS-targetArticle.pdf
   - Key data: Music evolved for credible signaling of coalition strength and parental attention; not Pinker's byproduct nor purely sexual selection

6. **Is Music Simply "Auditory Cheesecake"?** (BYU Humanities Center)
   - URL: https://humanitiescenter.byu.edu/is-music-simply-auditory-cheesecake/
   - Key data: Pinker (1997): music as pleasant byproduct of language evolution; no survival advantage; counter-evidence from universality and ancient instruments (40,000+ year bone flutes)

7. **A Comparison of Different Musical Modes Used in Different Parts of the World** (Global Music Theory)
   - URL: https://globalmusictheory.com/a-comparison-of-different-musical-modes-used-in-different-parts-of-the-world/
   - Key data: Indian ragas (time/season/emotion mapping, 22 shruti microtones), Arabic maqam (quarter tones), Chinese pentatonic (Gong scale, 24+ centuries old)

8. **Eastern Scales and Modes: A Journey into Timeless Melodies** (Barbad Piano)
   - URL: https://barbadpiano.ae/blog/self-improvement/eastern/
   - Key data: Comparative treatment of raga, maqam, and East Asian pentatonic systems; microtonal intervals; modal emotion mapping

## Cross-Cutting Tensions

1. **Nature vs. Nurture in Consonance** — Physics (simple ratios) explains some preference for consonance, but Javanese gamelan deliberately uses intervals Western theory calls "dissonant." How much is wired, how much learned?

2. **Pinker vs. Patel/Mehr** — Is music evolutionary adaptation or byproduct? Pinker says cheesecake; Patel says shared neural resources with language suggest deeper roots; Mehr says credible signaling.

3. **The Prolactin Paradox** — Huron's elegant theory (2011) that prolactin explains sad-music pleasure was not supported by empirical test (Ladinig 2021). Mechanism still unknown.

4. **AI Democratization vs. Homogenization** — Suno/Udio give everyone music creation tools, but outputs cluster around Western pop conventions. Spotify's algorithm further narrows discovery.

5. **Western Research Bias** — Most neuroscience uses WEIRD (Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, Democratic) populations and Western tonal stimuli. Findings about "universal" brain responses may not generalize.
