# Methodology — Constructed Languages Survey

## Research Question

What can a comparative survey of constructed languages — from the first known conlang in the 1150s to programming languages and fictional alien writing systems — reveal about the nature of language itself?

## Scope

Six dimensions, chosen to span the full range of conlang activity:

1. **Historical IALs** — the utopian tradition (Lingua Ignota through Esperanto)
2. **Tolkien** — the artistic tradition at its deepest
3. **Media conlangs** — the entertainment tradition (Klingon, Dothraki, Na'vi)
4. **Linguistic relativity** — the philosophical tradition (Heptapod B, Sapir-Whorf)
5. **Sign language emergence** — the natural tradition (NSL, ABSL)
6. **Programming languages** — the computational tradition (APL, Brainfuck, esolangs)

## Method

**Literature collection:** Web research across academic sources, encyclopedia references, linguistic databases, film analysis, cognitive science research, and computer science history. 55 sources total. Each section assessed for adequacy before building.

**Source types:**
- Wikipedia articles for factual grounding and cross-referencing
- Academic papers and university publications (Emory, McGill, Columbia, Haifa)
- Tolkien Estate scholarship (Hostetter, Smith)
- Interview sources (Peterson, Frommer, Okrand)
- Organization documentation (KLI, LCS, Esolang Wiki)
- Cognitive science research (Boroditsky, Winawer, Senghas)
- Primary documents (Dijkstra EWD 667)

**Deep-dive construction:** Each section built from markdown notes into interactive HTML deep-dives using the reading-assist template (three-panel: sidebar nav, summary center, detail right). 6-8 subsections per deep-dive, with SVG diagrams.

## What This Survey Is Not

- It is not a linguistics textbook. It covers conlangs, not general linguistics.
- It is not comprehensive. There are thousands of constructed languages; this covers the most significant or illustrative examples.
- It is not technical. It does not provide grammars, lexicons, or detailed phonological analyses. Those are available in the referenced sources.
- It is not prescriptive. It does not argue that any conlang tradition is superior to another.

## Limitations

1. **Source bias toward English-language accounts.** Non-English conlang traditions are underrepresented.
2. **Wikipedia reliance for factual grounding.** Mitigated by cross-referencing with academic sources.
3. **Recency bias.** Media conlangs (post-1984) receive more detailed coverage than historical conlangs because more sources are available.
4. **The programming language parallel is novel.** Few sources directly compare conlangs and programming languages. The connections drawn in Section 6 are original analysis rather than established scholarship.

## Key Findings

See `notes/00-synthesis.md` for the full synthesis. The central finding: language invention is a single impulse expressed through wildly different traditions, and the tensions between those traditions (art vs. utility, creator vs. community, strong vs. weak Whorf) reveal fundamental truths about what language is and what it's for.
