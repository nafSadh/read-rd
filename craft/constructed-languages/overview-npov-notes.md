# NPOV Audit Notes — overview.dd.html

Reviewed and edited 2026-03-27. File was flagged for Antigravity review but the automated pass returned a 503 error; fixes applied manually to match the NPOV standards used across the rest of the repo.

## Changes Made

### 1. Authorial opinion presented as fact

| Location | Original | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Summary §01 | "The most interesting findings are not within any single tradition…" | → "The most significant cross-cutting findings emerge not within any single tradition…" |
| Summary §04 | "The most valuable findings in any comparative survey are the tensions…" | → "Comparative surveys often yield their sharpest insights through tensions…" |
| Detail §04 | "The most valuable findings in any survey are the contradictions." | → "Contradictions between traditions are analytically productive: when different traditions…" |

### 2. Colloquial / snarky framing

| Location | Original | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Summary §05 | "The lesson: the same activity…produces wildly different results…" | Removed "The lesson:" framing; reworded as neutral observation |
| Detail §03 | "Brainfuck is a terrible programming language and a brilliant piece of conceptual art." | → "Brainfuck is impractical as a working programming language, yet functions as a coherent piece of conceptual art." |
| Detail §08 | "Regex is the Volapuk of programming: universally used and universally hated." | → "Regular expressions are widely deployed yet frequently described as opaque and error-prone — a pattern that parallels…" |

### 3. Unsupported superlatives

| Location | Original | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Summary §03 (feat-grid) | "Formal constructed languages that are the most successful conlangs ever." | → "…that, by speaker count and economic reach, have achieved the widest adoption of any conlang family." (grounds the claim in measurable dimensions) |
| Summary §08 bullet | "Programming languages are the most successful conlangs: By any measure…" | → "Programming languages are the highest-adoption conlangs: By speaker count, utility, and economic impact…" |
| Detail §08 finding 5 | Same as above; parallel fix applied | Same treatment |
| Timeline 1910s | "he builds the deepest conlang family ever created." | → "he develops a conlang family distinguished by its historical depth, sound changes, and internal mythology." |

### 4. Overconfident causal claims

| Location | Original | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Timeline 1980s | "the strongest evidence for innate language faculty." | → "widely cited as compelling evidence for an innate language faculty." (hedges unattributed superlative) |
| Detail §06 NSL paragraph | "This is the language instinct in action — and it proves that the most important ingredient in a language is a community that needs one." | → Removed "proves" and declarative framing; replaced with "The case is frequently cited as evidence of an innate language faculty, and suggests that community need may be a sufficient condition for language emergence…" |

### 5. Self-promotional authorial framing

| Location | Original | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Detail §01 "Why This Survey Exists" | "This survey is unusual in that it puts them all in the same frame. The reward for doing so is a set of cross-cutting insights…" | → "Placing them in a single comparative frame makes visible a set of cross-cutting findings that no single-tradition study produces." |

### 6. Temporal anachronisms

None found. No future dates appear as publication dates. The masthead-sub carries no publication date. All timeline dates refer to historical events.

## Items Left Unchanged (Judgment Calls)

- **"widely regarded"** (Timeline 1998, Story of Your Life): Retained the softened form ("widely discussed as a landmark…") rather than removing the qualifier entirely, as the Nebula Award provides concrete grounding.
- **"most successful IAL"** (Timeline 1887, Esperanto): Retained — this is a defensible comparative claim within the IAL category specifically, not a global superlative across all language types.
- **"The Control Paradox," "Communities beat grammars"** section headers in §08: These are analytical labels coined in the survey itself, not NPOV violations.
