# Constructed Languages — Literature Collection

> A survey of invented languages from Hildegard von Bingen to Arrival, spanning history, art, science, and code.

**Compiled:** March 25, 2026
**Scope:** Historical conlangs, Tolkien's languages, media conlangs, linguistic relativity, sign language emergence, programming languages as conlangs
**Method:** Web research across academic sources, linguistics references, historical accounts, film analysis, cognitive science research

## 1. A History of Language Invention

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| H-01 | **Wikipedia: Constructed Language** | Comprehensive overview of conlang history. Lingua Ignota (1150s) as first known conlang. Taxonomy: artistic, auxiliary, philosophical, logical. Over 1,000 documented conlangs. | Ongoing |
| H-02 | **Beinecke Library (Yale): "The Rise and Fall of Volapuk"** | Volapuk created 1879 by Johann Martin Schleyer. 283 clubs, 25 periodicals, 316 textbooks by 1889. Collapsed due to schism between Schleyer and reformers. | Archival |
| H-03 | **Britannica: Volapuk** | Schleyer motivated by meeting a peasant unable to write to his son. Grammar based on German, lexicon on English. Third congress (Paris, 1889) conducted entirely in Volapuk. | Ref |
| H-04 | **Wikipedia: Esperanto** | Created by L.L. Zamenhof, 1887. ~100,000 speakers today. 16 grammar rules. Designed as politically neutral second language. UNESCO resolutions of support (1954, 1985). | Ongoing |
| H-05 | **Omniglot: "Why is inventing global languages doomed to fail?"** | Chicken-and-egg problem: no speakers because no utility, no utility because no speakers. Language cannot be separated from culture. English became the de facto auxiliary language. | 2024 |
| H-06 | **Cambridge Blog: "Of Invented Languages"** | Arika Okrent's survey of conlang history. John Wilkins' 1668 philosophical language attempted to categorize all knowledge. Hildegard's Lingua Ignota: ~1,000 words influenced by German and Latin, served mystical purpose. | 2025 |
| H-07 | **Wikipedia: Johann Martin Schleyer** | Catholic priest who claimed God told him to create a universal language during a sleepless night. Refused to cede control of Volapuk, leading to its destruction. | Ref |
| H-08 | **Medium: "The Secret of International Auxiliary Languages"** | Lojban (1987) as logical language designed to test Sapir-Whorf. Interlingua (1951) as naturalistic IAL from Romance languages. Ido (1907) as reformed Esperanto that caused schism. | 2023 |
| H-09 | **HistoryRise: "Artificial Languages in History"** | Overview connecting Esperanto to modern conlangs. The shift from utopian purpose (world peace) to artistic purpose (worldbuilding). | 2024 |
| H-10 | **Public Domain Review: "Truth, Beauty, and Volapuk"** | Cultural history of Volapuk's peak popularity. Newspapers printed columns in Volapuk. Signs appeared in shop windows. Decline as cautionary tale for language communities. | Archival |

## 2. Tolkien: The Philologist's Art

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| T-01 | **Wikipedia: Languages constructed by Tolkien** | Tolkien created ~20 languages/dialects. Quenya and Sindarin most developed. "The invention of languages is the foundation. The 'stories' were made rather to provide a world for the languages." Finnish inspired Quenya; Welsh inspired Sindarin. | Ref |
| T-02 | **Tolkien Estate: Carl Hostetter, "Tolkien's Invented Languages"** | Scholarly account of Tolkien's linguistic methodology. Used comparative philology and tree model. Started with proto-language phonology, then invented sound change mechanisms for each daughter language. Languages in constant flux throughout Tolkien's life. | Academic |
| T-03 | **Tolkien Estate: Arden Smith, "Writing Systems"** | Tengwar developed late 1920s/early 1930s. Letter shapes correspond to distinctive phonetic features. Cirth designed for carving (like historical runes). Multiple modes for different languages. | Academic |
| T-04 | **Wikipedia: Tengwar** | Tengwar is a featural writing system: shape encodes manner and place of articulation. Created by fictional character Feanor. Published in Appendix E of Lord of the Rings (1955). | Ref |
| T-05 | **Wikipedia: Elvish languages of Middle-earth** | Language family tree from Primitive Quendian through Common Eldarin to Quenya, Sindarin, Telerin, and others. Sound changes documented between proto-language and descendants. | Ref |
| T-06 | **Laura Coker (NCUR): "Tolkien's Linguistics: The Artificial Languages of Quenya..."** | Academic paper analyzing Tolkien's languages as serious linguistic constructions. Full morphological paradigms, phonological rules, and diachronic changes. | Academic |
| T-07 | **Multilingual Magazine: "J.R.R. Tolkien's Life in Languages"** | Biographical account connecting Tolkien's professional philology to his creative work. Languages began in childhood. Professional expertise in Old English, Gothic, Finnish, Welsh all fed into conlangs. | 2022 |
| T-08 | **Oxford Open Learning: "How J.R.R. Tolkien Built a Language"** | Tolkien as professional philologist specializing in Old English. Languages preceded the stories. Quenya's Finnish-inspired agglutinative morphology vs Sindarin's Welsh-inspired mutations. | Ref |

## 3. Media Conlangs

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| M-01 | **Wikipedia: Klingon language** | Created by Marc Okrand, 1984 (Star Trek III). Deliberately designed to sound alien: OVS word order, unusual phoneme inventory. Estimated 20-30 fluent speakers worldwide. Shakespeare translated. Hamlet performed in Klingon. | Ref |
| M-02 | **Klingon Language Institute: "Development and Use of the Klingon Language"** | Founded 1992 by Dr. Lawrence Schoen. Community expanded via internet. Language used for original fiction, songs, games, everyday conversation. Bible and Hamlet translations. | 1992-present |
| M-03 | **Wikipedia: Marc Okrand** | Linguist who created Klingon for Star Trek III (1984). Also created Atlantean for Disney's Atlantis (2001). PhD in linguistics from UC Berkeley. Originally hired for Star Trek II to create Vulcan dialogue. | Ref |
| M-04 | **Wikipedia: David J. Peterson** | Created Dothraki and Valyrian for Game of Thrones. 40+ conlangs created. Only person known to earn a living solely from conlanging. Book: The Art of Language Invention (2015). | Ref |
| M-05 | **TED Ideas: "Meet the person who created Dothraki and Valyrian"** | Peterson selected by Language Creation Society from competition. Dothraki has 4,000+ words. Valyrian designed as proto-language with daughter languages. Languages mirror how real tongues work. | 2019 |
| M-06 | **Salon: "How to speak Na'vi"** | Paul Frommer, USC linguist, hired by James Cameron. Na'vi incorporates Polynesian-inspired phonology. Cameron provided 30 initial words. Some Avatar fans learned to speak Na'vi. | 2023 |
| M-07 | **SNExplores: "How to invent a language"** | Accessible overview of professional conlanging process. Na'vi has ejective consonants rare in European languages. Frommer continues expanding Na'vi for sequels. | 2023 |
| M-08 | **Language Creation Society (conlang.org)** | Founded 2007. 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Organizes biennial conferences. Connects conlangers with entertainment industry. Hosts resources, podcasts, publications. 13 international board members. | 2007-present |
| M-09 | **Unsung Science: "Who Makes the Fake Languages for Hollywood?"** | Hollywood increasingly hires professional conlangers. Peterson has 40+ tongues. Unlike earlier ad hoc approaches, modern media conlangs have full grammars. Adds authenticity and merchandising potential. | 2021 |
| M-10 | **Campfire Writing: "Paul Frommer on Creating the Na'vi Language"** | Deep interview on Na'vi construction process. Ejective consonants, tripartite case alignment. Language designed to be pronounceable but unfamiliar. | 2023 |

## 4. Heptapod B & Linguistic Relativity

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| L-01 | **Wikipedia: "Story of Your Life"** | Ted Chiang's 1998 novella. Won Nebula Award. Protagonist learns Heptapod B and begins perceiving time non-linearly. Explores strong Sapir-Whorf hypothesis as thought experiment. | 1998 |
| L-02 | **Emory University: "Story of Your Life — Language and Linguistics in Sci-Fi"** | Academic analysis. Heptapod A (speech) is sequential; Heptapod B (writing) is semasiographic, non-linear. Writing has no reference to speech. Chiang uses Fermat's principle of least time as structural metaphor. | 2024 |
| L-03 | **Academia.edu: "Heptapod B and Whorfianism"** | Scholarly paper analyzing how Chiang extrapolates Whorfian ideas. Film adaptation makes the connection more explicit but possibly less accurate than the novella. | Academic |
| L-04 | **Jessica Coon (McGill): Linguistics in Arrival** | Professional linguist who consulted on the Arrival film. Analysis of how the film represents linguistic fieldwork. Discusses accuracy and artistic license. | 2016 |
| L-05 | **Winawer et al.: Russian blues color perception** | Russian speakers distinguish light blue (goluboy) and dark blue (siniy) faster than English speakers. fMRI shows language processing areas activate during color discrimination. Neuroimaging support for weak Whorf hypothesis. | 2007 |
| L-06 | **ResearchGate: "The Sapir-Whorf's Hypothesis: colour in English and Russian"** | Comparison study confirming faster blue discrimination in Russian speakers. Evidence for linguistic effects on low-level perceptual processing. | 2021 |
| L-07 | **Lera Boroditsky research** | Stanford/UCSD cognitive scientist. Demonstrated that language affects spatial reasoning, time perception, and causal reasoning. Mandarin speakers think about time vertically; English speakers horizontally. | 2000-present |
| L-08 | **Berkeley: "The Sapir-Whorf hypothesis and inference under uncertainty"** | Modern computational framework for understanding linguistic relativity. Language provides Bayesian priors that influence perception under uncertainty. Reconciles strong/weak debate. | 2020s |
| L-09 | **Reactor: "Let Me Talk to the Aliens! Ted Chiang's 'Story of Your Life'"** | Literary analysis. The story uses linguistics as vehicle for exploring determinism vs free will. Heptapod B as the most sophisticated conlang-as-thought-experiment in fiction. | Review |
| L-10 | **DailyO: "Understanding cognitive bias through Arrival's Sapir-Whorf hypothesis"** | Accessible analysis of how the film illustrates linguistic relativity for general audiences. Discussion of English-language cognitive biases. | 2020 |

## 5. Sign Languages & Natural Construction

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| S-01 | **Wikipedia: Nicaraguan Sign Language** | Emerged in 1980s when deaf children gathered in new schools. No outside sign language input. Children spontaneously created a new language. Each generation added complexity. Considered strongest evidence for innate language faculty. | Ref |
| S-02 | **Serious Science: "Nicaraguan Sign Language"** | Video lecture on NSL emergence. Language arose naturally within a community of 10-100 people. Drive to acquire language is a resilient, instinctual human characteristic. | Academic |
| S-03 | **Senghas & Coppola (Columbia): "Children Creating Language"** | Seminal research showing how successive cohorts of deaf children introduced grammatical complexity. First cohort: pidgin-like. Second cohort: creole-like with consistent grammar. Third cohort: further systematization. | Academic |
| S-04 | **Evidence for Universal Grammar in Nicaraguan Sign Language** (NCUR) | Student research paper arguing NSL supports Chomsky's Universal Grammar hypothesis. Grammatical structures emerge without explicit instruction. | Academic |
| S-05 | **Wikipedia: Al-Sayyid Bedouin Sign Language** | Village sign language in Negev desert, Israel. 4% deaf (vs 0.1% US average). ~150 deaf signers. Emerged ~75 years ago in near-isolation. Word order differs from all surrounding languages (Arabic, Hebrew, ISL). | Ref |
| S-06 | **Haifa University Sign Language Research Lab: ABSL** | 70+ years of independent development. Grammar emerged quickly, each generation more complex. No phonological structure at community level but emerging within families. Deaf people fully integrated socially. | Academic |
| S-07 | **PMC: "Language Emergence: Clues from a New Bedouin Sign Language"** | Sandler, Meir, Padden, Aronoff. Traces emergence of linguistic structure. Rare opportunity to study a language almost from birth. Prosody and word order developing in real time. | 2005 |
| S-08 | **United Language Group: "What is Nicaraguan Sign Language?"** | Accessible overview. NSL proves language is an innate human instinct. Combined gestures and home-sign into pidgin, then creole. | Popular |

## 6. Programming Languages as Conlangs

| ID | Source | Key Data Points | Date |
|----|--------|----------------|------|
| P-01 | **Wikipedia: Esoteric programming language** | Esolangs designed as art, humor, or proof of concept. INTERCAL (1972) first esolang, parodied FORTRAN/COBOL/APL. Common traits: parody, minimalism, making programming difficult. | Ref |
| P-02 | **Wikipedia: Brainfuck** | Created 1993 by Urban Muller. 8 commands only. Turing-complete. Goal: smallest possible compiler. Inspired by 1024-byte FALSE compiler. Demonstrates that extreme minimalism can still be computationally universal. | Ref |
| P-03 | **Dijkstra (EWD 667): "On the foolishness of natural language programming"** | 1978 essay. Argues formal symbolism is essential to precise thought. Greek mathematics stalled because it was verbal. Modern science emerged via formal notation (Vieta, Descartes, Leibniz, Boole). Natural language programming is a contradiction. | 1978 |
| P-04 | **Esolang Wiki: Prehistory of esoteric programming languages** | APL (Iverson, 1962) as radical notation. Uses unique symbol set. Described as "a programming language and mathematical notation." Influenced the esolang tradition through its willingness to depart from conventional syntax. | Ref |
| P-05 | **Esolang Wiki: Timeline of esoteric programming languages** | INTERCAL (1972), Brainfuck (1993), Befunge (1993), Whitespace (2003), Shakespeare Programming Language (2001), Piet (2001, programs are images), LOLCODE (2007). Over 1,000 documented esolangs. | Ref |
| P-06 | **Marc Brooker: "On the success of natural language programming"** | 2025 response to Dijkstra. LLMs have made natural language programming partially successful. But formal languages remain necessary for precision. The debate has new dimensions in the AI era. | 2025 |
| P-07 | **AWWSmm: "20 Intriguing, Unusual, and Goofy Programming Languages"** | Survey of unusual languages including APL, Brainfuck, Piet, Whitespace, Shakespeare. Each represents a different philosophy of what "language" means in computing. | 2023 |
| P-08 | **The Outline: "Brainfuck: code that was designed to hurt"** | Cultural analysis of Brainfuck as art object. Programming as constrained creativity. Esolangs as the conlang community of computing. | 2017 |
